Derivative Properties
Since the derivative is defined through a limit, it is also a linear operator:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} (\alpha f(x) + \beta g(x)) = \alpha\frac{d}{dx} f(x) + \beta\frac{d}{dx} g(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{R} }$
which can be seen by direct evaluation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} (\alpha f(x) + \beta g(x)) &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{(\alpha f(x + h) + \beta g(x + h)) - (\alpha f(x) + \beta g(x))}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{\alpha(f(x + h) - f(x)) + \beta(g(x + h) - g(x))}{h} \\ &= \alpha \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h} + \beta \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{g(x + h) - g(x)}{h} \\ &= \alpha\frac{d}{dx} f(x) + \beta\frac{d}{dx} g(x) }$
Common Derivatives
In the following sections I will introduce you to rules that apply to handling all common cases of differentiation, but before doing that you must first know the fundamental derivatives. I will not go into proving how these are attained here, but perhaps in later examples you will see how the derivative is determined in some specific cases. So for now just accept the following:
$\textbf{f(x)}$ $\frac{d}{dx} \textbf{f(x)}$
$c$ where $c \in \mathbb{R}$ $0$
$x^n$ where $n \in \mathbb{R} \setminus \{0\}$ $nx^{n - 1}$
$e^x$ $e^x$
$a^x$ where $a \in \mathbb{R}_{> 0}$ $\ln(a)a^x$
$\ln(x)$ $\frac{1}{x}$
$\log_{a}(x)$ where $a \in \mathbb{R}_{> 0}$ $\frac{1}{\ln(a)x}$

$\csc(x)$ $-\csc(x)\cot(x)$

$\arccos(x)$ $-\frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - x^2}}$
