Problem
Using $\textit{Euler's Formula}$ the following can be deduced:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) = \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i} \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \cos(x) = \frac{e^{ix} + e^{-ix}}{2} }$
where $i$ is the imaginary number. Using these definitions prove:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \sin(x) = \cos(x) }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \sin(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i} \\ &= \frac{1}{2i} \Big[ \frac{d}{dx} (e^i)^x - (e^{-i})^x \Big] \\ &= \frac{1}{2i} \Big[ \ln(e^i)e^{ix} - \ln(e^{-i})e^{-ix} \Big] \\ &= \frac{1}{2i} \Big[ ie^{ix} + ie^{-ix} \Big] \\ &= \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2} \\ &= \cos(x) }$
