Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 + 5 }$
Determine $f'(3)$.
Solution
$\eqalign{ f'(3) &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(3 + h) - f(3)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{((3 + h)^2 + 5) - (9 + 5)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{9 + 6h + h^2 + 5 - 14}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{6h + h^2}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} (6 + h) \\ &= 6 }$
