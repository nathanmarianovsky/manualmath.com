Problem
Determine the equation of the tangent line to the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{x^2 + 1}} }$
at $x = 1$.
Solution
To accomplish this we are going to find the instantaneous rate of change and the point through which the line passes. For the point we just have to evaluate the function at $x = 1$:
$\eqalign{ f(1) = \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} }$
As for the slope:
$\eqalign{ f'(1) &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(1 + h) - f(1)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}} - \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}}}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{\frac{\pi\sqrt{2} - \pi\sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}}{\sqrt{2}\sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}}}{h} \\ &= \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sqrt{2} - \sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}}{h\sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}}{\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}} \\ &= \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{2 - 2 - 2h - h^2}{h\sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}(\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2})} \\ &= -\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{2 + h}{\sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2}(\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{2 + 2h + h^2})} \\ &= -\frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot \frac{1}{2} \\ &= -\frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}} }$
Now we form the line as:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = -\frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}}x + b }$
where we evaluate $b$ by using the point already known:
$\eqalign{ g(1) &= -\frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}} + b \\ \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} &= -\frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}} + b \\ b &= \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}} }$
So the final solution is:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = -\frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}}x + \frac{\pi}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{\pi}{2^{\frac{3}{2}}} }$
