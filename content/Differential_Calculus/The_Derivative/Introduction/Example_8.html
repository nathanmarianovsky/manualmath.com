Problem
Determine:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow -2} \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} - \sqrt{2}}{x + 2} }$
What does this physically represent?
Solution
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow -2} \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} - \sqrt{2}}{x + 2} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -2} \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} - \sqrt{2}}{x + 2} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x + 4} + \sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{x + 4} + \sqrt{2}} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -2} \frac{x + 4 - 2}{(x + 2)(\sqrt{x + 4} + \sqrt{2})} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -2} \frac{1}{\sqrt{x + 4} + \sqrt{2}} \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{6} + \sqrt{2}} }$
This limit represents the derivative of $\sqrt{x + 4}$ at $x = -2$.
