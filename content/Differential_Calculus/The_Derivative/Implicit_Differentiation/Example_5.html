Problem
Given:
$\eqalign{ \frac{y}{x^3} + \frac{x}{y^3} = x^2y^4 }$
Determine $y'(x)$.
Solution
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ \frac{y}{x^3} + \frac{x}{y^3} \Big] &= \frac{d}{dx} x^2y^4 \\ \frac{d}{dx} yx^{-3} + \frac{d}{dx} xy^{-3} &= \frac{d}{dx} x^2y^4 \\ y'x^{-3} - 3yx^{-4} + y^{-3} - 3xy^{-4}y' &= 2xy^4 + 4x^2y^3y' \\ y'(x^{-3} - 3xy^{-4} - 4x^2y^3) &= 2xy^4 + 3yx^{-4} - y^{-3} \\ y' &= \frac{2xy^4 + 3yx^{-4} - y^{-3}}{x^{-3} - 3xy^{-4} - 4x^2y^3} \\ &= \frac{2xy^4 + 3yx^{-4} - y^{-3}}{x^{-3} - 3xy^{-4} - 4x^2y^3} \cdot \frac{x^4y^4}{x^4y^4} \\ &= \frac{2x^5y^8 + 3y^5 - x^4y}{xy^4 - 3x^5 - 4x^6y^7} }$
