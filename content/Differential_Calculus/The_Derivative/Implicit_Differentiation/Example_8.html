Problem
Determine all points where the lines tangent to the graph of:
$\eqalign{ x^{\frac{2}{3}} + y^{\frac{2}{3}} = 8 }$
have a slope of $-1$.
Solution
To observe the slope at all points we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ x^{\frac{2}{3}} + y^{\frac{2}{3}} \Big] &= \frac{d}{dx} 8 \\ \frac{2}{3}x^{-\frac{1}{3}} + \frac{2}{3}y^{-\frac{1}{3}}y' &= 0 \\ y^{-\frac{1}{3}}y' &= -x^{-\frac{1}{3}} \\ y' &= -\Big( \frac{y}{x} \Big)^{\frac{1}{3}} }$
Now if we want the slope to be $-1$:
$\eqalign{ -1 &= -\Big( \frac{y}{x} \Big)^{\frac{1}{3}} \\ 1 &= \Big( \frac{y}{x} \Big)^{\frac{1}{3}} \\ 1 &= \frac{y}{x} \\ y &= x }$
So we now have two equations that have to be satisfied: the original equation of the graph and the condition on the slope. Plugging in the latter into the equation of the graph:
$\eqalign{ x^{\frac{2}{3}} + x^{\frac{2}{3}} &= 8 \\ 2x^{\frac{2}{3}} &= 8 \\ x^{\frac{2}{3}} &= 4 \\ x &= -8, 8 }$
We now know the $x$-coordinates, and the $y$ counterparts are exactly the same due to the slope condition. Therefore, the points where the lines tangent to the graph have a slope of $-1$ are:
$\eqalign{ (-8,-8) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} (8,8) }$
