Problem
Given:
$\eqalign{ y = \sin(3x + 4y) }$
Determine $y'(x)$.
Solution
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} y &= \frac{d}{dx} \sin(3x + 4y) \\ y' &= \cos(3x + 4y) \cdot (3 + 4y') \\ y' &= 3\cos(3x + 4y) + 4\cos(3x + 4y)y' \\ y'(1 - 4\cos(3x + 4y)) &= 3\cos(3x + 4y) \\ y' &= \frac{3\cos(3x + 4y)}{1 - 4\cos(3x + 4y)} }$
