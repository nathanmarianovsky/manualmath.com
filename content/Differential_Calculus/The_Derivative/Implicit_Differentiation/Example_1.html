Problem
Given:
$\eqalign{ x^3y^5 + 3x = 8y^3 + 1 }$
Determine $y'(x)$.
Solution
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ x^3y^5 + 3x \Big] &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ 8y^3 + 1 \Big] \\ \frac{d}{dx} x^2y^5 + 3\frac{d}{dx} x &= 8\frac{d}{dx} y^3 + \frac{d}{dx} 1 \\ 2xy^5 + 5x^2y^4y' + 3 &= 24y^2y' \\ y'(5x^2y^4 - 24y^2) &= -2xy^5 \\ y' &= -\frac{2xy^5}{5x^2y^4 - 24y^2} \\ &= \frac{2xy^5}{24y^2 - 5x^2y^4} }$
