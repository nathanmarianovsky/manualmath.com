Problem
Find the equation of the tangent line to the graph of:
$\eqalign{ x^2 + (y - x)^3 = 9 }$
at $x = 1$.
Solution
To accomplish this we are going to find the point of interest and instantaneous rate of change. For the point:
$\eqalign{ (1)^2 + (y - (1))^3 &= 9 \\ (y - 1)^3 &= 8 \\ y - 1 &= 2 \\ y &= 3 }$
Next we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} 9 &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ x^2 + (y - x)^3 \Big] \\ 0 &= 2x + 3(y - x)^2 \cdot (y' - 1) \\ 3y'(y - x)^2 &= 3(y - x)^2 - 2x \\ y' &= 1 - \frac{2x}{3(y - x)^2} }$
Now to determine the slope:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} \Bigg|_{(1,3)} &= 1 - \frac{2(1)}{3((3) - (1))^2} \\ &= 1 - \frac{2}{12} \\ &= \frac{5}{6} }$
Next we form the line as:
$\eqalign{ y - 3 = \frac{5}{6}(x - 1) }$
