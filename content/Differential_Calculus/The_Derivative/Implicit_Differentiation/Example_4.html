Problem
Given:
$\eqalign{ \cos^2(x) + \cos^2(y) = \cos(2x + 2y) }$
Determine $y'(x)$.
Solution
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ \cos^2(x) + \cos^2(y) \Big] &= \frac{d}{dx} \cos(2x + 2y) \\ -2\cos(x)\sin(x) - 2\cos(y)\sin(y)y' &= -\sin(2x + 2y) \cdot (2 + 2y') \\ y'(2\sin(2x + 2y) - 2\cos(y)\sin(y)) &= 2\cos(x)\sin(x) - 2\sin(2x + 2y) \\ y' &= \frac{\cos(x)\sin(x) - \sin(2x + 2y)}{\sin(2x + 2y) - \cos(y)\sin(y)} }$
