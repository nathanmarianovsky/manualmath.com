Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = \sin(2x)$.
Solution
According to the chain rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = \frac{df}{dg} \cdot \frac{dg}{dx} }$
so we identify our components as:
$\eqalign{ f(g) &= \sin(g) \\ g(x) &= 2x }$
and evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \sin(2x) \Bigg) \\ &= \Big( \cos(2x) \Big) \cdot \Big( 2 \Big) \\ &= 2\cos(2x) }$
