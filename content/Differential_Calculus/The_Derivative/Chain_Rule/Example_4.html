Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = f(g(k(x))) }$
Solution
According to the chain rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = \frac{df}{dg} \cdot \frac{dg}{dx} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( f(g(k(x))) \Bigg) \\ &= \Big( f'(g(k(x))) \Big) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(k(x)) \Big) \\ &= \Big( f'(g(k(x))) \Big) \cdot \Big( g'(k(x)) \Big) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( k(x) \Big) \\ &= f'(g(k(x)))g'(k(x))k'(x) }$
