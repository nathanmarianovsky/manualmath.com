Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = e^{x^7}e^{\sin(x)} }$
Solution
According to the chain rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = \frac{df}{dg} \cdot \frac{dg}{dx} }$
so we identify our components as:
$\eqalign{ f(g) &= e^g \\ g(x) &= x^7 + \sin(x) }$
and evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( e^{x^7}e^{\sin(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( e^{x^7 + \sin(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \Big( e^{x^7 + \sin(x)} \Big) \cdot \Big( 7x^6 + \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= (7x^6 + \cos(x))e^{x^7 + \sin(x)} }$
