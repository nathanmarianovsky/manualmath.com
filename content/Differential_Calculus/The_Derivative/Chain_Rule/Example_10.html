Problem
Determine a formula to compute $(f^{-1})''(x)$ given that we know $f(x)$, $f'(x)$, $f''(x)$, $f^{-1}(x)$, and $(f^{-1})'(x)$ are all differentiable at $x$.
Solution
We already know that the first derivative of the inverse is defined as:
$\eqalign{ (f^{-1})'(x) = \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(x))} }$
which can now be used to determine a formula for the second derivative:
$\eqalign{ &(f^{-1})''(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(x))} \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} (f'(f^{-1}(x)))^{-1} \\ &= -(f'(f^{-1}(x)))^{-2} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} f'(f^{-1}(x)) \\ &= -(f'(f^{-1}(x)))^{-2} f''(f^{-1}(x)) (f^{-1})'(x) \\ &= -\frac{f''(f^{-1}(x))(f^{-1})'(x)}{(f'(f^{-1}(x)))^2} }$
