Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \frac{e^x}{x^2} }$
Solution
According to the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Bigg) = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big)}{(g(x))^2} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{e^x}{x^2} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( e^x \Big) \cdot x^2 - e^x \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^2 \Big)}{x^4} \\ &= \frac{x^2e^x - 2xe^x}{x^4} }$
