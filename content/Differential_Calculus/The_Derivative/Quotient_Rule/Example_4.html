Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \sec(x) }$
Solution
According to the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Bigg) = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big)}{(g(x))^2} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \sec(x) \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{1}{\cos(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( 1 \Big) \cdot \cos(x) - 1 \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big)}{\cos^2(x)} \\ &= \frac{\sin(x)}{\cos^2(x)} \\ &= \frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)} \cdot \frac{1}{\cos(x)} \\ &= \tan(x)\sec(x) }$
