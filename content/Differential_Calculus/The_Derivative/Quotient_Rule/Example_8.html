Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = \csc(x)$.
Solution
According to the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Bigg) = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big)}{(g(x))^2} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \csc(x) \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{1}{\sin(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( 1 \Big) \cdot \sin(x) - 1 \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big)}{(\sin(x))^2} \\ &= -\frac{\cos(x)}{(\sin(x))^2} \\ &= -\frac{\cos(x)}{\sin(x)} \cdot \frac{1}{\sin(x)} \\ &= -\cot(x)\csc(x) }$
