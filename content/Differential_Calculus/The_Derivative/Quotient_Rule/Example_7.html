Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \frac{2x}{(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4)} }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{2x}{(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\Big( 2x \Big)' (x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4)}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{2x \Big( (x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4) \Big)'}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} \\ &= \frac{2(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4)}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{4x^2(x^3 + x^2 - 4)}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \frac{2x(x^2 + 1)(3x^2 + 2x)}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} }$
