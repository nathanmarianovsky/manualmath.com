Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = \frac{2x}{(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4)}$.
Solution
According to the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Bigg) = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big)}{(g(x))^2} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{2x}{(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( 2x \Big) \cdot (x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4) - 2x \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4) \Big)}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} \\ &= \frac{2(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4) - 2x \cdot \Bigg[ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^2 + 1 \Big) \cdot (x^3 + x^2 - 4) + (x^2 + 1) \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^3 + x^2 - 4 \Big) \Bigg]}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} \\ &= \frac{2(x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4) - 2x(2x(x^3 + x^2 - 4) + (x^2 + 1)(3x^2 + 2x))}{((x^2 + 1)(x^3 + x^2 - 4))^2} }$
