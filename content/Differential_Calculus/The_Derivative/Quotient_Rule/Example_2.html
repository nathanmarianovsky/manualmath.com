Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \tan(x) }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \tan(x) \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{\sin(x)}{\cos(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\Big( \sin(x) \Big)' \cdot \cos(x) - \sin(x) \cdot \Big( \cos(x) \Big)'}{\cos^2(x)} \\ &= \frac{\cos^2(x) + \sin^2(x)}{\cos^2(x)} \\ &= \frac{1}{\cos^2(x)} \\ &= \sec^2(x) }$
