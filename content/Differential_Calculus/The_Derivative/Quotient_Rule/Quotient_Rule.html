Definition
Given $k(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$ with the condition that $g(x) \neq 0$, the derivative is defined as:
$\eqalign{ k'(x) = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} }$
where $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are differentiable at $x$.
Proof by Formal Definition
Using the limit definition of the derivative and some simple trickery:
$\eqalign{ k'(x) &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{k(x + h) - k(x)}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{\frac{f(x + h)}{g(x + h)} - \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}{h} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{h} \cdot \frac{f(x + h)g(x) - f(x)g(x + h)}{g(x + h)g(x)} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{h} \cdot \frac{f(x + h)g(x) - f(x)g(x) - f(x)g(x + h) + f(x)g(x)}{g(x + h)g(x)} \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{h} \Bigg[ \frac{f(x + h)g(x) - f(x)g(x)}{g(x + h)g(x)} - \frac{f(x)g(x + h) - f(x)g(x)}{g(x + h)g(x)} \Bigg] \\ &= \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{g(x + h)} \cdot \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h} - \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(x)}{g(x + h)g(x)} \cdot \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{g(x + h) - g(x)}{h} \\ &= \frac{1}{g(x)} \cdot f'(x) - \frac{f(x)}{(g(x))^2} \cdot g'(x) \\ &= \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} }$
$\tag*{$\blacksquare$}$
Proof by Product Rule
By cross multiplying, differentiating using the product rule, and simplifying we arrive at the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ k(x) &= \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ g(x)k(x) &= f(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x)k(x) \Big) &= \frac{d}{dx} f(x) \\ g'(x)k(x) + g(x)k'(x) &= f'(x) \\ g(x)k'(x) &= f'(x) - g'(x) \cdot \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ k'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)}{g(x)} - \frac{f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} \\ k'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} }$
$\tag*{$\blacksquare$}$
Proof by Logarithmic Differentiation
This proof essentially consists of taking the logarithm of both sides, differentiating both sides, and simplifying:
$\eqalign{ h(x) &= \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ \ln(h(x)) &= \ln \Big( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Big) \\ \ln(h(x)) &= \ln(f(x)) - \ln(g(x)) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(h(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \ln(f(x)) - \ln(g(x)) \Big) \\ \frac{h'(x)}{h(x)} &= \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} - \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)} \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Big( \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} - \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)} \Big) \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)}{g(x)} - \frac{f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} }$
$\tag*{$\blacksquare$}$
