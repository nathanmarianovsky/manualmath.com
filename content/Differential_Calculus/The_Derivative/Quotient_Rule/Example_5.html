Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \frac{x^7 + 1}{x^2 + 3} }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{x^7 + 1}{x^2 + 3} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\Big( x^7 + 1 \Big)' (x^2 + 3) - (x^7 + 1) \Big( x^2 + 3 \Big)'}{(x^2 + 3)^2} \\ &= \frac{7x^6(x^2 + 3) - 2x(x^7 + 1)}{(x^2 + 3)^2} }$
