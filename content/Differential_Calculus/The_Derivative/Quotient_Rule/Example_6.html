Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = \frac{\tan(x)}{\sin(x) + \cos(x)}$.
Solution
According to the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Bigg) = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big)}{(g(x))^2} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{\tan(x)}{\sin(x) + \cos(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( \tan(x) \Big) \cdot (\sin(x) + \cos(x)) - \tan(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) + \cos(x) \Big)}{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))^2} \\ &= \frac{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))\sec^2(x) - (\cos(x) - \sin(x))\tan(x)}{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))^2} }$
