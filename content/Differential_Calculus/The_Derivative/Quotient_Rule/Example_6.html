Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \frac{\tan(x)}{\sin(x) + \cos(x)} }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{\tan(x)}{\sin(x) + \cos(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{\Big( \tan(x) \Big)' (\sin(x) + \cos(x))}{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))^2} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{\tan(x) \Big( \sin(x) + \cos(x) \Big)'}{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))^2} \\ &= \frac{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))\sec^2(x)}{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))^2} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{(\cos(x) - \sin(x))\tan(x)}{(\sin(x) + \cos(x))^2} }$
