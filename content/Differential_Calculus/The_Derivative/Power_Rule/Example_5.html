Problem
Find the derivative of $f(x) = \ln(e^{2x})$.
Solution
According to the power rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} x^n = nx^{n-1} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{R} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \ln(e^{2x}) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} (2x)\ln(e) \\ &= 2 \cdot \frac{d}{dx} x \\ &= 2 }$
