Problem
Find the second derivative of:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^{\pi + 1} }$
Solution
According to the power rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} x^n = nx^{n-1} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{R} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d^2}{dx^2} f(x) &= \frac{d^2}{dx^2} x^{\pi + 1} \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{d}{dx} x^{\pi + 1} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( (\pi + 1)x^{\pi} \Bigg) \\ &= (\pi + 1)(\pi)x^{\pi - 1} }$
