Problem
Find the derivative of $f(x) = \frac{x^7 - 3}{x^2}$.
Solution
According to the power rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} x^n = nx^{n-1} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{R} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \frac{x^7 - 3}{x^2} \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} (x^5 - 3x^{-2}) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} x^5 - 3 \frac{d}{dx} x^{-2} \\ &= 5x^4 + 6x^{-3} }$
