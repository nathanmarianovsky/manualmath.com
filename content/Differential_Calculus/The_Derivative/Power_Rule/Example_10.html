Problem
Given that $p,r \in \mathbb{N}$, derive the following relation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d^r}{dx^r} x^p = \mathcal{P}(p, r) x^{p - r} }$
where:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{P}(p, r) = \frac{p!}{(p - r)!} }$
is known as a $r$-permutation of $p$ which represents the number of ordered arrangements of $r$ unique items selected out of a group containing $p$ total items.
Solution
Given the power rule we compute $r$ derivatives directly and simplify:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d^r}{dx^r} x^p &= \frac{d^{r - 1}}{dx^{r - 1}} px^{p - 1} \\ &= \frac{d^{r - 2}}{dx^{r - 2}} p(p - 1)x^{p - 2} \\ &= \frac{d^{r - 3}}{dx^{r - 3}} p(p - 1)(p - 2)x^{p - 3} \\ &\vdots \\ &= \frac{d^2}{dx^2} p(p - 1)(p - 2)(p - (r - 3))x^{p - (r - 2)} \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} p(p - 1)(p - 2)\dots(p - (r - 3))(p - (r - 2))x^{p - (r - 1)} \\ &= p(p - 1)(p - 2)\dots(p - (r - 3))(p - (r - 2))(p - (r - 1))x^{p - r} \\ &= p(p - 1)\dots(p - (r - 2))(p - (r - 1)) \cdot \frac{(p - r)\dots(2)(1)}{(p - r)\dots(2)(1)} x^{p - r} \\ &= \frac{p!}{(p - r)!} x^{p - r} \\ &= \mathcal{P}(p, r) x^{p - r} }$
