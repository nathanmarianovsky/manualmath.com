Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = x\sin(x)\cos(x) }$
Solution
According to the product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x)g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big) }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x\sin(x)\cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x\sin(x) \Big) \cdot \cos(x) + x\sin(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \Big( \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x \Big) \cdot \sin(x) + x \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big) \Big) \cdot \cos(x) - x\sin^2(x) \\ &= (\sin(x) + x\cos(x))\cos(x) - x\sin^2(x) }$
