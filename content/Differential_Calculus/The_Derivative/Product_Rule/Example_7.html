Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = x\sin(x)\cos(x) }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x\sin(x)\cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x\sin(x) \Big) \cdot \cos(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + x\sin(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x \Big) \cdot \sin(x)\cos(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + x\cos(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big) - x\sin^2(x) \\ &= (\sin(x) + x\cos(x))\cos(x) - x\sin^2(x) }$
