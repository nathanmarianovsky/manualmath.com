Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \sin(2x) }$
Solution
According to the product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x)g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big) }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(2x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( 2\sin(x)\cos(x) \Big) \\ &= 2 \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big) \cdot \cos(x) + 2\sin(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= 2\cos^2(x) - 2\sin^2(x) }$
