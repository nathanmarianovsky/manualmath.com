Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = (5 + x^2)(7 + x + x^5) }$
Solution
According to the product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x)g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big) }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (5 + x^2)(7 + x + x^5) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( 5 + x^2 \Big) \cdot (7 + x + x^5) + (5 + x^2) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( 7 + x + x^5 \Big) \\ &= 2x(7 + x + x^5) + (5 + x^2)(1 + 5x^4) }$
