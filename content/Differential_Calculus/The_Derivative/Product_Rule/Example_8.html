Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = (x^2 + 1)\sin(x)\cot(x)$.
Solution
According to the product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x)g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big) }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (x^2 + 1)\sin(x)\cot(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (x^2 + 1)\cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^2 + 1 \Big) \cdot \cos(x) + (x^2 + 1) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= 2x\cos(x) - (x^2 + 1)\sin(x) }$
