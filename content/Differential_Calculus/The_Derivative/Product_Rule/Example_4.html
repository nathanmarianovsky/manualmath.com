Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = \frac{\sin(x)}{\sec(x)} }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{\sin(x)}{\sec(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x)\cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big) \cdot \cos(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \sin(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x) }$
