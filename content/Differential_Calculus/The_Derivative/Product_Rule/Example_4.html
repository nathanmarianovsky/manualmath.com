Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = \frac{\sin(x)}{\sec(x)}$.
Solution
According to the product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x)g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big) }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( \frac{\sin(x)}{\sec(x)} \Bigg) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x)\cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big) \cdot \cos(x) + \sin(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \cos(x) \Big) \\ &= \cos^2(x) - \sin^2(x) }$
