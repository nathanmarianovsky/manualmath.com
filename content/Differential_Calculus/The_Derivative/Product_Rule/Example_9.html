Problem
Prove the following statement:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} &\prod_{i = 1}^n f_i(x) = \sum_{i = 1}^n f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) \\ &\text{where} \hspace{.3cm} k \in \{ 1, 2, 3, \dots, n \} }$
Solution
This is nothing more than an extension to the product rule, one that handles the product of not just two functions, but rather $n$ of them. For a formal proof, consider using induction. For the base case let $n = 2$:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \prod_{i = 1}^2 f_i(x) = \sum_{i = 1}^2 f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) }$
which holds true since we accept the standard product rule. Now we assume that given statement and try to show that it holds true for $n + 1$:
$\eqalign{ &\sum_{i = 1}^{n + 1} f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \prod_{i = 1}^{n + 1} f_i(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^n f_i(x) \cdot f_{n + 1}(x) \Bigg] \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^n f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot f_{n + 1}(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^n f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ f_{n + 1}(x) \Bigg] \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) + \prod_{i = 1}^n f_i(x) \cdot f_{n + 1}'(x) \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^{n + 1} f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) }$
While a formal proof is always important, it just as important to understand how the statement is derived. In this scenario we can derive the derivative of a product of $n$ functions by recursively using the standard product rule:
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} \prod_{i = 1}^n f_i(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{n - 1} f_i(x) \cdot f_n(x) \Bigg] \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{n - 1} f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot f_n(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \prod_{i = 1}^{n - 1} f_i(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ f_n(x) \Bigg] \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{n - 1} f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot f_n(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + f_n'(x) \prod_{k \neq n} f_i(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{n - 2} f_i(x) \cdot f_{n - 1}(x) \Bigg] \cdot f_n(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + f_n'(x) \prod_{k \neq n} f_i(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{n - 2} f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot f_{n - 1}(x)f_n(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + f_{n - 1}'(x) \prod_{k \neq n - 1} f_i(x) + f_n'(x) \prod_{k \neq n} f_i(x) \\ &\vdots \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{2} f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot \prod_{k \neq 1,2} f_k \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \sum_{i = 3}^n f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg[ \prod_{i = 1}^{1} f_i(x) \Bigg] \cdot \prod_{k \neq 1} f_k \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \sum_{i = 2}^n f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n f_i'(x) \prod_{k \neq i} f_k(x) }$
