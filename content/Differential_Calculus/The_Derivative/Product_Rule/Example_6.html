Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = (\sin(x) + \cos(x))\tan(x) }$
Solution
According to the product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x)g(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) + f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big) }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (\sin(x) + \cos(x))\tan(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (\sin(x) + \cos(x)) \Big) \cdot \tan(x) + (\sin(x) + \cos(x)) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \tan(x) \Big) \\ &= (\cos(x) - \sin(x))\tan(x) + (\sin(x) + \cos(x))\sec^2(x) }$
