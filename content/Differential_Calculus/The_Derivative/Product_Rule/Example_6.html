Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ h(x) = (\sin(x) + \cos(x))\tan(x) }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ &\frac{d}{dx} h(x) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (\sin(x) + \cos(x))\tan(x) \Big) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( (\sin(x) + \cos(x)) \Big) \cdot \tan(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + (\sin(x) + \cos(x)) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \tan(x) \Big) \\ &= (\cos(x) - \sin(x))\tan(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + (\sin(x) + \cos(x))\sec^2(x) }$
