Problem
Consider the function:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = \pi^2 + 2^x + x^2 + x^{\frac{1}{x}} }$
Determine the slope of the line perpendicular to the graph at $x = 1$.
Solution
First we need to determine:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= x^{\frac{1}{x}} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(x^{\frac{1}{x}}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \frac{\ln(x)}{x} \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \frac{1 - \ln(x)}{x^2} \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \frac{1 - \ln(x)}{x^2} \\ &= x^{\frac{1}{x}} \frac{1 - \ln(x)}{x^2} }$
Now to determine the slope we need the derivative of the function:
$\eqalign{ y'(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ \pi^2 + 2^x + x^2 + x^{\frac{1}{x}} \Big] \\ &= \ln(2)2^x + 2x + x^{\frac{1}{x}} \frac{1 - \ln(x)}{x^2} }$
At $x = 1$:
$\eqalign{ y'(1) &= \ln(2)2^{(1)} + 2(1) + (1)^{\frac{1}{(1)}} \frac{1 - \ln((1))}{(1)^2} \\ &= 2\ln(2) + 3 }$
Since we want the slope to the perpendicular line, we want the negative reciprocal:
$\eqalign{ m = -\frac{1}{2\ln(2) + 3} }$
