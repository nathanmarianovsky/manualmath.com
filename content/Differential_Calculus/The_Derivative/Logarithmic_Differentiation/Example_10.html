Problem
You are given that:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} x^x = x^x (\ln(x) + 1) }$
and tetration, the next hyperoperation after exponentiation, is defined as:
$\eqalign{ {\ ^{n}x = \ \atop {\ }} {{\underbrace{x^{x^{\cdot^{\cdot^{x}}}}}} \atop n} }$
Determine the first derivative of the general tetration function by developing an algorithm.
Solution
We are given the base case, so all that is left is the general step:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= {\ ^{n}x \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln({\ ^{n}x) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(\ \atop {\ }} {{\underbrace{x^{x^{\cdot^{\cdot^{x}}}}}} \atop n}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= {\ ^{n - 1}x \ln(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x \ln(x) \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \ln(x) \frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x + \frac{\frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x}{x} \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \Big[ \ln(x) \frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x + \frac{\frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x}{x} \Big] \\ &= {\ ^{n}x \Big[ \ln(x) \frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x + \frac{\frac{d}{dx} {\ ^{n - 1}x}{x} \Big] }$
