Problem
You are given that:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} x^x = x^x (\ln(x) + 1) }$
and tetration, the next hyperoperation after exponentiation, is defined as:
$\eqalign{ {\ ^{n}x = \ \atop {\ }} {{\underbrace{x^{x^{\cdot^{\cdot^{x}}}}}} \atop n} }$
Determine the first derivative of the general tetration function by developing a recursive algorithm.
Solution
We are given the base case, so all that is left is the general step:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= {}^{n}x \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln({}^{n}x) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln\Bigg( x^{x^{\cdot^{\cdot^{x}}}} \Bigg) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= {}^{n - 1}x \ln(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} ({}^{n - 1}x \ln(x)) \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \ln(x) \frac{d}{dx} ({}^{n - 1}x) + \frac{\frac{d}{dx} {}^{n - 1}x}{x} \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \Big[ \ln(x) \frac{d}{dx} ({}^{n - 1}x) + \frac{{}^{n - 1}x}{x} \Big] \\ &= {}^{n}x \Big[ \ln(x) \frac{d}{dx} ({}^{n - 1}x) + \frac{{}^{n - 1}x}{x} \Big] }$
Now we have everything we need. The algorithm involves a function that performs the following:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{Procedure}$
$> 2$ Take the derivative of the function according to the general rule and have it call on itself inside
$= 2$ Take the derivative of the function according to the base case
