General Approach
Logarithmic differentiation is not really a topic on its own, but is definitely worth a quick look. In certain scenarios taking the logarithm before differentiating might make the latter easier. Specifically consider the following fact:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(f(x)) = \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} }$
which leads to:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \ln(f(x)) }$
So essentially in the case where taking the derivative of the logarithm is easier, this method should be used.
