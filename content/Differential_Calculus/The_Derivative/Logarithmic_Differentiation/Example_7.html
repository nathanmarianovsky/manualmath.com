Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} }$
Solution
Taking the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln\Big( \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} \Big) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= x\ln(\ln(x)) - (3x + 1)\ln(2) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ x\ln(\ln(x)) - (3x + 1)\ln(2) \Big] \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \ln(\ln(x)) + \frac{1}{\ln(x)} - 3\ln(2) \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \Big[ \ln(\ln(x)) + \frac{1}{\ln(x)} - 3\ln(2) \Big] \\ &= \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} \Big[ \ln(\ln(x)) + \frac{1}{\ln(x)} - 3\ln(2) \Big] }$
