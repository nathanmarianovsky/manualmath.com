Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln\Big( \frac{(\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} \Big) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= x\ln(\ln(x)) - (3x + 1)\ln(2) \\ (\ln(y(x)))' &= \Big[ x\ln(\ln(x)) - (3x + 1)\ln(2) \Big]' \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \ln(\ln(x)) + \frac{1}{\ln(x)} - 3\ln(2) \\ y'(x) &= \ln(\ln(x))y(x) + \frac{y(x)}{\ln(x)} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 3\ln(2)y(x) \\ &= \frac{(\ln(x))^x\ln(\ln(x))}{2^{3x + 1}} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \frac{(\ln(x))^{x - 1}}{2^{3x + 1}} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{(3\ln(2)\ln(x))^x}{2^{3x + 1}} }$
