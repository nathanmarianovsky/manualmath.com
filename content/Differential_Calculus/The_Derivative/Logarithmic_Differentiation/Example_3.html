Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = (3x^2 + 5)^{\frac{1}{x}} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= (3x^2 + 5)^{\frac{1}{x}} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln\Big( (3x^2 + 5)^{\frac{1}{x}} \Big) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{\ln(3x^2 + 5)}{x} \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \frac{\ln(3x^2 + 5)}{x} \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \frac{\frac{6x^2}{3x^2 + 5} - \ln(3x^2 + 5)}{x^2} \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \cdot \frac{\frac{6x^2}{3x^2 + 5} - \ln(3x^2 + 5)}{x^2} \\ &= (3x^2 + 5)^{\frac{1}{x}} \Big[ \frac{6}{3x^2 + 5} - \frac{\ln(3x^2 + 5)}{x^2} \Big] }$
