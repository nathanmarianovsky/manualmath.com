Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(x)\ln(x) + 3x\ln(\sec(x)) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ (\ln(x))^2 + 3x\ln(\sec(x)) \Big] \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \frac{2\ln(x)}{x} + 3\ln(\sec(x)) + 3x\tan(x) \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \Big[ \frac{2\ln(x)}{x} + 3\ln(\sec(x)) + 3x\tan(x) \Big] \\ &= x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x} \Big[ \frac{2\ln(x)}{x} + 3\ln(\sec(x)) + 3x\tan(x) \Big] }$
