Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(x)\ln(x) + 3x\ln(\sec(x)) \\ (\ln(y(x)))' &= \Big[ (\ln(x))^2 + 3x\ln(\sec(x)) \Big]' \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \frac{2\ln(x)}{x} + 3\ln(\sec(x)) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3x\tan(x) \\ y'(x) &= \frac{2\ln(x)}{x}y(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3\ln(\sec(x))y(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3x\tan(x)y(x) \\ &= 2\ln(x)x^{\ln(x) - 1}(\sec(x))^{3x} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3\ln(\sec(x))x^{\ln(x)}(\sec(x))^{3x} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3x^{\ln(x) + 1}(\sec(x))^{3x}\tan(x) }$
