Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = 7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= 7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(7x) + \frac{x}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ \ln(7x) + \frac{x}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) \Big] \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{x\sin(x)}{2\cos(x)} \\ y'(x) &= \frac{1}{x}y(x) + \frac{1}{2}\ln(\cos(x))y(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{x\sin(x)}{2\cos(x)}y(x) \\ &= 7(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \frac{7}{2}\ln(\cos(x))x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{7x^2\sin(x)(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2} - 1}}{2} }$
