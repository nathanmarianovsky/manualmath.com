Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = 7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= 7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(7) + \ln(x) + \frac{x}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ \ln(7) + \ln(x) + \frac{x}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) \Big] \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) - \frac{x\sin(x)}{2\cos(x)} \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \Big[ \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) - \frac{x\sin(x)}{2\cos(x)} \Big] \\ &= 7x(\cos(x))^{\frac{x}{2}} \Big[ \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{2}\ln(\cos(x)) - \frac{x\sin(x)}{2\cos(x)} \Big] }$
