Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = x^x }$
Solution
Taking the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= x^x \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln(x^x) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= x\ln(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} x\ln(x) \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= \ln(x) + 1 \\ y'(x) &= y(x)(\ln(x) + 1) \\ &= x^x(\ln(x) + 1) }$
