Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = (\sin(x))^{x^3} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= (\sin(x))^{x^3} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln((\sin(x))^{x^3}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= x^3\ln(\sin(x)) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} x^3\ln(\sin(x)) \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= 3x^2\ln(\sin(x)) + \frac{\cos(x)x^3}{\sin(x)} \\ y'(x) &= 3x^2\ln(\sin(x))y(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \frac{\cos(x)x^3}{\sin(x)}y(x) \\ &= 3x^2(\sin(x))^{x^3}\ln(\sin(x)) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \cos(x)x^3(\sin(x))^{x^3 - 1} }$
