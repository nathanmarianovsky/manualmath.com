Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = (1 - 3x)^{\cos(x)} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= (1 - 3x)^{\cos(x)} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln((1- 3x)^{\cos(x)}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \cos(x)\ln(1 - 3x) \\ (\ln(y(x)))' &= \Big[ \cos(x)\ln(1 - 3x) \Big]' \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= -\sin(x)\ln(1 - 3x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{3\cos(x)}{1 - 3x} \\ y'(x) &= -\sin(x)\ln(1 - 3x)y(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - \frac{3\cos(x)y(x)}{1 - 3x} \\ &= \ln\Bigg( \Big( \frac{1}{1 - 3x} \Big)^{\sin(x)(1 - 3x)^{\cos(x)}} \Bigg) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 3\cos(x)(1 - 3x)^{\cos(x) - 1} }$
