Problem
Find the derivative of:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = (1 - 3x)^{\cos(x)} }$
Solution
Take the logarithm of both sides and differentiate:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= (1 - 3x)^{\cos(x)} \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln((1- 3x)^{\cos(x)}) \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \cos(x)\ln(1 - 3x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(y(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \cos(x)\ln(1 - 3x) \\ \frac{y'(x)}{y(x)} &= -\sin(x)\ln(1 - 3x) - \frac{3\cos(x)}{1 - 3x} \\ y'(x) &= y(x) \Big[ -\sin(x)\ln(1 - 3x) - \frac{3\cos(x)}{1 - 3x} \Big] \\ &= -(1 - 3x)^{\cos(x)} \Big[ \sin(x)\ln(1 - 3x) + \frac{3\cos(x)}{1 - 3x} \Big] }$
