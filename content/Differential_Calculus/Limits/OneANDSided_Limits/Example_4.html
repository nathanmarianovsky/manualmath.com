Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \begin{cases} 2x + 1 & x < 1 \\ 5 & x = 1 \\ 7x^2 - 4 & x > 1 \end{cases} }$
determine the following:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^-} f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^+} f(x) }$
using an analytical approach. After having found the results for the above, what can be said of the limit as $x \rightarrow 1$?
Solution
Since each component of the piecewise function is continuous, we can directly evaluate the limits using the correct components:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^-} f(x) &= 2(1) + 1 = 3 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^+} f(x) &= 7(1)^2 - 4 = 3 }$
Since the limits equate, the general limit is:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1} f(x) = 3 }$
