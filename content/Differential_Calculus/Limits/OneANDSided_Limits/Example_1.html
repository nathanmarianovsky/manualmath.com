Problem
Given:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{x^2 - 2x} & x \neq 2 \\ 6 & x = 2 \end{cases} }$
determine the following:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^-} g(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^+} g(x) }$
using a numerical approach. After having found the results for the above, what can be said of the limit as $x \rightarrow 2$?
Solution
From the table of values:
$\textbf{x}$ $\textbf{f(x)}$
$1.900$ $4.158$
$1.990$ $4.015$
$1.999$ $4.002$
$2.001$ $3.999$
$2.010$ $3.985$
$2.100$ $3.857$
we have enough evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^-} g(x) = \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^+} g(x) = 4 }$
thus giving:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2} g(x) = 4 }$
