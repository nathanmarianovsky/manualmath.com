Problem
Prove that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{x - 2|x|}{|x|} = \text{DNE} }$
Solution
The function of interest can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ \frac{x - 2|x|}{|x|} = \begin{cases} -3 & x < 0 \\ -1 & x > 0 \end{cases} }$
which provides evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} f(x) &= -3 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} f(x) &= -1 }$
Since the limits do not equate, the general limit is:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} f(x) = \text{DNE} }$
