Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = e^{\frac{1}{x}} }$
determine the following:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} f(x) }$
using a numerical approach. After having found the results for the above, what can be said of the limit as $x \rightarrow 0$?
Solution
First the table of values:
$\textbf{x}$ $\textbf{f(x)}$
$-0.100$ $4.540 \times 10^{-5}$
$-0.010$ $3.720 \times 10^{-44}$
$-0.001$ $5.076 \times 10^{-435}$
$0.001$ $1.970 \times 10^{434}$
$0.010$ $2.688 \times 10^{43}$
$0.100$ $2.203 \times 10^4$
which provides evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} f(x) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} f(x) &= \infty }$
Since the limits do not equate, the general limit is:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} f(x) = \text{DNE} }$
