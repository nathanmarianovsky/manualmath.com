Problem
Given:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = \frac{\sin(x)}{x} }$
determine the following:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} g(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} g(x) }$
using a numerical approach. After having found the results for the above, what can be said of the limit as $x \rightarrow 0$?
Solution
From the table of values:
$\textbf{x}$ $\textbf{f(x)}$
$-0.100$ $0.998$
$-0.010$ $1.000$
$-0.001$ $1.000$
$0.001$ $1.000$
$0.010$ $1.000$
$0.100$ $0.998$
we have enough evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} g(x) = \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} g(x) = 1 }$
thus giving:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} g(x) = 1 }$
