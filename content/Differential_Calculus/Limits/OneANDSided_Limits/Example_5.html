Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \frac{x^4 - 13x^3 - 120x + 64}{x - 4} }$
determine the following:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 4^-} f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{x \rightarrow 4^+} f(x) }$
using a numerical approach. After having found the results for the above, what can be said of the limit as $x \rightarrow 4$?
Solution
First the table of values:
$\textbf{x}$ $\textbf{f(x)}$
$3.900$ $9.438 \times 10^3$
$3.990$ $9.871 \times 10^4$
$3.999$ $9.915 \times 10^5$
$4.001$ $-9.925 \times 10^5$
$4.010$ $-9.969 \times 10^4$
$4.100$ $-1.041 \times 10^4$
which provides evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 4^-} f(x) &= \infty \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 4^+} f(x) &= -\infty }$
Since the limits equate, the general limit is:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 4} f(x) = \text{DNE} }$
