Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \frac{x^4 - 13x^3 - 120x + 64}{x - 4} }$
determine the following:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^-} f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^+} f(x) }$
using a numerical approach. After having found the results for the above, what can be said of the limit as $x \rightarrow 1$?
Solution
First the table of values:
$\textbf{x}$ $\textbf{f(x)}$
$0.900$ $0.839$
$0.990$ $1.889$
$0.999$ $1.989$
$1.001$ $2.011$
$1.010$ $2.109$
$1.100$ $3.041$
which provides evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^-} f(x) &= 2 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1^+} f(x) &= 2 }$
Since the limits equate, the general limit is:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 1} f(x) = 2 }$
