Problem
Prove that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{1 + 2^{-\frac{1}{x}}} = \text{DNE} }$
Solution
First the table of values:
$\textbf{x}$ $\textbf{f(x)}$
$-0.100$ $9.756 \times 10^{-4}$
$-0.010$ $7.889 \times 10^{-31}$
$-0.001$ $9.333 \times 10^{-302}$
$0.001$ $1.000$
$0.010$ $1.000$
$0.100$ $0.999$
which provides evidence to state that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} f(x) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} f(x) &= 1 }$
Since the limits do not equate, the general limit is:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} f(x) = \text{DNE} }$
