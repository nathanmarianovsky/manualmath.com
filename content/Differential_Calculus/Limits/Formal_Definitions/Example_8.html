Problem
Given that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} cf(x) &= c\lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(g(x)) &= f\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) \Big) }$
Use this to prove:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n = \Big[ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big]^n \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{R} }$
where you may assume the limit on the right-hand side exists.
Solution
Taking the left-hand side of the statement and rewriting it:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} e^{\ln((f(x))^n)} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} e^{n \cdot \ln(f(x))} \\ &= e^{\lim_{x \rightarrow a} n \cdot \ln(f(x))} \\ &= e^{n\lim_{x \rightarrow a} \ln(f(x))} \\ &= e^{n\ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big)} \\ &= e^{\ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big)^n} \\ &= \Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big)^n }$
