Problem
You may assume the following properties:
$\eqalign{ &\lim_{x \rightarrow a} c = c \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} c \in \mathbb{R} \\ &\lim_{x \rightarrow a} (\alpha f(x) + \beta g(x)) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} = \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) + \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \alpha, \beta \in \mathbb{R} }$
to prove that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x)g(x) = \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) }$
by using the $(\delta, \epsilon)$ definition of the limit and assuming that the limits on the right-hand side exist.
Solution
Assuming that the limit of the functions exist:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) &= L_1 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) &= L_2 }$
then by the properties allowed to use we can state:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x) - L_1) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (g(x) - L_2) &= 0 }$
If these new limits exist, then we know $\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta_1, \delta_2 > 0$ s.t.:
$\eqalign{ |(f(x) - L_1) &- 0| < \sqrt{\epsilon} \\ &\text{whenever} \hspace{.3cm} |x - a| < \delta_1 \\ |(g(x) - L_2) &- 0| < \sqrt{\epsilon} \\ &\text{whenever} \hspace{.3cm} |x - a| < \delta_2 }$
Now observe what happens to the multiplication of the two:
$\eqalign{ &\Big| (f(x) - L_1)(g(x) - L_2) - 0 \Big| \\ &= \Big| f(x) - L_1 \Big| \Big| g(x) - L_2 \Big| \\ &< \sqrt{\epsilon}\sqrt{\epsilon} \\ &= \epsilon }$
which proves that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x) - L_1)(g(x) - L_2) = 0 }$
Of course this is not the limit we want, but it is going to be useful in what comes next. Notice what happens when we multiply out of the product:
$\eqalign{ &(f(x) - L_1)(g(x) - L_2) \\ &= f(x)g(x) - L_1g(x) - L_2f(x) + L_1L_2 }$
and rewriting gives:
$\eqalign{ &f(x)g(x) \\ &= (f(x) - L_1)(g(x) - L_2) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + L_1g(x) + L_2f(x) - L_1L_2 }$
which provides a way to write out the multiplication of the two original functions. Applying the limit to both sides and using the allowed properties:
$\eqalign{ &\lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x)g(x) \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \Big[ (f(x) - L_1)(g(x) - L_2) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + L_1g(x) + L_2f(x) - L_1L_2 \Big] \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x) - L_1)(g(x) - L_2) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + L_1\lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) + L_2\lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) - \lim_{x \rightarrow a} L_1L_2 \\ &= 0 + L_1L_2 + L_2L_1 - L_1L_2 \\ &= L_1L_2 }$
