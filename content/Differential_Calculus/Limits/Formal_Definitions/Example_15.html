Problem
Given that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} cf(x) &= c\lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(g(x)) &= f\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) \Big) }$
Use this to prove that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n = \Big[ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big]^n \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{R} }$
by assuming the limit on the right-hand side exists.
Solution
Begin by defining a new function for the one of interest, take the logarithm of both sides, apply the limit, and simplify:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= (f(x))^n \\ \ln(y(x)) &= \ln((f(x))^n) \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \ln(y(x)) &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} n\ln(f(x)) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} y(x) \Big) &= n \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \ln(f(x)) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} y(x) \Big) &= n \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} y(x) \Big) &= \ln\Big( \Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big)^n \Big) \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} y(x) &= \Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big)^n \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n &= \Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big)^n }$
