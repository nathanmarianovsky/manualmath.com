Problem
Prove:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} cf(x) = c\lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} c \in \mathbb{R} }$
assuming that the limit on the right-hand side exists.
Solution
Assuming that the limit of the function exists:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = L }$
then we know $\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists \delta_1 > 0$ s.t.:
$\eqalign{ |f(x) - L| < \frac{\epsilon}{|c|} \hspace{.3cm} \text{whenever} \hspace{.3cm} |x - a| < \delta_1 }$
We need to show:
$\eqalign{ |cf(x) - cL| < \epsilon \hspace{.3cm} \text{whenever} \hspace{.3cm} |x - a| < \delta }$
So we choose $\delta = \delta_1$ to use the inequality for $f(x)$ to obtain:
$\eqalign{ |cf(x) - cL| &= |c||f(x) - L| \\ &< |c| \cdot \frac{\epsilon}{|c|} \\ &= \epsilon }$
