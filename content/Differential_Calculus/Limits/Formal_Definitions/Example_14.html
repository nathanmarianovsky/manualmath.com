Problem
Given that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x)g(x) = \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) }$
Use this to prove that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n = \Big[ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big]^n \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{N} }$
by assuming the limit on the right-hand side exists.
Solution
For the natural numbers, induction is a perfect method to prove the given statement. For the base case we can do $n = 1$, but a more interesting case is $n = 2$ which gives:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^2 &= \Big[ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big]^2 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x)f(x) &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \cdot \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) }$
which holds true due to what we are given. Now we assume the original statement and try to see if it holds true for $n + 1$:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^{n + 1} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n f(x) \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow a} (f(x))^n \cdot \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \\ &= \Big[ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big]^n \cdot \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \\ &= \Big[ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big]^{n + 1} }$
