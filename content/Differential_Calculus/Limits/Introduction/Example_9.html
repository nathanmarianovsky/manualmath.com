Problem
Evaluate both expressions:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} a^b \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} a^b }$
using an analytical approach where interchanging the limits is strictly prohibited. Do you get the same value for both limits? If not, discuss the numerical nature of $0^0$.
Solution
The first expression provides:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} a^b &= \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} \Big( \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} a^b \Big) \\ &= \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} a^0 \\ &= \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} 1 \\ &= 1 }$
while the second expression gives:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} a^b &= \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} \Big( \lim_{a \rightarrow 0} a^b \Big) \\ &= \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} 0^b \\ &= \lim_{b \rightarrow 0} 0 \\ &= 0 }$
After evaluation it becomes clear that these two expressions approach different values even though they both go to $0^0$. It is exactly because of this that $0^0$ is called an indeterminate form since it cannot take on one specific numerical value.
