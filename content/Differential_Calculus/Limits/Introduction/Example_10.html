Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^i \Bigg[ \prod_{k = 0}^m x^{-k} \Bigg]^{-1} }$
assuming that $n$ and $m$ are some positive integers.
Solution
This can be broken up into two different scenarios. Simplifying the expression provides:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^i \Bigg[ \prod_{k = 0}^m x^k \Bigg]^{-1} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^i \Bigg[ (1)(x)(x^2)\dots(x^m) \Bigg] \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^i \Bigg[ x^{1 + 2 + \dots + m} \Bigg] \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^i \Bigg[ x^{\sum_{k = 1}^m k} \Bigg] \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^{i + \sum_{k = 1}^m k} }$
It should be clear that all terms have strictly positive powers, thus:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \sum_{i = 0}^n x^{i + \sum_{k = 1}^m k} = 0 }$
since any positive power of zero is always zero.
