Problem
Determine the number of steps needed for the Bisection Method on the interval $[a,b]$ given we want the error to be $\epsilon$ at most?
Solution
From the notes we know that the subintervals satisfy the relation:
$\eqalign{ |I_n| = \frac{|I_0|}{2^n} }$
and so we can rewrite this as:
$\eqalign{ |c_n - c| = \frac{|b - a|}{2^n} }$
where $c_n$ is the $n$th approximation and $c$ is the desired value. The left side represents the error and can be used to solve for the number of steps:
$\eqalign{ \epsilon &= \frac{\epsilon_0}{2^n} \\ 2^n &= \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} \\ n &= \log_2\Big( \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} \Big) }$
Obviously we should account for the fact that $n$ is strictly a natural number in real life, and thus to guarantee a whole number we round up:
$\eqalign{ \text{# of steps} \hspace{.2cm} = \Bigg\lceil \log_2\Big( \frac{\epsilon_0}{\epsilon} \Big) \Bigg\rceil }$
