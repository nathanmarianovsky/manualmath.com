Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) = x^2 }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Bisection Method to determine $x_5$, the fifth iteration, on the interval $\Big[ \frac{1}{2}, 1 \Big]$.
Solution
First we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = \sin(x) - x^2 }$
and identify the function of interest to be:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(x) - x^2 }$
The iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$ $f(\textbf{x}_n)$
$0$ $0.75000$ $0.11914$
$1$ $0.87500$ $0.00192$
$2$ $0.93750$ $-0.07283$
$3$ $0.90625$ $-0.03409$
$4$ $0.89063$ $-0.015753$
$5$ $0.88281$ $-0.00683$
