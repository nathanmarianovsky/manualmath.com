Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ e^{\sin(x)} = -x }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Bisection Method to determine $x_5$, the fifth iteration, on the interval $[-1,0]$.
Solution
First we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = e^{\sin(x)} + x }$
and identify the function of interest to be:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = e^{\sin(x)} + x }$
The iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$ $f(\textbf{x}_n)$
$0$ $-0.50000$ $0.11914$
$1$ $-0.75000$ $-0.24421$
$2$ $-0.62500$ $-0.06795$
$3$ $-0.56250$ $0.02416$
$4$ $-0.59375$ $-0.02224$
$5$ $-0.57813$ $0.00087$
