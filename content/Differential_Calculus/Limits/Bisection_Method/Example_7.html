Problem
Using the Bisection Method determine $x_5$, the fifth iteration up to five decimal places, as an approximation of the root to the equation:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(x) }$
on the interval $[1,99]$. Does the Bisection Method manage to find all the roots on the interval? What does this indicate about the algorithm?
Solution
The iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$ $f(\textbf{x}_n)$
$0$ $50.00000$ $-0.26237$
$1$ $25.50000$ $0.35906$
$2$ $37.75000$ $0.05087$
$3$ $43.87500$ $-0.10709$
$4$ $40.81250$ $0.02820$
$5$ $42.34375$ $-0.99771$
Unfortunately the method only produced one root, but it is a known fact that there are more. This indicates that the algorithm in general does not find all the roots on a given interval, nor does it even indicate where the other ones are.
