Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ \ln(x) = x^2 - 4 }$
approximate the rightmost solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Bisection Method to determine $x_5$, the fifth iteration.
Solution
Before anything else we need to determine a suitable interval to search. This can be accomplished by graphing the left and right hand sides independently:
Looking at the graph shows that $[1,3]$ is a proper interval. Now we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = \ln(x) - x^2 + 4 }$
and identify the function of interest to be:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \ln(x) - x^2 + 4 }$
The iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$ $f(\textbf{x}_n)$
$0$ $2.00000$ $0.69315$
$1$ $2.50000$ $-1.33371$
$2$ $2.25000$ $-0.25157$
$3$ $2.12500$ $0.23815$
$4$ $2.18750$ $-0.00240$
$5$ $2.15625$ $0.11896$
