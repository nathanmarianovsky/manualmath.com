Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ 6x = \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Bisection Method to determine $x_5$, the fifth iteration.
Solution
Before anything else we need to determine a suitable interval to search. This can be accomplished by graphing the left and right hand sides independently:
Looking at the graph shows that $[0,1]$ is a proper interval. Now we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = 6x - \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
and identify the function of interest to be:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = 6x - \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
The iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$ $f(\textbf{x}_n)$
$0$ $0.50000$ $2.13397$
$1$ $0.25000$ $0.53175$
$2$ $0.12500$ $-0.24216$
$3$ $0.18750$ $0.14274$
$4$ $0.15625$ $-0.05022$
$5$ $0.17188$ $0.04613$
