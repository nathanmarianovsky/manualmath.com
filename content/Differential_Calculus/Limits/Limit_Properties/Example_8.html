Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{|x - 2|} }$
Solution
With the absolute value in play we have to be a little careful at $x = 2$. Formally:
$\eqalign{ \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{|x - 2|} = \begin{cases} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{-(x - 2)} & x < 2 \\ \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{x - 2} & x > 2 \end{cases} }$
Looking at the left side of the limit:
$\eqalign{ &\lim_{x \rightarrow 2^-} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{-(x - 2)} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^-} \frac{(x + 6)(x - 2)}{-(x - 2)} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^-} -(x + 6) \\ &= -8 }$
and the right side:
$\eqalign{ &\lim_{x \rightarrow 2^+} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{x - 2} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^+} \frac{(x + 6)(x - 2)}{x - 2} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^+} (x + 6) \\ &= 8 }$
This provides enough evidence to state:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2} \frac{x^2 + 4x - 12}{|x - 2|} = \text{DNE} }$
