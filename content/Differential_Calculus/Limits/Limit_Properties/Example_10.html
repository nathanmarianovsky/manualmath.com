Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{|2x - 1| - |2x + 1|}{x} }$
Solution
Approaching this directly is too hard, so instead lets simplify the expression at hand. First:
$\eqalign{ |2x - 1| &= \begin{cases} -(2x - 1) & x < \frac{1}{2} \\ 2x - 1 & x \geq \frac{1}{2} \end{cases} \\ |2x + 1| &= \begin{cases} -(2x + 1) & x < -\frac{1}{2} \\ 2x + 1 & x \geq -\frac{1}{2} \end{cases} }$
Notice that this breakup produces three regions of interest in relation to our original expression. Explicitly we have:
$\eqalign{ \frac{|2x - 1| - |2x + 1|}{x} = \begin{cases} \frac{2}{x} & x < -\frac{1}{2} \\ -4 & -\frac{1}{2} \leq x \leq \frac{1}{2} \\ -\frac{2}{x} & x > \frac{1}{2} \end{cases} }$
Clearly we have no issue at our point of interest so:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{|2x - 1| - |2x + 1|}{x} = -4 }$
