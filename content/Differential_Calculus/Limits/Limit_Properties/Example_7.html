Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2} \frac{x^2 + 1}{x - 2} }$
Solution
Note that when plugged in directly we arrive at division by zero. To make sense of this lets observe what happens on each side. The numerator in this scenario is always positive so we only have to watch out for the behavior of the denominator. Specifically, from the left side the denominator will always be negative giving:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^-} \frac{x^2 + 1}{x - 2} &= -\infty }$
On the right side the denominator is always positive giving:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2^+} \frac{x^2 + 1}{x - 2} &= \infty }$
With this we are allowed to say:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 2} \frac{x^2 + 1}{x - 2} &= \text{DNE} }$
