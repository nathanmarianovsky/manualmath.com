Problem
Find numbers $a$ and $b$ s.t.:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sqrt{ax + b} - 2}{x} = 1 }$
Solution
Begin by reorganizing the fraction:
$\eqalign{ \frac{\sqrt{ax + b} - 2}{x} &= \frac{\sqrt{ax + b} - 2}{x} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{ax + b} + 2}{\sqrt{ax + b} + 2} \\ &= \frac{ax + b - 4}{x(\sqrt{ax + b} + 2)} }$
Now notice that we ultimately want to get rid of the $x$ in the denominator because it causes issues for the limit. So let $b = 4$ to get:
$\eqalign{ \frac{ax + b - 4}{x(\sqrt{ax + b} + 2)} &= \frac{ax}{x(\sqrt{ax + 4} + 2)} \\ &= \frac{a}{\sqrt{ax + 4} + 2} }$
Now taking the limit gives:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{a}{\sqrt{ax + 4} + 2} &= \frac{a}{4} }$
To make this limit equate to one, we let $a = 1$.
