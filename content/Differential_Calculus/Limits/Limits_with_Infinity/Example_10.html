Problem
You are told that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \ln(f(x)) = \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big) }$
Use this information to evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} n^{\frac{1}{n}} }$
Solution
To begin let:
$\eqalign{ y(n) = n^{\frac{1}{n}} }$
and continue by taking the logarithm of both sides and applying a limit:
$\eqalign{ y(n) &= n^{\frac{1}{n}} \\ \ln(y(n)) &= \ln\Big( n^{\frac{1}{n}} \Big) \\ \ln(y(n)) &= \frac{\ln(n)}{n} \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \ln(y(n)) &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln(n)}{n} \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y(n) \Big) &= 0 \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y(n) &= e^0 \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y(n) &= 1 }$
where the limit of the logarithm over the polynomial approaches zero because polynomials dominate logarithms at large values of $n$.
