Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \sqrt{x^2 + x + 1} - x }$
Solution
Multiply the numerator and denominator by the conjugate. Then as $x$ approaches large values throw out the insignificant components:
$\eqalign{ &\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \sqrt{x^2 + x + 1} - x \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} (\sqrt{x^2 + x + 1} - x) \cdot \frac{\sqrt{x^2 + x + 1} + x}{\sqrt{x^2 + x + 1} + x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x + 1}{\sqrt{x^2 + x + 1} + x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x + 1}{\sqrt{x^2} + x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x}{2x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} }$
