Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x}{\sqrt{16x^2 + 1}} }$
Solution
As $x$ approaches large values the constant in the denominator becomes insignificant:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x}{\sqrt{16x^2 + 1}} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x}{\sqrt{16x^2}} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x}{4x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3}{4} \\ &= \frac{3}{4} }$
