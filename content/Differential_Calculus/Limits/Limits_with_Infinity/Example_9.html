Problem
You are told that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \ln(f(x)) = \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \Big) }$
Use this information to evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \log\Big( \frac{x^6 - 500}{x^6 + 500} \Big) }$
Solution
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \log\Big( \frac{x^6 - 500}{x^6 + 500} \Big) &= \log\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x^6 - 500}{x^6 + 500} \Big) \\ &= \log(1) \\ &= 0 }$
