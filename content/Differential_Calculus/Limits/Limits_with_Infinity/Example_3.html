Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow -\infty} \frac{x - 1}{2x^2 + 3} }$
Solution
As $x$ approaches small values the constants in the numerator and denominator become insignificant:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow -\infty} \frac{x - 1}{2x^2 + 3} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -\infty} \frac{x}{2x^2} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -\infty} \frac{1}{2x} \\ &= 0 }$
