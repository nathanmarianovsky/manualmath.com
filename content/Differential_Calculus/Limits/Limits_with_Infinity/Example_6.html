Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{5^x}{3^x + 2^x} }$
Solution
Divide throughout by the biggest exponential and conclude what happens to a fractional value when raised to higher powers:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{5^x}{3^x + 2^x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{\Big( \frac{3}{5} \Big)^x + \Big( \frac{2}{5} \Big)^x} \\ &= \frac{1}{0 + 0} \\ &= \infty }$
Another way to arrive at infinity can be done by noting that the exponential in the numerator dominates both of the ones in the denominator at very large values of $x$.
