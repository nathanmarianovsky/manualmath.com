Limits at Infinity
To analyze a function's end behavior, we look at the two limits all the way on the left and right:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow -\infty} f(x) &= L_1 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} f(x) &= L_2 }$
where $L_1$ and $L_2$ are some real numbers, generally not the same.
Horizontal Asymptotes
Determining the $\textit{horizontal asymptotes}$ for a given rational function is exactly the same as computing the limits at infinity. In general given the rational function:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = \frac{a_0 + a_1x + a_2x^2 + \dots + a_nx^n}{b_0 + b_1x + b_2x^2 + \dots + b_mx^m} }$
there exist a couple of different scenarios:
$\textbf{Situation}$ $\eqalign{\lim_{x \rightarrow -\infty} g(x)}$ $\eqalign{\lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} g(x) = \textbf{Horizontal Asymptote}}$
$n > m$: One odd, the other even $-\infty$ $\infty$
$n > m$: Both odd, or even $\infty$ $\infty$
$n = m$ $\frac{a_n}{b_m}$ $\frac{a_n}{b_m}$
$n < m$ $0$ $0$
