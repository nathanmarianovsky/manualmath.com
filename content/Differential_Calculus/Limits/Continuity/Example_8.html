Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} }$
Solution
We know that the range of the sine function is restricted as:
$\eqalign{ -1 \leq \sin(u) \leq 1 \hspace{.3cm} \forall u \in \mathbb{R} }$
This gives the perfect setup for the squeeze theorem:
$\eqalign{ -1 \leq &\sin(x) \leq 1 \\ -\frac{1}{x} \leq &\frac{\sin(x)}{x} \leq \frac{1}{x} \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} -\frac{1}{x} \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} &\frac{\sin(x)}{x} \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{x} \\ 0 \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} &\frac{\sin(x)}{x} \leq 0 }$
Since both sides approach zero, then so must the middle giving:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} = 0 }$
