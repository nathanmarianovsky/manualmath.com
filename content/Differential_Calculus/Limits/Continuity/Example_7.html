Problem
Evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} x^2\sin\Big( \frac{1}{x} \Big) }$
Solution
We know that the range of the sine function is restricted as:
$\eqalign{ -1 \leq \sin(u) \leq 1 \hspace{.3cm} \forall u \in \mathbb{R} }$
This gives the perfect setup for the squeeze theorem:
$\eqalign{ -1 \leq &\sin\Big( \frac{1}{x} \Big) \leq 1 \\ -x^2 \leq &x^2\sin\Big( \frac{1}{x} \Big) \leq x^2 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} -x^2 \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} &x^2\sin\Big( \frac{1}{x} \Big) \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} x^2 \\ 0 \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} &x^2\sin\Big( \frac{1}{x} \Big) \leq 0 }$
Since both sides approach zero, then so must the middle giving:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} x^2\sin\Big( \frac{1}{x} \Big) = 0 }$
