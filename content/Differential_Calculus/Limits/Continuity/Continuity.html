Introduction
Now we finally introduce continuity and unravel the "nice" behavior that is seen across all continuous functions.
Continuity at a Point
A function, $f(x)$, is said to be $\textit{continuous at a point}$, $x = a$, if:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} &f(x) \hspace{.2cm} \text{exists}, \hspace{.1cm} f(a) \hspace{.2cm} \text{is defined,} \\ &\text{and} \hspace{.2cm} f(a) = \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) }$
Continuity on an Interval
A function, $f(x)$, is said to be $\textit{continuous}$ on an interval, $S \subseteq \mathbb{R}$, if it continuous at every point in $S$.
Continuous Functions
Given two continuous functions, $f(x)$ and $g(x)$, the following are also continuous:
$\eqalign{ \text{Scalar Multiple:}& \hspace{.3cm} \alpha f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \alpha \in \mathbb{R} \\ \text{Sum:}& \hspace{.3cm} f(x) + g(x) \\ \text{Product:}& \hspace{.3cm} f(x)g(x) \\ \text{Quotient:}& \hspace{.3cm} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} g(x) \neq 0 }$
Limit of Composition of Functions
Given that a function, $f(x)$, is continuous at $x = L$ where:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) = L }$
then:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(g(x)) = f\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) \Big) }$
Squeeze Theorem
Limits are known to preserve inequalities, so given two functions s.t.:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \leq g(x) }$
then:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) \leq \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) }$
Using this information consider a scenario where we have three functions s.t.:
$\eqalign{ h(x) \leq f(x) \leq g(x) }$
and it is known that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} h(x) = \lim_{x \rightarrow a} g(x) = L }$
then:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} f(x) = L }$
Intermediate Value Theorem
Given a continuous function on the closed interval $[a,b]$, according to the $\textit{Intermediate Value Theorem}$ the function is guaranteed to attain:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{R}(f) = \Big[ \min\{f(a),f(b)\}, \max\{f(a),f(b)\} \Big] }$
In other words:
$\eqalign{ \forall \alpha \in \mathcal{R}(f) \hspace{.1cm} \exists \hspace{.1cm} c \in [a,b] \hspace{.3cm} \text{s.t.} \hspace{.3cm} f(c) = \alpha }$
