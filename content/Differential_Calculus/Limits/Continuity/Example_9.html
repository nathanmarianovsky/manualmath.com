Problem
Given the polynomial:
$\eqalign{ p(x) = 2x^3 - 5x^2 - 10x + 5 }$
Show that it has a root in the interval $[-1,2]$.
Solution
To have a root is the same thing as saying there exists a value for $x$ in the interval s.t. $p(x) = 0$. Notice that the endpoints evaluate out to:
$\eqalign{ p(-1) &= 8 \\ p(2) &= -19 }$
Now we state that by the Intermediate Value Theorem since $p(x)$ is a continuous function on the interval and $0 \in [-19,8]$, then there must exist a number in the interval, $c \in [-1,2]$, s.t. $p(c) = 0$.
