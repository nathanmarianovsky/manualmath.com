Problem
Show that there exists some $u \in (0,2)$ s.t.:
$\eqalign{ u^2 + \cos(\pi u) = 4 }$
Solution
Denote the general function of interest to be:
$\eqalign{ f(u) = u^2 + \cos(\pi u) }$
Notice that the endpoints evaluate out to:
$\eqalign{ f(0) &= (0)^2 + \cos(\pi \cdot 0) = 1 \\ f(2) &= (2)^2 + \cos(\pi \cdot 2) = 5 }$
Now we state that by the Intermediate Value Theorem since $p(x)$ is a continuous function on the interval and $4 \in [1,5]$, then there must exist a number in the interval, $c \in (0,2)$, s.t. $f(c) = 4$.
