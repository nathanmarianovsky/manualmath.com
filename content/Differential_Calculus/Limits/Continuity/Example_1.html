Problem
Is the given function continuous?
$\eqalign{ f(t) = \frac{45t^3 - 47}{t^3 - t} }$
If not, where are the discontinuities?
Solution
The rational function will be discontinuous whenever the denominator equates to zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= t^3 - t \\ 0 &= t(t^2 - 1) \\ 0 &= t(t - 1)(t + 1) \\ t &= -1, 0, 1 }$
In other words the above represents the vertical asymptotes.
