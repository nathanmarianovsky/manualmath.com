Problem
Find the derivative of $f(x) = 2x^\frac{1}{2} + \frac{4}{x^2}$.
Solution
According to the power rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} x^n = nx^{n-1} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{R} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( 2x^\frac{1}{2} + \frac{4}{x^2} \Big) = 2 \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^\frac{1}{2} \Big) + 4 \frac{d}{dx} \Big( x^{-2} \Big) = 2 \cdot \frac{1}{2}x^{-\frac{1}{2}} + 4 \cdot -2x^{-3} = x^\frac{1}{2} - 8x^{-3} }$
