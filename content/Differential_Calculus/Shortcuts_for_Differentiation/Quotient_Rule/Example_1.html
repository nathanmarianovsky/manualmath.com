Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = \frac{2x}{\sin(x)}$.
Solution
According to the quotient rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( f(x) \Big) \cdot g(x) - f(x) \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x) \Big)}{(g(x))^2} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) = \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \frac{2x}{\sin(x)} \Big) = \frac{\frac{d}{dx} \Big( 2x \Big) \cdot sin(x) - 2x \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \sin(x) \Big)}{\sin^2(x)} = \frac{2\sin(x) - 2x\cos(x)}{\sin^2(x)} }$
