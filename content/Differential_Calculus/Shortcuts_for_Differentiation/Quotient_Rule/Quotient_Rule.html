Definition
Given $h(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}$, the derivative is defined as:
$\eqalign{h'(x) = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2}}$
where a prime denotes the first derivative. Same as the product rule, $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ must be differentiable at $x$ with an extra requirement that $g(x) \neq 0$.
Proof by Product Rule
$\eqalign{ h(x) &= \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ g(x)h(x) &= f(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big( g(x)h(x) \Big) &= \frac{d}{dx} f(x) \\ g'(x)h(x) + g(x)h'(x) &= f'(x) \\ g(x)h'(x) &= f'(x) - g'(x) \cdot \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)}{g(x)} - \frac{f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} }$
Proof by Logarithmic Differentiation
$\eqalign{ h(x) &= \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ \ln(h(x)) &= \ln \Big( \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Big) \\ \ln(h(x)) &= \ln(f(x)) - \ln(g(x)) \\ \frac{d}{dx} \ln(h(x)) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big( \ln(f(x)) - \ln(g(x)) \Big) \\ \frac{h'(x)}{h(x)} &= \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} - \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)} \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \Big( \frac{f'(x)}{f(x)} - \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)} \Big) \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)}{g(x)} - \frac{f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} \\ h'(x) &= \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{(g(x))^2} }$
