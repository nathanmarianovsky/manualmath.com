Problem
Find the derivative of $h(x) = (x^9 - x^7 + 4x - 3)^{100}$.
Solution
According to the chain rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} f(g(x)) = \frac{df}{dg} \cdot \frac{dg}{dx} }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} h(x) &= \frac{d}{dx} \Bigg( (x^9 - x^7 + 4x - 3)^{100} \Bigg) \\ &= \Big( 100(x^9 - x^7 + 4x - 3)^{99} \Big) \cdot \Big( 9x^8 - 7x^6 + 4 \Big) \\ &= 100(9x^8 - 7x^6 + 4)(x^9 - x^7 + 4x - 3)^{99} }$
