Problem
You are given the following information:
$x$ $f(x)$ $f'(x)$
$0$ $7$ $5$
$1$ $-2$ $-1$
$2$ $3$ $-4$
Determine the values of:
$\eqalign{ (f^{-1})'(3) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} (f^{-1})'(7) }$
Solution
Given a function, $f(x)$, we can determine the derivative of its inverse according to:
$\eqalign{ (f^{-1})'(x) = \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(x))} }$
so we have:
$\eqalign{ (f^{-1})'(3) &= \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(3))} \\ &= \frac{1}{f'(2)} \\ &= -\frac{1}{4} \\ (f^{-1})'(7) &= \frac{1}{f'(f^{-1}(7))} \\ &= \frac{1}{f'(0)} \\ &= \frac{1}{5} }$
