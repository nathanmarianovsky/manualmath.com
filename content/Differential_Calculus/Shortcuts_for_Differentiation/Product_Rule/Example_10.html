Problem
Given that $f(x)$ and $g(x)$ are two differentiable functions, prove the $\textit{General Leibniz Rule}$:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d^n}{dx^n} (fg) = \sum_{i = 0}^n {n \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n - i)} }$
where:
$\eqalign{ {n \choose i} = \frac{n!}{i!(n - i)!} }$
is known as the $\textit{binomial coefficient}$.
Solution
This is nothing more than an extension to the product rule, one that handles not the first derivative, but rather the $n$th derivative. For a formal proof, consider using induction. For the base case let $n = 1$:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dx} (fg) &= \sum_{i = 0}^1 {1 \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n - 1)} \\ f'g + fg' &= {1 \choose 0} f^{(0)}g^{(1)} + {1 \choose 1}f^{(1)}g^{(0)} \\ f'g + fg' &= fg' + f'g \\ }$
which holds true since we accept the standard product rule. Now we assume that given statement and try to show that it holds true for $n + 1$:
$\eqalign{ \sum_{i = 0}^{n + 1} {n + 1 \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} &= \frac{d^{n + 1}}{dx^{n + 1}} (fg) \\ &= \frac{d^n}{dx^n} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} (fg) \\ &= \frac{d^n}{dx^n} (f'g + fg') \\ &= \frac{d^n}{dx^n} (f^{(1)}g) + \frac{d^n}{dx^n} (fg^{(1)}) \\ &= \sum_{i = 0}^n {n \choose i} f^{(i + 1)}g^{(n - i)} + \sum_{i = 0}^n {n \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^{n + 1} {n \choose i - 1} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} + \sum_{i = 0}^n {n \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} \\ &= {n \choose n} f^{(n + 1)}g^{(0)} + \sum_{i = 1}^{n} {n \choose i - 1} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} + \sum_{i = 1}^n {n \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} + {n \choose 0} f^{(0)}g^{(n + 1)} \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n \Bigg[ {n \choose i} + {n \choose i - 1} \Bigg] f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} + f^{(n + 1)}g^{(0)} + f^{(0)}g^{(n + 1)} \\ &= \sum_{i = 1}^n {n + 1 \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} + {n + 1 \choose n + 1} f^{(n + 1)}g^{(0)} + {n + 1 \choose 0} f^{(0)}g^{(n + 1)} \\ &= \sum_{i = 0}^{n + 1} {n + 1 \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n + 1 - i)} }$
While a formal proof is always important, it just as important to understand how the statement is derived. In this scenario we can derive the $n$th derivative of a product of two functions by recursively using the standard product rule:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d^n}{dx^n} (fg) &= \frac{d^{n - 1}}{dx^{n - 1}} (f^{(1)}g^{(0)} + f^{(0)}g^{(1)}) \\ &= \frac{d^{n - 1}}{dx^{n - 1}} (f^{(1)}g^{(0)}) + \frac{d^{n - 1}}{dx^{n - 1}} (f^{(0)}g^{(1)}) \\ &= \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(2)}g^{(0)} + f^{(1)}g^{(1)}) + \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(1)}g^{(1)} + f^{(0)}g^{(2)}) \\ &= \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(2)}g^{(0)}) + \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(1)}g^{(1)}) + \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(1)}g^{(1)}) + \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(0)}g^{(2)}) \\ &= \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(2)}g^{(0)}) + 2\frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(1)}g^{(1)}) + \frac{d^{n - 2}}{dx^{n - 2}} (f^{(0)}g^{(2)}) \\ &= \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(3)}g^{(0)} + f^{(2)}g^{(1)}) + 2\frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(2)}g^{(1)} + f^{(1)}g^{(2)}) + \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(1)}g^{(2)} + f^{(0)}g^{(3)}) \\ &= \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(3)}g^{(0)}) + 3\frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(2)}g^{(1)}) + 3\frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(1)}g^{(2)}) + \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(0)}g^{(3)}) \\ &= {3 \choose 3} \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(3)}g^{(0)}) + {3 \choose 2} \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(2)}g^{(1)}) + {3 \choose 1} \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(1)}g^{(2)}) + {3 \choose 0} \frac{d^{n - 3}}{dx^{n - 3}} (f^{(0)}g^{(3)}) \\ &\vdots \\ &= {n - 1 \choose n - 1} \frac{d}{dx} (f^{(n - 1)}g^{(0)}) + {n - 1 \choose n - 2} \frac{d}{dx} (f^{(n - 2)}g^{(1)}) + \dots + {n - 1 \choose 1} \frac{d}{dx} (f^{(1)}g^{(n - 2)}) + {n - 1 \choose 0} \frac{d}{dx} (f^{(0)}g^{(n - 1)}) \\ &= {n \choose n} (f^{(n)}g^{(0)}) + {n \choose n - 1} (f^{(n - 1)}g^{(1)}) + \dots + {n \choose 1} (f^{(1)}g^{(n - 1)}) + {n \choose 0} \frac{d}{dx} (f^{(0)}g^{(n)}) \\ &= \sum_{i = 0}^n {n \choose i} f^{(i)}g^{(n - i)} }$
