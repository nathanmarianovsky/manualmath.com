Problem
Determine the absolute maximum and minimum of:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = (x + 1)^{\frac{2}{3}} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [-3,4] }$
Solution
First we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \frac{2}{3(x + 1)^{\frac{1}{3}}} }$
and the critical point:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 3(x + 1)^{\frac{1}{3}} \\ x &= -1 }$
Now we compare the necessary evaluations:
$\eqalign{ f(-3) &= 2^{\frac{2}{3}} \\ f(-1) &= 0 \\ f(4) &= 5^{\frac{2}{3}} }$
This allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{has a absolute maximum of} \hspace{.3cm} 5^{\frac{2}{3}} \\ &\text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = 4 \\ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{has a absolute minimum of} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \\ &\text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = -1 }$
