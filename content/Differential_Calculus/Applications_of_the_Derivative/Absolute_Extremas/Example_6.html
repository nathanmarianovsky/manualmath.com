Problem
A stone is thrown straight up from the roof of an 80-ft building. The height, in feet, of the stone at any time $t$, in seconds, measured from the ground is given by:
$\eqalign{ h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t + 80 }$
What is the maximum height the stone reaches?
Solution
First we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ h'(t) = -32t + 64 }$
and the critical point:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= -32t + 64 \\ t &= 2 }$
Now we compare the necessary evaluations:
$\eqalign{ f(0) &= 80 \\ f(2) &= 144 \\ f(5) &= 0 }$
where the last value corresponds to the time when the stone hits the ground. The above allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ h(t) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{has a absolute maximum of} \hspace{.3cm} 144 \\ &\text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} t = 2 }$
