Problem
Show that the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = xe^x }$
attains the same critical point as:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = \ln(x) + x }$
and that at the critical point $x = c$:
$\eqalign{ f(c) = \lim_{x \rightarrow c} e^{g(x)} }$
Solution
Differentiating both functions gives:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= e^x + xe^x \\ g'(x) &= \frac{1}{x} + 1 }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= e^x + xe^x \\ 0 &= (1 + x)e^x \\ x &= -1 \\ &\vdots \\ 0 &= \frac{1}{x} + 1 \\ -1 &= \frac{1}{x} \\ x &= -1 }$
The critical point is exactly the same and:
$\eqalign{ f(-1) &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -1} e^{g(x)} \\ -e^{-1} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -1} e^{\ln(x) + x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow -1} xe^x \\ &= -e^{-1} }$
