Problem
Determine the absolute maximum and minimum of:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(2x) + \cos(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [0, \pi] }$
Solution
First we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 2\cos(2x) - \sin(x) }$
and the critical points:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 2\cos(2x) - \sin(x) \\ 0 &= 2\Big[ 1 - 2\sin^2(x) \Big] - \sin(x) \\ 0 &= -4\sin^2(x) - \sin(x) + 2 \\ 0 &= 4\sin^2(x) + \sin(x) - 2 \\ \sin(x) &= \frac{-1 + \sqrt{33}}{8} \\ x &= \arcsin\Big( \frac{-1 + \sqrt{33}}{8} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} ,\pi - \arcsin\Big( \frac{-1 + \sqrt{33}}{8} \Big) }$
Now we compare the necessary evaluations:
$\eqalign{ f(0) &= 1 \\ f(0.63487) &\approx 1.76017 \\ f(2.50673) &\approx -1.76017 \\ f(\pi) &= -1 }$
This allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{has a absolute maximum of} \hspace{.3cm} 1.76017 \hspace{.3cm} \text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0.63487 \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{has a absolute minimum of} \hspace{.3cm} -1.76017 \hspace{.3cm} \text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = 2.50673 }$
