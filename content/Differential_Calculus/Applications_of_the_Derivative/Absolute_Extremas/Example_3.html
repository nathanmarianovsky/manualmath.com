Problem
Determine the absolute maximum and minimum of:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2e^x \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [-5,1] }$
Solution
First we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 2xe^x + x^2e^x }$
and the critical points:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 2xe^x + x^2e^x \\ 0 &= (2 + x)xe^x \\ x &= -2, 0 }$
Now we compare the necessary evaluations:
$\eqalign{ f(-5) &= 25e^{-5} \\ f(-2) &= 4e^{-2} \\ f(0) &= 0 \\ f(1) &= e }$
This allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{has a absolute maximum of} \hspace{.3cm} e \hspace{.3cm} \text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = 1 \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{has a absolute minimum of} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \hspace{.3cm} \text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0 }$
