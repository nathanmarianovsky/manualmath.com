Problem
Determine the absolute maximum and minimum of:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \ln(x^2 + 2x + 4) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [-4,3] }$
Solution
First we need the derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \frac{2x + 2}{x^2 + 2x + 4} }$
and the critical point:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 2x + 2 \\ x &= -1 }$
Now we compare the necessary evaluations:
$\eqalign{ f(-4) &= \ln(12) \\ f(-1) &= \ln(3) \\ f(3) &= \ln(19) }$
This allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{has a absolute maximum of} \hspace{.3cm} \ln(19) \hspace{.3cm} \text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = -1 \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{has a absolute minimum of} \hspace{.3cm} \ln(3) \hspace{.3cm} \text{achieved at} \hspace{.3cm} x = 3 }$
