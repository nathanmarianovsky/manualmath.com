Problem
A square sheet of tin $a$ inches on a side is to be used to make an open-top box by cutting a small square of tin from each corner and bending up the sides. How large a square should be cut from each corner in order that the box shall have as large a volume as possible?
Solution
On the square bottom we shall end up with sides of length:
$\eqalign{ l = a - 2x }$
since $x$ is taken away from each side. As for the height it will exactly correspond to:
$\eqalign{ h = x }$
because it represents the width of the piece that gets bend upwards. Now we define the volume as:
$\eqalign{ V &= l^2h \\ &= x(a - 2x)^2 }$
and to determine the extrema we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ V' = (a - 2x)^2 - 4x(a - 2x) }$
Equating to zero will provide us the critical points:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= (a - 2x)^2 - 4x(a - 2x) \\ 0 &= (a - 2x)((a - 2x) - 4x) \\ 0 &= (a - 2x)(a - 6x) \\ x &= \frac{a}{6}, \frac{a}{2} }$
Now using the fact that $x \in \Big[ 0, \frac{a}{2} \Big]$:
$\eqalign{ V\Big( 0 \Big) &= 0 \\ V\Big( \frac{a}{6} \Big) &= \frac{2a}{27} \\ V\Big( \frac{a}{2} \Big) &= 0 }$
With this we can state that in order to form the largest possible volume the optimal choice is $x = \frac{a}{6}$.
