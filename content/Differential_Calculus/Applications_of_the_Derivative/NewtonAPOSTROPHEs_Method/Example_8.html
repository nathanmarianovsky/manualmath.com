Problem
Given the recurrence relation:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_n + \frac{c}{x_n}}{2} }$
Assume that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_n = L }$
and prove that $L = \pm\sqrt{c}$.
Solution
Given the limit we can also state:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n + 1} = L }$
Plugging all of this into the recurrence relation provides:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} &= \frac{x_n + \frac{c}{x_n}}{2} \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n + 1} &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x_n + \frac{c}{x_n}}{2} \\ L &= \frac{1}{2} \Big[ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_n + c\lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} (x_n)^{-1} \Big] \\ 2L &= L + \frac{c}{L} \\ L &= \frac{c}{L} \\ L^2 &= c \\ L &= \pm\sqrt{c} }$
