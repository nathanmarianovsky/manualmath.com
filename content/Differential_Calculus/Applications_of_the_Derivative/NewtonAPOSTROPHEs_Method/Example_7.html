Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - c \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} c \in \mathbb{R} }$
show that the sequence $x_0, x_1, x_2, \dots$ of Newton's Method satisfies the difference equation:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_n + \frac{c}{x_n}}{2} }$
Solution
For the given function Newton's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} &= x_n - \frac{x_n^2 - c}{2x_n} \\ &= x_n - \frac{x_n - \frac{c}{x_n}}{2} \\ &= \frac{x_n - \frac{c}{x_n}}{2} }$
