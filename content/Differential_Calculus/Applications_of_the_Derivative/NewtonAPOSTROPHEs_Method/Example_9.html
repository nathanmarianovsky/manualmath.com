Problem
Use Newton's Method to approximate $\sqrt{2}$ up to $5$ decimal places at $x_5$.
Solution
The number of interest is a root of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - 2 }$
and looking at the graph:
shows that a suitable interval to restrict ourselves to is $[1,2]$. Next up Newton's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{x^2 - 2}{2x} }$
Starting at $x = 1$, the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $1.00000$
$1$ $1.50000$
$2$ $1.41667$
$3$ $1.41422$
$4$ $1.41421$
$5$ $1.41421$
