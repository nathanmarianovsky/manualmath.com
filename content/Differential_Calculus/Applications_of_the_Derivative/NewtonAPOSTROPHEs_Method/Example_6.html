Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ e^{\sin(x)} = -x }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using Newton's Method to determine $x_5$ on the interval $[-1,0]$.
Solution
First we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = e^{\sin(x)} + x }$
and in this scenario Newton's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{e^{\sin(x_n)} + x_n}{\cos(x_n)e^{\sin(x_n)} + 1} }$
We can choose the starting point to be $x = -1$ and the iterations takes on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $-1.00000$
$1$ $-0.53855$
$2$ $-0.57832$
$3$ $-0.57871$
$4$ $-0.57871$
$5$ $-0.57871$
