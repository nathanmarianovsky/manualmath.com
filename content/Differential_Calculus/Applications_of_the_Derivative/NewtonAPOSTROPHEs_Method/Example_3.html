Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(x) - x^2 }$
approximate the critical point up to $5$ decimal places by using Newton's Method to determine $x_5$ on the interval $[0,1]$.
Solution
First we differentiate the equation since we want to determine the critical point:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \cos(x) - 2x }$
and in this scenario Newton's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{\cos(x_n) - 2x_n}{-\sin(x_n) - 2} }$
We can choose the starting point to be $x = 0$ and the iterations takes on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $0.00000$
$1$ $0.50000$
$2$ $0.45063$
$3$ $0.45018$
$4$ $0.45018$
$5$ $0.45018$
