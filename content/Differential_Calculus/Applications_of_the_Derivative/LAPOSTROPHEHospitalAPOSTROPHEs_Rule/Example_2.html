Problem
Evaluate the following using L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2} }$
Solution
The initial limit will approach:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2} = \frac{\infty}{\infty} }$
This provides reasoning to use L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\frac{1}{x}}{2x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{2x^2} \\ &= 0 }$
