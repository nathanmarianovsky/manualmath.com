Problem
Evaluate the following using L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} (\ln(x))^{\frac{1}{x}} }$
Solution
The initial limit will approach:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} (\ln(x))^{\frac{1}{x}} = \infty^0 }$
The result is an indeterminate form, and so using the logarithm:
$\eqalign{ y &= (\ln(x))^{\frac{1}{x}} \\ \ln(y) &= \frac{1}{x}\ln(\ln(x)) \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \ln(y) &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{x}\ln(\ln(x)) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} y \Big) &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{x}\ln(\ln(x)) }$
Now the limit approaches:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln(\ln(x))}{x} = \frac{\infty}{\infty} }$
This provides reasoning to use L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln(\ln(x))}{x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\frac{1}{x}}{\ln(x)} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{x\ln(x)} \\ &= 0 }$
This allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} y \Big) &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{1}{x}\ln(\ln(x)) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} y \Big) &= 0 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} y &= 1 \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} (\ln(x))^{\frac{1}{x}} &= 1 }$
