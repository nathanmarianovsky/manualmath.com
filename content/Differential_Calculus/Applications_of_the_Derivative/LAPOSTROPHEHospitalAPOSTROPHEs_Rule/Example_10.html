Problem
Prove the following:
$\eqalign{ e^x = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big)^n }$
Solution
Using the logarithm we form the following:
$\eqalign{ y &= \Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big)^n \\ \ln(y) &= n\ln\Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big) \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \ln(y) &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} n\ln\Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y \Big) &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} n\ln\Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big) }$
and evaluate the limit on the right side as:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\ln\Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big)}{\frac{1}{n}} &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{-\frac{\frac{x}{n^2}}{1 + \frac{x}{n}}}{-\frac{1}{n^2}} \\ &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x}{1 + \frac{x}{n}} \\ &= x }$
This allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ \ln\Big( \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y \Big) &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} n\ln\Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big) \\ \ln\Big( \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y \Big) &= x \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} y &= e^x \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \Big( 1 + \frac{x}{n} \Big)^n &= e^x }$
