Problem
Evaluate the following using L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x^2 + 4}{8^x} }$
Solution
The initial limit will approach:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x^2 + 4}{8^x} = \frac{\infty}{\infty} }$
This provides reasoning to use L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x^2 + 4}{8^x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{2x}{\ln(8)8^x} \\ &= \frac{\infty}{\infty} }$
Since the result is still an indeterminate form, we use the rule once more:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{2x}{\ln(8)8^x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{2}{(\ln(8))^2 8^x} \\ &= 0 }$
