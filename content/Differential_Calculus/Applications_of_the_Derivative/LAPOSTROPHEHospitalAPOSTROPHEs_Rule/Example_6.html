Problem
Evaluate the following using L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\arctan(3x) - \frac{\pi}{2}}{\arctan(7x) - \frac{\pi}{2}} }$
Solution
The initial limit will approach:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\arctan(3x) - \frac{\pi}{2}}{\arctan(7x) - \frac{\pi}{2}} = \frac{0}{0} }$
This provides reasoning to use L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\arctan(3x) - \frac{\pi}{2}}{\arctan(7x) - \frac{\pi}{2}} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\frac{3}{1 + 9x^2}}{\frac{7}{1 + 49x^2}} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3(1 + 49x^2)}{7(1 + 9x^2)} \\ &= \frac{\infty}{\infty} }$
Since the result is still an indeterminate form, we use the rule once more:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3(1 + 49x^2)}{7(1 + 9x^2)} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3 \cdot 98x}{7 \cdot 18x} \\ &= \frac{7}{3} }$
