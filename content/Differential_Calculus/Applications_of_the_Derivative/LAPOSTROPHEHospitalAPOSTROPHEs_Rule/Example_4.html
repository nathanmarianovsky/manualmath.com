Problem
Evaluate the following using L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} x\tan\Big( \frac{7}{x} \Big) }$
Solution
The initial limit will approach:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\tan\Big( \frac{7}{x} \Big)}{\frac{1}{x}} = \frac{0}{0} }$
This provides reasoning to use L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{\tan\Big( \frac{7}{x} \Big)}{\frac{1}{x}} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{-\frac{7}{x^2}\sec^2\Big( \frac{7}{x} \Big)}{-\frac{1}{x^2}} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} 7\sec^2\Big( \frac{7}{x} \Big) \\ &= 7 }$
