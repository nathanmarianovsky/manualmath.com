Problem
Evaluate the following using L'Hospital's Rule:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x + \sin(x)}{2x} }$
Solution
We first restrict the limit as:
$\eqalign{ \frac{3x - 1}{2x} \leq \frac{3x + \sin(x)}{2x} \leq \frac{3x + 1}{2x} }$
and use L'Hospital's Rule on both sides:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x - 1}{2x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3}{2} \\ &= \frac{3}{2} \\ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x + 1}{2x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3}{2} \\ &= \frac{3}{2} }$
Since both sides approach the same value, by the Squeeze Theorem:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow \infty} \frac{3x + \sin(x)}{2x} = \frac{3}{2} }$
