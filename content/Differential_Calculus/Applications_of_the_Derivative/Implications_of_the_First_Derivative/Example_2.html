Problem
For the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = 2x^3 - 9x^2 - 108x + 2 }$
determine all of the critical points and identify the relative extremas.
Solution
To observe the behavior we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 6x^2 - 18x - 108 }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= x^2 - 3x - 18 \\ &= (x - 6)(x + 3) \\ x &= -3, 6 }$
Now we observe the behavior of the derivative:
Domain $f'(x)$ Behavior
$(-\infty, -3)$ $+$
$(-3, 6)$ $-$
$(6, \infty)$ $+$
With this we can state:
$\eqalign{ x = -3 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative maximum} \\ x = 6 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative minimum} }$
