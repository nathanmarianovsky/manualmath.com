Problem
For the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - 8x + 4 }$
determine the intervals on which the function is increasing or decreasing, and any relative extremas.
Solution
To observe the behavior we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 2x - 8 }$
where the only critical point is:
$\eqalign{ x = 4 }$
Now we observe, for any value to the left of the critical point the derivative is negative. To the right of the critical point the derivative is positive. Therefore:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is decreasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (-\infty, 4) \\ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is increasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (4, \infty) \\ x = 4 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative minimum} }$
