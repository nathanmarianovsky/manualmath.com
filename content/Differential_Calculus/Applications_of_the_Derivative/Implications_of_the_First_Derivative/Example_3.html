Problem
For the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2e^x }$
determine the intervals on which the function is increasing or decreasing, and any relative extremas.
Solution
To observe the behavior we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 2xe^x + x^2e^x }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 2xe^x + x^2e^x \\ &= (x^2 + 2x)e^x \\ &= x(x + 2) \\ x &= -2, 0 }$
Now we observe the behavior of the derivative:
Domain $f'(x)$ Behavior
$(-\infty, -2)$ $+$
$(-2, 0)$ $-$
$(0, \infty)$ $+$
With this we can state:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is decreasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (-2, 0) \\ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is increasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (-\infty, -2) \cup (0, \infty) \\ x = -2 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative maximum} \\ x = 0 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative minimum} }$
