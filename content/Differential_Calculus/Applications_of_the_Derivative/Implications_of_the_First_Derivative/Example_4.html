Problem
For the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = |x^2 - 9| }$
determine the intervals on which the function is increasing or decreasing, and any relative extremas.
Solution
To observe the behavior we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \begin{cases} 2x & x < -3 \\ -2x & -3 < x < 3 \\ 2x & x > 3 \end{cases} }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ x = -3, 0, 3 }$
Now we observe the behavior of the derivative:
Domain $f'(x)$ Behavior
$(-\infty, -3)$ $-$
$(-3, 0)$ $+$
$(0, 3)$ $-$
$(3, \infty)$ $+$
With this we can state:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is decreasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (-\infty, -3) \cup (0, 3) \\ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is increasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (-3, 0) \cup (3, \infty) \\ x = 0 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative maximum} \\ x = -3,3 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{are relative minimums} }$
