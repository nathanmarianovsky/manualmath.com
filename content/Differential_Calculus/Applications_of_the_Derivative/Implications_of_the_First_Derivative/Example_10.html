Problem
Determine two real numbers with difference $\kappa$ and minimum possible product. You can assume $\kappa \in \mathbb{R}_{> 0}$.
Solution
We setup our product function as:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) = xy }$
with the condition that:
$\eqalign{ y = x + \kappa }$
provides:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x(x + \kappa) = x^2 + \kappa x }$
To find a minimum we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 2x + \kappa }$
where the critical point is:
$\eqalign{ x = -\frac{\kappa}{2} }$
Now we observe the behavior of the derivative:
Domain $f'(x)$ Behavior
$\Big( -\infty, -\frac{\kappa}{2} \Big)$ $-$
$\Big( -\frac{\kappa}{2}, \infty \Big)$ $+$
According to the First Derivative Test we can state that our critical point is a relative minimum. Therefore, the two real numbers we want are:
$\eqalign{ x = -\frac{\kappa}{2} \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} y = \frac{\kappa}{2} }$
