Problem
For the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sqrt{4x - x^2} }$
determine the intervals on which the function is increasing or decreasing, and any relative extremas.
Solution
To observe the behavior we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \frac{4 - 2x}{2\sqrt{4x - x^2}} }$
where we restrict ourselves to the open interval $(0,4)$ for the derivative. We do not consider boundaries as we only know behavior of one side and so the only critical point is:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 4 - 2x \\ x &= 2 }$
Now we observe the behavior of the derivative:
Domain $f'(x)$ Behavior
$(0, 2)$ $+$
$(2, 4)$ $-$
With this we can state:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is decreasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (2, 4) \\ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is increasing on} \hspace{.3cm} (0, 2) \\ x = 2 \hspace{.3cm} &\text{is a relative maximum} }$
