Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = -x^3 + 3x^2 - 3x }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= -3x^2 + 6x - 3 \\ 0 &= x^2 - 2x + 1 \\ 0 &= (x - 1)^2 \\ x &= 1 }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) = -6x + 6 &\implies f''(1) = 0 \\ f'''(x) = -6 &\implies f'''(1) = -6 }$
So our case $n = 3$ and $f^{(n)}(0) < 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test the critical point $x = 1$ is a strictly decreasing point of inflection for the original function.
