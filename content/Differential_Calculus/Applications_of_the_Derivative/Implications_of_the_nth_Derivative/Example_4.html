Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = (\ln(x))^3 }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= \frac{3\ln^2(x)}{x} \\ 0 &= \frac{3\ln^2(x)}{x} \\ 0 &= \ln(x) \\ x &= 1 }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) = \frac{3(2 - \ln(x))\ln(x)}{x^2} &\implies f''(1) = 0 \\ f'''(x) = \frac{6(\ln^2(x) - 3\ln(x) + 1)}{x^3} &\implies f'''(1) = 6 }$
So in our case $n = 3$ and $f^{(n)}(0) > 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test the critical point $x = 1$ is a strictly increasing point of inflection for the original function.
