Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^6 + \ln(2) }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 6x^5 \\ 0 &= 6x^5 \\ x &= 0 }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) = 30x^4 &\implies f''(0) = 0 \\ f'''(x) = 120x^3 &\implies f'''(0) = 0 \\ f^{(4)}(x) = 360x^2 &\implies f^{(4)}(0) = 0 \\ f^{(5)}(x) = 720x &\implies f^{(5)}(0) = 0 \\ f^{(6)}(x) = 720x &\implies f^{(6)}(0) = 720 }$
So our case $n = 6$ and $f^{(n)}(0) > 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test the critical point $x = 0$ is a relative minimum for the original function.
