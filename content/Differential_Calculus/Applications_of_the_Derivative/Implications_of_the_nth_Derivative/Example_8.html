Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \frac{x^2 + 1}{e^x} }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= -(x - 1)^2 e^{-x} \\ 0 &= -(x - 1)^2 e^{-x} \\ x &= 1 }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) &= (x^2 - 4x + 3)e^{-x} \\ &\implies f''(1) = 0 \\ f'''(x) &= x(x^2 - 6x + 7)e^{-x} \\ &\implies f'''(1) = 0 \\ f^{(4)}(x) &= (x^2 - 8x + 13)e^{-x} \\ &\implies f^{(4)}(1) = 6e^{-1} }$
So in our case $n = 4$ and $f^{(n)}(0) > 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test the critical point $x = 1$ is a relative minimum for the original function.
