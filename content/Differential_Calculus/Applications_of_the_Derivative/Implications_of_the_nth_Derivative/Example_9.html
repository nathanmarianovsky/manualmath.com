Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^n \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{N} }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= nx^{n - 1} \\ 0 &= nx^{n - 1} \\ x &= 0 }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) &= n(n - 1)x^{n - 2} \\ &\implies f''(0) = 0 \\ f'''(x) &= n(n - 1)(n - 2)x^{n - 3} \\ &\implies f'''(0) = 0 \\ &\vdots \\ f^{(n - 1)}(x) &= n!x \\ &\implies f^{(n - 1)}(0) = 0 \\ f^{(n)}(x) &= n! \\ &\implies f^{(n)}(0) = n! }$
We know that $n!$ is strictly positive so now depending upon the nature of $n$ the behavior changes:
$n$ Implication
Even $x = c$ is a relative minimum of $f$
Odd $x = c$ is a strictly increasing point of inflection of $f$
