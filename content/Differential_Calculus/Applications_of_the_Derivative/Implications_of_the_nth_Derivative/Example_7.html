Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = e^{x^3} }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 3x^2e^{x^3} \\ 0 &= 3x^2e^{x^3} \\ x &= 0 }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) &= 3x(3x^3 + 2)e^{x^3} \\ &\implies f''(0) = 0 \\ f'''(x) &= 3(9x^6 + 18x^3 + 2)e^{x^3} \\ &\implies f'''(0) = 6 }$
So in our case $n = 3$ and $f^{(n)}(0) > 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test the critical point $x = 0$ is a strictly increasing point of inflection for the original function.
