Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \tan^3(x) }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 3\tan^2(x)\sec^2(x) \\ 0 &= 3\tan^2(x)\sec^2(x) \\ 0 &= \tan(x) \\ x &= k\pi \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} k \in \mathbb{Z} }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical points until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) &= 6\tan(x)\sec^4(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 6\tan^3(x)\sec^2(x) \implies f''(k\pi) = 0 \\ f'''(x) &= 6\sec^6(x) + 42\tan^2(x)\sec^4(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 12\tan^4(x)\sec^2(x) \implies f'''(k\pi) = 6 }$
So in our case $n = 3$ and $f^{(n)}(0) > 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test all of the critical points, $x = k\pi$, are strictly increasing points of inflection for the original function.
