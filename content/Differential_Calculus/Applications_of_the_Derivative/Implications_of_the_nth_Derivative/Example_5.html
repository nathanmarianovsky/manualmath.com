Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2\arctan(x) }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 2x\arctan(x) + \frac{x^2}{x^2 + 1} \\ 0 &= x\Big( \frac{x}{x^2 + 1} + 2\arctan(x) \Big) \\ x &= 0 }$
where $x = 0$ is the only solution since the other factor is strictly negative for $x < 0$ and positive for $x > 0$. Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) &= \frac{2(x(x^2 + 2) + (x^2 + 1)^2\arctan(x))}{(x^2 + 1)^2} \\ &\implies f''(0) = 0 \\ f'''(x) &= -\frac{2(x^2 - 3)}{(x^2 + 1)^3} \\ &\implies f'''(0) = 6 }$
So in our case $n = 3$ and $f^{(n)}(0) > 0$. According to the Higher-Order Derivative Test the critical point $x = 0$ is a strictly increasing point of inflection for the original function.
