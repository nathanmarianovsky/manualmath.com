Problem
Determine all critical points of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = g(x)(x - a)^n \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{N} \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} a \in \mathbb{R} }$
given the conditions:
$\eqalign{ g(a) \neq 0 \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} \frac{a - x}{n} \neq \frac{g(x)}{g'(x)} \hspace{.3cm} \forall x }$
and identify each one's behavior according to the Higher-Order Derivative Test.
Solution
To determine the critical points:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= g'(x)(x - a)^n + ng(x)(x - a)^{n - 1} \\ 0 &= g'(x)(x - a)^n + ng(x)(x - a)^{n - 1} \\ 0 &= (x - a)^{n - 1}(g'(x)(x - a) + ng(x)) \\ x &= a }$
Now we keep taking derivatives at the critical point until it evaluates to something other than zero:
$\eqalign{ f''(x) &= g''(x)(x - a)^n + 2ng'(x)(x - a)^{n - 1} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + n(n - 1)g(x)(x - a)^{n - 2} \\ &\implies f''(a) = 0 \\ f'''(x) &= g'''(x)(x - a)^n + 3ng''(x)(x - a)^{n - 1} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3n(n - 1)g'(x)(x - a)^{n - 2} \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + n(n - 1)(n - 2)g(x)(x - a)^{n - 3} \\ &\implies f'''(a) = 0 \\ &\vdots \\ f^{(k)}(x) &= \sum_{i = 0}^k {k \choose i} \frac{n!}{(n - i)!} g^{(k - i)}(x) (x - a)^{n - i} \\ &\implies f^{(k)}(a) = 0 \hspace{.3cm} \text{if} \hspace{.3cm} k \leq n \\ f^{(n)}(x) &= \sum_{i = 0}^n {n \choose i} \frac{n!}{(n - i)!} g^{(n - i)}(x) (x - a)^{n - i} \\ &\implies f^{(n)}(a) = {n \choose n} \frac{n!}{0!} g(a) = n!g(a) }$
Now depending on the specific parameters the result is different:
$n$ Sign of $g(a)$ Implication
Even $-$ $x = c$ is a relative maximum of $f$
Even $+$ $x = c$ is a relative minimum of $f$
Odd $-$ $x = c$ is a strictly decreasing point of inflection of $f$
Odd $+$ $x = c$ is a strictly increasing point of inflection of $f$
