Problem
Determine all values that satisfy Rolle's Theorem for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - 2x \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [0,2] }$
Solution
First we have to determine that all of the conditions are satisfied. We are dealing with a polynomial which is known to be continuous and differentiable. As for the third condition:
$\eqalign{ f(0) &= f(2) \\ (0)^2 - 2(0) &= (2)^2 - 2(2) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
With all of the conditions satisfied by using the setup for Rolle's Theorem:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 0 \\ 2x - 2 &= 0 \\ x &= 1 }$
