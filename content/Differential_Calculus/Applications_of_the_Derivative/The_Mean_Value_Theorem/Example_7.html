Problem
Determine all values that satisfy the Mean Value Theorem for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sqrt{x(1 - x)} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [0,1] }$
Solution
First we have to determine that all of the conditions are satisfied. We are dealing with a power function which is known to be continuous and differentiable. With all of the conditions satisfied by using the setup for the Mean Value Theorem:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \\ \frac{d}{dx} (x - x^2)^{\frac{1}{2}} &= \frac{\sqrt{0} - \sqrt{0}}{1 - 0} \\ \frac{1}{2}(1 - 2x)(x - x^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}} &= 0 \\ 1 - 2x &= 0 \\ x &= \frac{1}{2} }$
