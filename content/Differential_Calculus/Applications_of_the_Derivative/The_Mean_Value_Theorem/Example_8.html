Problem
Use the Mean Value Theorem to prove:
$\eqalign{ |\cos(\kappa b) - \cos(\kappa a)| \leq |\kappa| |b - a| \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} a,b,\kappa \in \mathbb{R} }$
Solution
Let us consider the setup:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \cos(\kappa x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [a,b] }$
We are dealing with a trigonometric function which is known to be continuous and differentiable. With all of the conditions satisfied by using the setup for the Mean Value Theorem:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \\ -\kappa \sin(\kappa x) &= \frac{\cos(\kappa b) - \cos(\kappa a)}{b - a} \\ }$
and using the range of sine:
$\eqalign{ \frac{\cos(\kappa b) - \cos(\kappa a)}{b - a} &= -\kappa \sin(\kappa x) \\ \Bigg| \frac{\cos(\kappa b) - \cos(\kappa a)}{b - a} \Bigg| &= |-\kappa \sin(\kappa x)| \\ \Bigg| \frac{\cos(\kappa b) - \cos(\kappa a)}{b - a} \Bigg| &\leq |\kappa| \\ |\cos(\kappa b) - \cos(\kappa a)| &\leq |\kappa||b - a| }$
