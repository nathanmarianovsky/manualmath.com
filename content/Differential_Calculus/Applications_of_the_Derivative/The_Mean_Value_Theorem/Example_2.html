Problem
Determine all values that satisfy Rolle's Theorem for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = 2\sin(x)\cos(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [0,\pi] }$
Solution
First we have to determine that all of the conditions are satisfied. We are dealing with trigonometric functions which are known to be continuous and differentiable. As for the third condition:
$\eqalign{ f(0) &= f(\pi) \\ 2\sin(0)\cos(0) &= 2\sin(\pi)\cos(\pi) \\ 2(0)(1) &= 2(0)(-1) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
With all of the conditions satisfied by using the setup for Rolle's Theorem:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 0 \\ \frac{d}{dx} (2\sin(x)\cos(x)) &= 0 \\ \frac{d}{dx} (\sin(2x)) &= 0 \\ 2\cos(2x) &= 0 \\ \cos(2x) &= 0 \\ 2x &= \frac{\pi}{2} \\ x &= \frac{\pi}{4} }$
