Problem
Determine all values that satisfy the Mean Value Theorem for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - 5x + 7 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [-1,3] }$
Solution
First we have to determine that all of the conditions are satisfied. We are dealing with a polynomial which is known to be continuous and differentiable. With all of the conditions satisfied by using the setup for the Mean Value Theorem:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \\ 2x - 5 &= \frac{((3)^2 - 5(3) + 7) - ((-1)^2 - 5(-1) + 7)}{(3) - (-1)} \\ 2x - 5 &= \frac{(1) - (13)}{4} \\ 2x - 5 &= -3 \\ 2x &= 2 \\ x &= 1 }$
