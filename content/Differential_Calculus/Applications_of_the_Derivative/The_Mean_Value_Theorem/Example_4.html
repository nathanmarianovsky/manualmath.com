Problem
Determine all values that satisfy the Mean Value Theorem for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = 8x + e^{-3x} \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} x \in [-2,3] }$
Solution
First we have to determine that all of the conditions are satisfied. We are dealing with a polynomial and exponential which are known to be continuous and differentiable. With all of the conditions satisfied by using the setup for the Mean Value Theorem:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= \frac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a} \\ 8 - 3e^{-3x} &= \frac{(8(3) + e^{-9}) - (8(-2) + e^{6})}{(3) - (-2)} \\ 8 - 3e^{-3x} &= \frac{40 + e^{-9} - e^{6}}{5} \\ 3e^{-3x} &= 8 - \frac{40 + e^{-9} - e^{6}}{5} \\ e^{-3x} &= \frac{e^{6} - e^{-9}}{15} \\ -3x &= \ln\Big( \frac{e^{6} - e^{-9}}{15} \Big) \\ x &= -\frac{1}{3}\ln\Big( \frac{e^{6} - e^{-9}}{15} \Big) }$
