Problem
Determine a linear approximation to the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{near} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0 }$
and use it to evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} }$
Solution
To determine a linear approximation we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \cos(x) }$
which evaluated at $x = 0$ produces:
$\eqalign{ f'(0) = 1 }$
Now we can write down the approximation as:
$\eqalign{ f(x) &\approx f(0) + f'(0)(x - 0) \\ &= x }$
So now by direct computation:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{x}{x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} 1 \\ &= 1 }$
