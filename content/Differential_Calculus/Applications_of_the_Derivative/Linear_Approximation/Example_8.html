Problem
Using $\textit{Euler's Formula}$ the following can be deduced:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) = \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i} }$
where $i$ is the imaginary number. Determine a linear approximation for the right side near $x = 0$ to prove that:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) \approx x \hspace{.3cm} \text{near} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0 }$
Solution
To determine a linear approximation we need to focus on the function:
$\eqalign{ g(x) = e^x }$
where the first derivative is:
$\eqalign{ g'(x) = e^x }$
which evaluated at $x = 0$ produces:
$\eqalign{ g'(0) = 1 }$
Now we can write down the approximation as:
$\eqalign{ g(x) &\approx g(0) + g'(0)(x - 0) \\ &= 1 + x }$
Applying this to our scenario gives:
$\eqalign{ \frac{e^{ix} - e^{-ix}}{2i} &\approx \frac{(1 + ix) - (1 - ix)}{2i} \\ &= \frac{2ix}{2i} \\ &= x }$
which states that:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) \approx x \hspace{.3cm} \text{near} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0 }$
