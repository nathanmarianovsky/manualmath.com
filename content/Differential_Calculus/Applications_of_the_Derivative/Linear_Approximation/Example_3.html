Problem
Determine a linear approximation to the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \ln(1 + 3x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{near} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0 }$
and use it to approximate $\ln(0.7)$. What is the error up to $5$ decimal places?
Solution
To determine a linear approximation we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \frac{3}{1 + 3x} }$
which evaluated at $x = 0$ produces:
$\eqalign{ f'(0) = 3 }$
Now we can write down the approximation as:
$\eqalign{ f(x) &\approx f(0) + f'(0)(x - 0) \\ &= 3x }$
So now by direct computation:
$\eqalign{ \ln(0.7) &= -0.35667 \\ \ln(0.7) &= \ln(1 + 3(-0.1)) \\ &\approx 3(-0.10000) \\ &= -0.30000 }$
which gives the error:
$\eqalign{ \Delta &= |f(x) - x| \\ &= |f(-0.1) + 0.1| \\ &= |-0.35667 + 0.30000| \\ &= 0.05667 }$
