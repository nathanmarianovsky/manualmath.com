Derivation
Given a function, $f \in C^{1}$, we have:
$\eqalign{ f'(a) = \lim_{h \rightarrow 0} \frac{f(a + h) - f(a)}{h} }$
Now assuming that $h$ is very small we can approximate the derivative as:
$\eqalign{ f'(a) \approx \frac{f(a + h) - f(a)}{h} }$
and with a little reorganization:
$\eqalign{ f'(a)h &\approx f(a + h) - f(a) \\ f(a + h) &\approx f(a) + f'(a)h }$
we arrive at what is known as the linear approximation for a given function near $a$. By letting $h = x - a$ the above becomes:
$\eqalign{ f(x) &\approx f(a) + f'(a)(x - a) }$
which is equivalent to the first form.
