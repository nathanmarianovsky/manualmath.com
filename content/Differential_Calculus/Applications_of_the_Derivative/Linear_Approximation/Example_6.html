Problem
Determine a linear approximation to the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \cos(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{near} \hspace{.3cm} x = 0 }$
and use it to evaluate:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\cos(x) - 1}{x} }$
Solution
To determine a linear approximation we need the first derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = -\sin(x) }$
which evaluated at $x = 0$ produces:
$\eqalign{ f'(0) = 0 }$
Now we can write down the approximation as:
$\eqalign{ f(x) &\approx f(0) + f'(0)(x - 0) \\ &= 1 }$
So now by direct computation:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{\cos(x) - 1}{x} &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{0}{x} \\ &= \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} 0 \\ &= 0 }$
