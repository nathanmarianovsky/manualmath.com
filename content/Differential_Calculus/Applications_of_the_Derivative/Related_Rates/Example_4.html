Problem
A particle is moving around the ellipse:
$\eqalign{ 4x^2 + 16y^2 = 64 }$
where at any time, $t$, the coordinates are given by:
$\eqalign{ x(t) = 4\cos(t) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} y(t) = 2\sin(t) }$
At what rate is the particles distance to the origin changing at any time? At what rate is the distance changing when $t = \frac{\pi}{4}$?
Solution
We can write away compute the derivatives since they will be needed:
$\eqalign{ x'(t) = -4\sin(t) \hspace{.3cm} \text{and} \hspace{.3cm} y'(t) = 2\cos(t) }$
Now using the Euclidean distance formula and differentiating:
$\eqalign{ (d(t))^2 &= (x(t) - 0)^2 + (y(t) - 0)^2 \\ \frac{d}{dt} (d(t))^2 &= \frac{d}{dt} \Big( (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 \Big) \\ 2dd' &= 2xx' + 2yy' \\ d'(t) &= \frac{xx' + yy'}{d} \\ &= \frac{xx' + yy'}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} \\ &= -\frac{6\cos(t)\sin(t)}{\sqrt{4\cos^2(t) + \sin^2(t)}} }$
The above formula produces the rate at which the distance changes for any time. When looking at $t = \frac{\pi}{4}$ specifically:
$\eqalign{ d'\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big) &= -\frac{6\cos\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big) \sin\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big)}{\sqrt{4\cos^2\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big) + \sin^2\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big)}} \\ &= -\frac{3}{\sqrt{\frac{5}{2}}} \\ &= -3\sqrt{\frac{2}{5}} }$
