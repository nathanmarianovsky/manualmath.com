Problem
A conical paper cup $3 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}$ across the top and $4 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}$ deep is full of water. The cup springs a leak at the bottom and loses water at the rate of $2 \frac{\text{in}^3}{\text{min}}$. How fast is the water level dropping at the instant when the water is exactly $3 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}$ deep?

where the volume of the cone is known to be:
$\eqalign{ V &= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h \\ V(t) &= \frac{\pi}{3} r^2 h(t) }$
To compute we need to determine what $r$ is in terms of $h$. Notice that we can use similar triangles to setup the following:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1.5}{4} &= \frac{h}{r} \\ r &= \frac{3}{8}h }$
Now by differentiation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dt} V(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} \frac{\pi}{3} \Big( \frac{3}{8} h(t) \Big)^2 h(t) \\ \frac{d}{dt} V(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} \frac{3\pi}{64} (h(t))^3 \\ \frac{dV}{dt} &= \frac{9\pi}{64} h^2 \frac{dh}{dt} \\ \frac{dh}{dt} &= \frac{64}{9\pi h^2} \frac{dV}{dt} \\ &= \frac{64}{9\pi (3 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in})^2} \cdot \Big( -2 \frac{\text{in}^3}{\text{min}} \Big) \\ &= -\frac{128}{81\pi} \frac{\text{in}}{\text{min}} }$
