Problem
Ship $A$ is $15 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}$ east of $P$ and is moving west at $20 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph}$; ship $B$ is $60 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}$ south of $P$ and is moving north at $15 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph}$. At what rate is the distance between them changing after $1$ hour?
Solution
The situation can be modeled with the following image:
Using the Pythagorean Theorem and differentiation:
$\eqalign{ (d(t))^2 &= (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 \\ \frac{d}{dt} (d(t))^2 &= \frac{d}{dt} \Big[ (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 \Big] \\ 2dd' &= 2xx' + 2yy' \\ d' &= \frac{xx' + yy'}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} }$
After one hour we have the following:
$\eqalign{ d' &= \frac{xx' + yy'}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} \\ &= \frac{(-5)(-20) + (-45)(15)}{\sqrt{(-5)^2 + (-45)^2}} \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph} \\ &= -\frac{115}{\sqrt{82}} \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph} }$
