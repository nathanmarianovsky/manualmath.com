Problem
A balloon is $200 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft}$ off the ground and rising vertically at the constant rate of $15 \frac{\text{ft}}{\text{sec}}$. An automobile passes beneath it traveling along a straight road at the constant rate of $66 \frac{\text{ft}}{\text{sec}}$. How fast is the distance between them changing one second later?
Solution
The situation can be modeled with the following image:
Using the Pythagorean Theorem and differentiation:
$\eqalign{ (d(t))^2 &= (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 \\ \frac{d}{dt} (d(t))^2 &= \frac{d}{dt} \Big[ (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 \Big] \\ 2dd' &= 2xx' + 2yy' \\ d' &= \frac{xx' + yy'}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} }$
After one sec we have the following:
$\eqalign{ d' &= \frac{xx' + yy'}{\sqrt{x^2 + y^2}} \\ &= \frac{(66 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})(66 \frac{\text{ft}}{\text{sec}}) + (215 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})(15 \frac{\text{ft}}{\text{sec}})}{\sqrt{(66 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})^2 + (215 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})^2}} \\ &= -\frac{7581}{\sqrt{50581}} \frac{\text{ft}}{\text{sec}} }$
