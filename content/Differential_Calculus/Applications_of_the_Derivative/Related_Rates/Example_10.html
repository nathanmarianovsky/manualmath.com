Problem
Two ships $A$ and $B$ are sailing away from the point $O$ along routes such that the angle $AOB = 120^\circ$. How fast is the distance between them changing if, at a certain instant $|OA| = 8 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}$, $|OB| = 6 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}$, ship $A$ is sailing at the rate of $20 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph}$, and ship $B$ at the rate of $30 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph}$?
Solution
The situation can be modeled with the following image:
Using the Law of Cosines and differentiation:
$\eqalign{ (d(t))^2 &= (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 - 2x(t)y(t)\cos(\theta) \\ (d(t))^2 &= (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 - 2x(t)y(t)\cos(120^\circ) \\ \frac{d}{dt} (d(t))^2 &= \frac{d}{dt} \Big[ (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 + x(t)y(t) \Big] \\ 2dd' &= 2xx' + 2yy' + x'y + xy' \\ d' &= \frac{(2x + y)x' + (2y + x)y'}{2\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + xy}} }$
At the one instant:
$\eqalign{ d' &= \frac{(2x + y)x' + (2y + x)y'}{2\sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + xy}} \\ &= \frac{(2(6 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}) + (8 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}))(30 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph}) + (2(8 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}) + (6 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}))((20 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph}))}{2\sqrt{(6 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi})^2 + (8 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi})^2 + (6 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi})(8 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi})}} \\ &= \frac{260}{\sqrt{37}} \hspace{.1cm} \text{mph} }$
