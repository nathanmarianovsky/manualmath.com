Problem
Two parallel sides of a rectangle are being lengthened at the rate of $2 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}}$, while the other two sides are shortened in such a way that the figure remains a rectangle with constant area of $50 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}^2$. What is the rate of change of the perimeter when the length of an increasing side is $5 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}$?
Solution
For a rectangle with the given parameters the width is:
$\eqalign{ A &= lw \\ w &= \frac{A}{l} \\ &= \frac{50 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}^2}{5 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in}} \\ &= 10 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in} }$
and by differentiation the width is changing at the rate:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dt} A(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} l(t)w(t) \\ \frac{dA}{dt} &= \frac{dl}{dt}w + l\frac{dw}{dt} \\ 0 &= \frac{dl}{dt}w + l\frac{dw}{dt} \\ \frac{dw}{dt} &= -\frac{w}{l} \frac{dl}{dt} \\ &= -\frac{10}{5} \cdot \Big( 2 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} \Big) \\ &= -4 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} }$
With this we can determine the rate of change for the perimeter:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dt} P(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} \Big( 2l(t) + 2w(t) \Big) \\ \frac{dP}{dt} &= 2\frac{dl}{dt} + 2\frac{dw}{dt} \\ &= 2\Big( 2 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} ) + 2\Big( -4 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} \Big) \\ &= -4 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} }$
