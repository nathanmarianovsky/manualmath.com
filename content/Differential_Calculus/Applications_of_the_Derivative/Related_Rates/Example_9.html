Problem
Suppose that a raindrop is a perfect sphere. Assume that, through condensation, the raindrop accumulates moisture at a rate proportional to its surface area. Show that the radius increases at a constant rate.
Solution
Using the setup for the surface area of a sphere and differentiating:
$\eqalign{ A(t) &= 4\pi (r(t))^2 \\ \frac{d}{dt} A(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} 4\pi (r(t))^2 \\ \frac{dA}{dt} &= 8\pi r \frac{dr}{dt} }$
Let $\kappa$ represent the rate for the raindrop accumulation and using the proportionality:
$\eqalign{ \kappa &\sim \frac{dA}{dt} \\ \kappa &\sim 8\pi r \frac{dr}{dt} \\ \frac{dr}{dt} &\sim \frac{\kappa}{8\pi r} }$
Therefore, for a constant radius, the radius increases at a constant rate.
