Problem
Oil spilled from a ruptured tanker spreads out in a circle whose area increases at a constant rate of $6 \frac{\text{mi}^2}{\text{hr}}$. How fast is the radius of the spill increasing when the area is $9 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}^2$?
Solution
For a circle with a given area we can write the radius as:
$\eqalign{ A &= \pi r^2 \\ r &= \sqrt{\frac{A}{\pi}} }$
and using differentiation on the area formula:
$\eqalign{ A(t) &= \pi (r(t))^2 \\ \frac{d}{dt} A(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} \pi (r(t))^2 \\ \frac{dA}{dt} &= 2\pi r \frac{dr}{dt} \\ \frac{dr}{dt} &= \frac{1}{2\pi r} \frac{dA}{dt} \\ &= \frac{1}{2\pi \sqrt{\frac{9 \hspace{.1cm} \text{mi}^2}{\pi}}} \cdot \Big( 6 \frac{\text{mi}^2}{\text{hr}} \Big) \\ &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{\pi}} \frac{\text{mi}}{\text{hr}} }$
