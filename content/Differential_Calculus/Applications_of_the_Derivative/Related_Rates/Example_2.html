Problem
A $25$ foot ladder is leaning against a vertical wall. The floor is slightly slippery and the foot of the ladder slips away from the wall at the rate of $0.2 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}}$. How fast is the top of the ladder sliding down the wall when the top is $20$ feet above the floor?

where by the Pythagorean Theorem we have the setup:
$\eqalign{ (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 &= (25 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})^2 \\ \frac{d}{dt} \Big[ (x(t))^2 + (y(t))^2 \Big] &= \frac{d}{dt} (25 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})^2 \\ 2x\frac{dx}{dt} + 2y\frac{dy}{dt} &= 0 \\ \frac{dy}{dt} &= -\frac{x}{y} \frac{dx}{dt} }$
and evaluation with the given parameters provides:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dt} &= -\frac{x}{y} \frac{dx}{dt} \\ &= -\frac{\sqrt{(25 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})^2 - (20 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft})^2}}{20 \hspace{.1cm} \text{ft}} \cdot \Big( 0.2 \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} \Big) \\ &= -\frac{3}{20} \frac{\text{in}}{\text{sec}} }$
