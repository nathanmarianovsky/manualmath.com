Problem
Air is being pumped into a spherical balloon at a rate of $20 \frac{\text{in}^3}{\text{min}}$. How fast is the radius of the balloon increasing when the radius is $6$ in?
Solution
For a spherical balloon it is known that:
$\eqalign{ V &= \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \\ V(t) &= \frac{4}{3}\pi (r(t))^3 }$
which is rewritten as a function of time since it is the dependent variable in the situation. Now by differentiation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d}{dt} V(t) &= \frac{d}{dt} \Big( \frac{4}{3}\pi (r(t))^3 \Big) \\ \frac{dV}{dt} &= 4\pi r^2 \frac{dr}{dt} \\ \frac{dr}{dt} &= \frac{1}{4\pi r^2} \frac{dV}{dt} }$
and evaluation with the given parameters provides:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dr}{dt} &= \frac{1}{4\pi r^2} \frac{dV}{dt} \\ &= \frac{1}{4\pi (6 \hspace{.1cm} \text{in})^2} \cdot \Big( 20 \frac{\text{in}^3}{\text{min}} \Big) \\ &= \frac{5}{36\pi} \frac{\text{in}}{\text{min}} }$
