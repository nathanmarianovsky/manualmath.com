Problem
There are $50$ apple trees in an orchard. Each tree produces $800$ apples and for each additional tree planted in the orchard, the output per tree drops by $10$ apples. How many trees should be added to the existing orchard in order to maximize the total output of trees?
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = (50 + x)(800 - 10x) }$
where $x$ is the number of additional trees. Now to identify the optimal number of trees we take the derivative:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = (800 - 10x) - 10(50 + x) }$
and determine the critical point:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 300 - 20x \\ x &= 15 }$
Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ f(0) &= 40,000 \\ f(15) &= 42,250 \\ f(\infty) &= -\infty }$
With this we can state the maximum possible output achieved is $42,250$ $\text{apples}$ given $15$ additional trees.
