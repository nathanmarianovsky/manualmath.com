Problem
A farmer has $2400$ $\text{ft}$ of fencing and wants to fence off a rectangular field that borders a straight river. He needs no fence along the river. What are the dimensions of the field that has the largest area?
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) = xy }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ 2y + x = 2400 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq y \leq 1200 }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(y) &= f(2400 - 2y, y) \\ &= (2400 - 2y)y \\ &= 2400y - 2y^2 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(y) &= 2400 - 4y }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 2400 - 4y \\ 0 &= 600 - y \\ y &= 600 }$
Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ k(0) &= 0 \\ k(600) &= 720,000 \\ k(1200) &= 0 }$
With this we can state the maximum possible area achieved is $720,000$ $\text{ft}^2$ given a height of $600$ $\text{ft}$ and length of $1200$ $\text{ft}$.
