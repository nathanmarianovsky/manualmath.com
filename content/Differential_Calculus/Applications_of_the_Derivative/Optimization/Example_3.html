Problem
A container in the shape of a right circular cylinder with no top has surface area $3\pi$ $\text{ft}^2$. What height, $h$, and base radius, $r$, will maximize the volume of the cylinder?
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(r,h) = \pi r^2h }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ 2\pi rh + \pi r^2 = 3\pi \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq r \leq \sqrt{3} }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(r) &= f\Big( r, \frac{3\pi - \pi r^2}{2\pi r} \Big) \\ &= \frac{3\pi}{2}r - \frac{\pi}{2}r^3 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(r) &= \frac{3\pi}{2} - \frac{3\pi}{2}r^2 }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ \require{cancel} 0 &= \frac{3\pi}{2} - \frac{3\pi}{2}r^2 \\ 0 &= 1 - r^2 \\ r &= \cancel{-1}, 1 }$
and the negative value is ignored due to restrictions on the problem. Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ k(0) &= 0 \\ k(1) &= \pi \\ k(\sqrt{3}) &= 0 }$
With this we can state the maximum possible volume achieved is $\pi$ $\text{ft}^3$ given a height of $1$ $\text{ft}$ and base radius of $1$ $\text{ft}$.
