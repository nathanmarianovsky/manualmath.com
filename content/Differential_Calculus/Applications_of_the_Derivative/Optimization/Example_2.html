Problem
An open rectangular box with square base is to be made from $48$ $\text{ft}^2$ of material. What dimensions will result in a box with the largest possible volume?
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) = x^2y }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ x^2 + 4xy = 48 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq x \leq \sqrt{48} }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(x) &= f\Big( x, \frac{48 - x^2}{4x} \Big) \\ &= 12x - \frac{1}{4}x^3 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(x) &= 12 - \frac{3}{4}x^2 }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ \require{cancel} 0 &= 12 - \frac{3}{4}x^2 \\ 0 &= 16 - x^2 \\ x &= \cancel{-4}, 4 }$
and the negative value is ignored due to restrictions on the problem. Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ k(0) &= 0 \\ k(4) &= 32 \\ k(\sqrt{48}) &= 0 }$
With this we can state the maximum possible volume achieved is $32$ $\text{ft}^3$ given a height of $2$ $\text{ft}$ and square side length of $4$ $\text{ft}$.
