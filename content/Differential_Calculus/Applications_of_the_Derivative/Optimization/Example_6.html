Problem
Determine the area of the largest rectangle that can be inscribed in a circle of radius $4$.
Solution
Let each variable represent the length in a particular direction in reference to the origin. Therefore, we identify the objective function as:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) = 4xy }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ x^2 + y^2 = 16 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} -4 \leq x,y \leq 4 }$
Now the setup we have is good to go, but there is a better way to reduce stress. If we want to maximize the area, we may as well maximize the area squared. So first define the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x^2 \\ v &= y^2 }$
and use it to rewrite the objective and constraint functions:
$\eqalign{ (f(u,v))^2 &= 16uv \\ u + v &= 16 }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(u) &= (f(u, 16 - u))^2 \\ &= 16u(16 - u) \\ &= 256u - 16u^2 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(u) &= 256 - 32u }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 256 - 32u \\ u &= 8 }$
Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ k(0) &= 0 \\ k(8) &= 1024 \\ k(16) &= 0 }$
With this we can state the maximum possible area achieved is $1024$ given height and length $\sqrt{8}$, which turns out to be a square.
