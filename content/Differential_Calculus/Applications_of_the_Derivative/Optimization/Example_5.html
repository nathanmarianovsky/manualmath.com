Problem
A manufacturer needs to make a cylindrical can that will hold $1500$ $\text{cm}^3$ of liquid. Determine the dimensions of the can that will minimize the amount of material used in its construction.
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(r,h) = 2\pi rh + 2\pi r^2 }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ \pi r^2h = 1500 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq r \leq \infty }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(r) &= f\Big( r, \frac{1500}{\pi r^2} \Big) \\ &= \frac{3000}{r} + 2\pi r^2 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(r) &= -\frac{3000}{r^2} + 4\pi r }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ \require{cancel} 0 &= -\frac{3000}{r^2} + 4\pi r \\ 0 &= -3000 + 4\pi r^3 \\ r &= \Big( \frac{750}{\pi} \Big)^{\frac{1}{3}} }$
Notice that to the left of the point the derivative is negative and positive afterwards. Therefore, by the First Derivative Test we can state this is a relative minimum. Since the boundaries are not included in this case, this is the only minimum, thus it is also the absolute minimum. With this we can state the minimum amount of material that can be achieved is $\approx 725.3964$ $\text{cm}^2$ given a height of $\approx 12.4070$ $\text{cm}$ and base radius of $\approx 6.2035$ $\text{cm}$.
