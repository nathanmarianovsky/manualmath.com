Problem
Construct a window in the shape of a semi-circle over a rectangle. If the distance around the outside of the window is $12$ $\text{ft}$, what dimensions will result in the rectangle having largest possible area?
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) = xy }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ x + 2y + \frac{\pi}{2}x = 12 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq x \leq \frac{24}{2 + \pi} }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(x) &= f\Big( x, 6 - \frac{1}{2}x - \frac{\pi}{4}x \Big) \\ &= 6x - \frac{1}{2}x^2 - \frac{\pi}{4}x^2 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(x) &= 6 - x - \frac{\pi}{2}x }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ \require{cancel} 0 &= 6 - x - \frac{\pi}{2}x \\ 6 &= x\Big( 1 + \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) \\ x &= \frac{6}{1 + \frac{\pi}{2}} }$
and the negative value is ignored due to restrictions on the problem. Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ k\Big( 0 \Big) &= 0 \\ k\Big( \frac{6}{1 + \frac{\pi}{2}} \Big) &= \frac{32}{2 + \pi} \\ k\Big( \frac{24}{2 + \pi} \Big) &= 0 }$
With this we can state the maximum possible area for the rectangle achieved is $\approx 7.0017$ $\text{ft}^2$ given a height of $\approx 3$ $\text{ft}$ and length of $\approx 2.3339$ $\text{ft}$.
