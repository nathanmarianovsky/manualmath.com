Problem
A cylindrical can is to hold $20\pi$ $\text{m}^3$ of liquid. The material for the top and bottom costs $\frac{\$ 10}{\text{m}^2}$ and $\frac{\$ 8}{\text{m}^2}$ for the side. Find the radius, $r$, and height, $h$ of the most economical can.
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(r,h) = 20\pi r^2 + 16\pi rh }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ \pi r^2h = 20\pi \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq r \leq \infty }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(r) &= f\Big( r, \frac{28}{r^2} \Big) \\ &= 20\pi r^2 + \frac{320\pi}{r} }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(r) &= 40\pi r - \frac{320\pi}{r^2} }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 40\pi r - \frac{320\pi}{r^2} \\ 0 &= 40r^3 - 320 \\ r &= 2 }$
Notice that to the left of the point the derivative is negative and positive afterwards. Therefore, by the First Derivative Test we can state this is a relative minimum and since the boundaries are not included in this case, this is the only minimum, thus it is also the absolute minimum. With this we can state the minimum cost that can be achieved is $\$240\pi$ given a height of $7$ $\text{m}$ and base radius of $2$ $\text{m}$.
