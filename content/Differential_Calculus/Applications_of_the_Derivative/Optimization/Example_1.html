Problem
Find two non-negative numbers whose sum is $9$ and so that the product of one number and the square of the other number is a maximum.
Solution
First identify the objective function:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) = xy^2 }$
and the constraint function:
$\eqalign{ x + y = 9 \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} 0 \leq x,y \leq 9 }$
Now the objective function can be rewritten as:
$\eqalign{ k(y) &= f(9 - y, y) \\ &= (9 - y)y^2 }$
Differentiation gives:
$\eqalign{ k'(y) &= -y^2 + 2y(9 - y) }$
where the critical points are:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= -y^2 + 2y(9 - y) \\ 0 &= y(-y + 2(9 - y)) \\ 0 &= y(18 - 3y) \\ y &= 0, 6 }$
Now the necessary evaluations are:
$\eqalign{ k(0) &= 0 \\ k(6) &= 108 \\ k(9) &= 0 }$
With this we can state the maximum product achieved is $108$ given the two numbers $3$ amd $6$ where $6$ is the number being squared.
