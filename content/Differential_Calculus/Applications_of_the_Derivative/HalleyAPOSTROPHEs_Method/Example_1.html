Problem
Using Halley's Method determine $x_5$, up to $5$ decimal places, as an approximation of the root to the equation:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^3 - x + 1 }$
on the interval $[-2,-1]$.
Solution
In this scenario Halley's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{2(x_n^3 - x_n + 1)(3x_n^2 - 1)}{2(3x_n^2 - 1)^2 - (x_n^3 - x_n + 1)(6x_n)} }$
We can choose the starting point to be $x = -1$ and the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $-1.00000$
$1$ $-1.28571$
$2$ $-1.32468$
$3$ $-1.32472$
$4$ $-1.32472$
$5$ $-1.32472$
