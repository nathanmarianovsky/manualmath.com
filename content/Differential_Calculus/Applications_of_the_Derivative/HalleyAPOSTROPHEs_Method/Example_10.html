Problem
Use Halley's Method to approximate $\sqrt{11}$ up to $5$ decimal places at $x_5$.
Solution
The number of interest is a root of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - 11 }$
and looking at the graph:
shows that a suitable interval to restrict ourselves to is $[3,4]$. Next up Halley's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_n^3 + 33x_n}{3x_n^2 + 11} }$
Starting at $x = 3$, the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $1.00000$
$1$ $3.31579$
$2$ $3.31662$
$3$ $3.31662$
$4$ $3.31662$
$5$ $3.31662$
