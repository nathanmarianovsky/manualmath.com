Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) = x^2 }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using Halley's Method to determine $x_5$ on the interval $\Big[ \frac{1}{2}, 1 \Big]$.
Solution
First we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = \sin(x) - x^2 }$
and in this scenario Halley's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{2(\cos(x_n) - 2x_n)}{\frac{2(\cos(x_n) - 2x_n)^2}{\sin(x_n) - x_n^2} + \sin(x_n) + 2} }$
We can choose the starting point to be $x = 1$ and the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $1.00000$
$1$ $0.87856$
$2$ $0.87673$
$3$ $0.87673$
$4$ $0.87673$
$5$ $0.87673$
