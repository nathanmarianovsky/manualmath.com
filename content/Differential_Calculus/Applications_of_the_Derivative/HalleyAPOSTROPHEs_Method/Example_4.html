Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ 6x = \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using Halley's Method to determine $x_5$.
Solution
Before anything else we need to determine a suitable interval to search. This can be accomplished by graphing the left and right hand sides independently:
Looking at the graph shows that $[0,1]$ is a proper interval. Now we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = 6x - \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
and in this scenario Halley's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{2(6x_n - \sqrt{1 - x_n^2})\Big( 6 + \frac{2x_n}{\sqrt{1 - x_n^2}} \Big)}{2\Big( 6 + \frac{2x_n}{\sqrt{1 - x_n^2}} \Big)^2 + (6x_n - \sqrt{1 - x_n^2})\Bigg( \frac{2x_n^2 + 2}{(1 - x_n^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}} \Bigg)} }$
We can choose the starting point to be $x = 0$ and the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $0.00000$
$1$ $0.17143$
$2$ $0.16460$
$3$ $0.16440$
$4$ $0.16440$
$5$ $0.16440$
