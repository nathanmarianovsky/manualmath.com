Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - c \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} c \in \mathbb{R} }$
show that the sequence $x_0, x_1, x_2, \dots$ of Halley's Method satisfies the difference equation:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_n^3 + 3cx_n}{3x_n^2 + c} }$
Solution
For the given function Halley's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} &= x_n - \frac{2(x_n^2 - c)(2x_n)}{2(2x_n)^2 - (x_n^2 - c)(2)} \\ &= x_n - \frac{2x_n^3 - 2cx_n}{3x_n^2 + c} \\ &= \frac{x_n^3 + 3cx_n}{3x_n^2 + c} }$
