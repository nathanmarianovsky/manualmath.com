Problem
Given the recurrence relation:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_n^3 + 3cx_n}{3x_n^2 + c} }$
Assume that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_n = L }$
and prove that $L = 0, \pm\sqrt{c}$.
Solution
Given the limit we can also state:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n + 1} = L }$
Plugging all of this into the recurrence relation provides:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} &= \frac{x_n^3 + 3cx_n}{3x_n^2 + c} \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n + 1} &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x_n^3 + 3cx_n}{3x_n^2 + c} \\ L &= \frac{L^3 + 3cL}{3L^2 + c} \\ 3L^3 + cL &= L^3 + 3cL \\ 0 &= 2L^3 - 2cL \\ 0 &= 2L(L^2 - c) \\ L &= 0, \pm \sqrt{c} }$
