Problem
Describe the concavity of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = 6x + \sin(3x) }$
and classify the critical points according to the Second Derivative Test on the interval $\Big( 0, \frac{\pi}{2} \Big)$.
Solution
To get everything we need, we have to determine both the first and second derivatives:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 6 + 3\cos(3x) \\ f''(x) &= -9\sin(3x) }$
Now to determine the critical points we equate the first derivative to zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 6 + 3\cos(3x) \\ 0 &= 3 + \cos(3x) }$
So we arrive at stating that there are no critical points. To observe the concavity behavior we need to determine where the second derivative is zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= -9\sin(3x) \\ 0 &= \sin(3x) \\ 3x &= \kappa \pi \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \kappa \in \mathbb{Z} \\ x &= \frac{\kappa \pi}{3} }$
which allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave downwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( 0, \frac{\pi}{3} \Big) \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave wpwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( \frac{\pi}{3}, \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) }$
