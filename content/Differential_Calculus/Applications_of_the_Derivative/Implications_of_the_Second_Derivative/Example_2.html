Problem
Describe the concavity of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(2x) }$
and classify the critical points according to the Second Derivative Test on the interval $(0, \pi)$.
Solution
To get everything we need, we have to determine both the first and second derivatives:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 2\cos(2x) \\ f''(x) &= -4\sin(2x) }$
Now to determine the critical points we equate the first derivative to zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 2\cos(2x) \\ 2x &= \frac{\pi}{2} + \kappa \pi \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \kappa \in \mathbb{Z} \\ x &= \frac{\pi(1 + 2\kappa)}{4} }$
and to classify the points we plug the only ones in the region into the second derivative:
$\eqalign{ f''\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big) &= -4 \\ f''\Big( \frac{3\pi}{4} \Big) &= 4 }$
So we arrive at stating that:
$\eqalign{ x &= \frac{\pi}{4} \hspace{.3cm} \text{is a relative maximum} \\ x &= \frac{3\pi}{4} \hspace{.3cm} \text{is a relative minimum} }$
To observe the concavity behavior we need to determine where the second derivative is zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= -4\sin(2x) \\ 2x &= \alpha \pi \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} \alpha \in \mathbb{Z} \\ x &= \frac{\alpha \pi}{2} }$
which allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave downwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( 0, \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave upwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( \frac{\pi}{2}, \pi \Big) }$
