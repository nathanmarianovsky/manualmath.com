Problem
Describe the concavity of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \ln(x) - x }$
and classify the critical points according to the Second Derivative Test.
Solution
To get everything we need, we have to determine both the first and second derivatives:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= \frac{1}{x} - 1 \\ f''(x) &= -\frac{1}{x^2} }$
Now to determine the critical points we equate the first derivative to zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= \frac{1}{x} - 1 \\ 1 &= \frac{1}{x} \\ x &= 1 }$
and to classify the points we plug them into the second derivative:
$\eqalign{ f''(0) &= \hspace{.1cm} \text{DNE} \\ f''(1) &= -1 }$
So we arrive at stating that:
$\eqalign{ x = 1 \hspace{.3cm} \text{is a relative maximum} }$
To observe the concavity behavior we need to determine where the second derivative is zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 = -\frac{1}{x^2} }$
which allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x) \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave negative on} \hspace{.3cm} ( 0, \infty ) }$
