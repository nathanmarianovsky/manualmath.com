Problem
Describe the concavity of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^4 - 2x^2 + 3 }$
and classify the critical points according to the Second Derivative Test.
Solution
To get everything we need, we have to determine both the first and second derivatives:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) &= 4x^3 - 4x \\ f''(x) &= 12x^2 - 4 }$
Now to determine the critical points we equate the first derivative to zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 4x^3 - 4x \\ 0 &= 4x(x^2 - 1) \\ 0 &= 4x(x - 1)(x + 1) }$
and to classify the points we plug them into the second derivative:
$\eqalign{ f''(-1) &= 8 \\ f''(0) &= -4 \\ f''(1) &= 8 }$
So we arrive at stating that:
$\eqalign{ x &= -1 \hspace{.3cm} \text{is a relative minimum} \\ x &= 0 \hspace{.3cm} \text{is a relative maximum} \\ x &= 1 \hspace{.3cm} \text{is a relative minimum} }$
To observe the concavity behavior we need to determine where the second derivative is zero:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 12x^2 - 4 \\ 1 &= 3x^2 \\ x &= \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} }$
which allows us to state:
$\eqalign{ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave upwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( -\infty, -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \Big) \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave downwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \Big) \\ f(x)& \hspace{.3cm} \text{is concave upwards on} \hspace{.3cm} \Big( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}, \infty \Big) }$
