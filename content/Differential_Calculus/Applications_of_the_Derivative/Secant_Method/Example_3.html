Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sin(x) - x^2 }$
approximate the critical point up to $5$ decimal places by using the Secant Method to determine $x_6$ on the interval $[0,1]$. Let $x_0$ and $x_1$ represent the two initial points.
Solution
First we differentiate the equation since we want to determine the critical point:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = \cos(x) - 2x }$
and in this scenario the Secant Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ &x_{n + 1} \\ &= x_n - \Big[ (\cos(x_n) - 2x_n)(x_n - x_{n - 1}) \Big] \\ & \hspace{.2cm} \cdot \Big[ \cos(x_n) - \cos(x_{n - 1}) - 2x_n + 2x_{n - 1} \Big]^{-1} }$
We can choose the starting point to be $x = 0$ and the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $0.00000$
$1$ $1.00000$
$2$ $0.40655$
$3$ $0.44651$
$4$ $0.45021$
$5$ $0.45018$
