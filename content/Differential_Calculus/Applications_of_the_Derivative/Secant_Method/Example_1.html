Problem
Using the Secant Method determine $x_6$, up to $5$ decimal places, as an approximation of the root to the equation:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^3 - x + 1 }$
on the interval $[-2,-1]$ and let $x_0$ and $x_1$ represent the two initial points.
Solution
In this scenario the Secant Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{(x_n^3 - x_n + 1)(x_n - x_{n - 1})}{(x_n^3 - x_{n - 1}^3) - (x_n - x_{n - 1})} }$
We can choose the starting points to be $x_0 = -2$ and $x_1 = -1$. Now the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $-2.00000$
$1$ $-1.00000$
$2$ $-1.16667$
$3$ $-1.39560$
$4$ $-1.31366$
$5$ $-1.32402$
$6$ $-1.32473$
