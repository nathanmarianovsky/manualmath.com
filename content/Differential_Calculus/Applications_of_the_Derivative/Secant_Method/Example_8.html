Problem
Given the recurrence relation:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_nx_{n - 1} + c}{x_n + x_{n - 1}} }$
Assume that:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_n = L }$
and prove that $L = \pm\sqrt{c}$.
Solution
Given the limit we can also state:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n + 1} = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n - 1} = L }$
Plugging all of this into the recurrence relation provides:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} &= \frac{x_nx_{n - 1} + c}{x_n + x_{n - 1}} \\ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} x_{n + 1} &= \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} \frac{x_nx_{n - 1} + c}{x_n + x_{n - 1}} \\ L &= \Big[ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} (x_nx_{n - 1} + c) \Big] \\ & \hspace{.2cm} \cdot \Big[ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} (x_n + x_{n - 1}) \Big]^{-1} \\ L &= \frac{L^2 + c}{2L} \\ 2L^2 &= L^2 + c \\ L^2 &= c \\ L &= \pm \sqrt{c} }$
