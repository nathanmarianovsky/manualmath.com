Problem
Use the Secant Method to approximate $\sqrt{2}$ up to $5$ decimal places at $x_6$. Let $x_0$ and $x_1$ represent the two initial points.
Solution
The number of interest is a root of the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - 2 }$
and looking at the graph:
shows that a suitable interval to restrict ourselves to is $[1,2]$. Next up the Secant Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{(x_n^2 - 2)(x_n - x_{n - 1})}{x_n^2 - x_{n - 1}^2} }$
Starting at $x_0 = 1$ and $x_1 = 2$, the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $1.00000$
$1$ $2.00000$
$2$ $1.33333$
$3$ $1.40000$
$4$ $1.41463$
$5$ $1.41421$
$6$ $1.41421$
