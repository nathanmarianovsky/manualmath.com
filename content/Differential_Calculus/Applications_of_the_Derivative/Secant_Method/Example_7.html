Problem
Given the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = x^2 - c \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} c \in \mathbb{R} }$
show that the sequence $x_0, x_1, x_2, \dots$ of the Secant Method satisfies the difference equation:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = \frac{x_nx_{n - 1} + c}{x_n + x_{n - 1}} }$
Solution
For the given function the Secant Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} &= x_n - \frac{(x_n^2 - c)(x_n - x_{n - 1})}{x_n^2 - x_{n - 1}^2} \\ &= x_n - \frac{x_n^2 + c}{x_n + x_{n - 1}} \\ &= \frac{x_nx_{n - 1} + c}{x_n + x_{n - 1}} }$
