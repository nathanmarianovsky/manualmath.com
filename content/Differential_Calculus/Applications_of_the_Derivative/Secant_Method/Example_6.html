Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ e^{\sin(x)} = -x }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Secant Method to determine $x_6$ on the interval $[-1,0]$. Let $x_0$ and $x_1$ represent the two initial points.
Solution
First we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = e^{\sin(x)} + x }$
and in this scenario the Secant Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ &x_{n + 1} \\ &= x_n - \Big[ (e^{\sin(x_n)} + x_n)(x_n - x_{n - 1}) \Big] \\ & \hspace{.2cm} \cdot \Big[ (e^{\sin(x_n)} - e^{\sin(x_{n - 2})}) + (x_n - x_{n - 1}) \Big]^{-1} }$
We can choose the starting points to be $x_0 = -1$ and $x_1 = 0$. Now the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $-1.00000$
$1$ $0.00000$
$2$ $-0.63738$
$3$ $-0.58698$
$4$ $-0.57860$
$5$ $-0.57871$
$6$ $-0.57871$
