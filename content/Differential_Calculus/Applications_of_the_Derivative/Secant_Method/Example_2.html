Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) = x^2 }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Secant Method to determine $x_6$ on the interval $\Big[ \frac{1}{2}, 1 \Big]$. Let $x_0$ and $x_1$ represent the two initial points.
Solution
First we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = \sin(x) - x^2 }$
and in this scenario the Secant Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ &x_{n + 1} \\ &= x_n - \Big[ (\sin(x_n) - x_n^2)(x_n - x_{n - 1}) \Big] \\ & \hspace{.2cm} \cdot \Big[ \sin(x_n) - \sin(x_{n - 1}) - x_n^2 + x_{n - 1}^2 \Big]^{-1} }$
We can choose the starting points to be $x_0 = \frac{1}{2}$ and $x_1 = 1$. Now the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $0.50000$
$1$ $1.00000$
$2$ $0.79569$
$3$ $0.86491$
$4$ $0.87806$
$5$ $0.87671$
$6$ $0.87673$
