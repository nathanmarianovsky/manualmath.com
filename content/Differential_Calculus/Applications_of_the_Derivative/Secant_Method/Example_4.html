Problem
Given the equation:
$\eqalign{ 6x = \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
approximate the solution up to $5$ decimal places by using the Secant Method to determine $x_6$. Let $x_0$ and $x_1$ represent the two initial points.
Solution
Before anything else we need to determine a suitable interval to search. This can be accomplished by graphing the left and right hand sides independently:
Looking at the graph shows that $[0,1]$ is a proper interval. Now we rewrite the equation as:
$\eqalign{ 0 = 6x - \sqrt{1 - x^2} }$
and in this scenario Newton's Method takes the form:
$\eqalign{ x_{n + 1} = x_n - \frac{(6x_n - \sqrt{1 - x_n^2})(x_n - x_{n - 1})}{(6x_n - 6x_{n - 1}) - (\sqrt{1 - x_n^2} - \sqrt{1 - x_{n - 1}^2})} }$
We can choose the starting points to be $x_0 = 0$ and $x_1 = 1$. Now the iterations take on the values:
$\textbf{n}$ $\textbf{x}_n$
$0$ $0.00000$
$1$ $1.00000$
$2$ $0.14286$
$3$ $0.16139$
$4$ $0.16440$
$5$ $0.16440$
$6$ $0.16440$
