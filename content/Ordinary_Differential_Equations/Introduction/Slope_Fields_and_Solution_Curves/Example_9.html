Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \sin(x) }$
find the general solution by determining the antiderivative and sketch some of the solution curves.
Solution
In order to find the solution:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= \int \frac{dy}{dx} dx \\ &= \int \sin(x) dx \\ &= -\cos(x) + C }$
With this the solution curves take on the form:
which is a family of cosine curves with different vertical shifts.
