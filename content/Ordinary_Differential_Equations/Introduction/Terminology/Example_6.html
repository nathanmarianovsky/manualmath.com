Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \sqrt{1 - y^2} }$
find the solution by determining the antiderivative and determine its interval of existance.
Solution
Essentially we need to use an integral to work backwards:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= \sqrt{1 - y^2} \\ \frac{dx}{dy} &= \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - y^2}} \\ \int \Big[ \frac{dx}{dy} \Big] dy &= \int \Big[ \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - y^2}} \Big] dy \\ \int dx &= \int \frac{dy}{\sqrt{1 - y^2}} \\ x &= \int \frac{\cos(\theta)}{\sqrt{1 - \sin^2(\theta)}} d\theta \\ x &= \int \frac{\cos(\theta)}{\sqrt{\cos^2(\theta)}} d\theta \\ x &= \int \frac{\cos(\theta)}{|\cos(\theta)|} d\theta \\ x &= \int d\theta \\ x &= \theta + C \\ x &= \arcsin(y) + C \\ 0 &= \arcsin(y) - x + C }$
where the absolute value can ignored because:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= \arcsin(y) - x + C \\ \frac{d}{dx} (0) &= \frac{d}{dx} (\arcsin(y) - x + C) \\ 0 &= \frac{y'}{\sqrt{1 - y^2}} - 1 \\ y' &= \sqrt{1 - y^2} }$
On the other hand if we assumed that $cos(\theta) < 0$, then the solution would take on the form and give rise to the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= -\arcsin(y) - x + C \\ \frac{d}{dx} (0) &= \frac{d}{dx} (-\arcsin(y) - x + C) \\ 0 &= -\frac{y'}{\sqrt{1 - y^2}} - 1 \\ y' &= -\sqrt{1 - y^2} }$
which is not the one we want. With this we have an implicit solution, but the explicit solution can also be determined:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= \arcsin(y) - x + C \\ \arcsin(y) &= x - C \\ y &= \sin(x - C) \\ y &= \sin(x + K) }$
where:
$\eqalign{ K = -C }$
gets rid of the unneccesary negative sign. To look at the interval of existance, it is important to first take into account the domain of the differential equation. For the differential equation to exist, we must have it such that:
$\eqalign{ 1 - y^2 &\geq 0 \\ 1 &\geq y^2 \\ 1 &\geq \sqrt{y^2} \\ 1 &\geq |y| \\ -1 \leq &y \leq 1 }$
which if you notice, also satisfies the implicit solution's domain since $\arcsin$ is only defined for $y \in [-1,1]$. As for $x$, it can be any real number since it does not give any issues in either the derivative or the general solution.
