Problem
The graphs of members of the one-parameter family:
$\eqalign{ x^3 + y^3 = 3Cxy \hspace{.5cm} \text{where} \hspace{.5cm} C \in \mathbb{R} }$
are called Folia of Descartes. Verify that this family is an implicit solution of the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y(y^3 - 2x^3)}{x(2y^3 - x^3)} }$
Solution
The easiest way to verify is to just use implicit differentiation and solve for the derivative:
$\eqalign{ x^3 + y^3 &= 3Cxy \\ \frac{d}{dx} (x^3 + y^3) &= 3C \frac{d}{dx} (xy) \\ 3x^2 + 3y^2y' &= 3C(y + xy') \\ x^2 - Cy &= Cxy' - y^2y' \\ x^2 - Cy &= (Cx - y^2)y' \\ y' &= \frac{x^2 - Cy}{Cx - y^2} \\ y' &= \frac{x^2 - y \cdot \frac{x^3 + y^3}{3xy}}{x \cdot \frac{x^3 + y^3}{3xy} - y^2} \\ y' &= \frac{x^2 - \frac{x^2}{3} - \frac{y^3}{3x}}{\frac{x^3}{3y} + \frac{y^2}{3} - y^2} \\ y' &= \frac{\frac{2x^2}{3} - \frac{y^3}{3x}}{\frac{x^3}{3y} - \frac{2y^2}{3}} \\ y' &= \frac{2x^2 - \frac{y^3}{x}}{\frac{x^3}{y} - 2y^2} \\ y' &= \frac{y(2x^3 - y^3)}{x(x^3 - 2y^3)} \\ y' &= \frac{y(y^3 - 2x^3)}{x(2y^3 - x^3)} \\ }$
Notice that in order to arrive at the differential equation, I substituted the definition of $C$ in terms of the given solution. This has to be done in order to make sense since the parameter $C$ comes out of the integration and should only be a part of the general solution, not the differential equation.
