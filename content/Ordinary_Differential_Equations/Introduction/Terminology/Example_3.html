Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x}{y} }$
show that the curve:
$\eqalign{ x^2 + y^2 = 25 }$
is an implicit solution.
Solution
Using implicit differentiation:
$\eqalign{ x^2 + y^2 &= 25 \\ \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ x^2 + y^2 \Big] &= \frac{d}{dx} \Big[ 25 \Big] \\ 2x + 2y\cdot\frac{dy}{dx} &= 0 \\ 2y\cdot\frac{dy}{dx} &= -2x \\ \frac{dy}{dx} &= -\frac{x}{y} }$
