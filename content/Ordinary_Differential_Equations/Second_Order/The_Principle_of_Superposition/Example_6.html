Problem
Given the set of functions:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{S} = \Big\{ e^{\frac{x}{2}}, xe^{\frac{x}{2}} \Big\} }$
Determine whether $\mathcal{S}$ satisfies as a fundamental set for the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ 4y'' - 4y' + y = 0 }$
Solution
First we have to check for the independence of the functions by looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1, y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} e^{\frac{x}{2}} & xe^{\frac{x}{2}} \\ \frac{1}{2}e^{\frac{x}{2}} & e^{\frac{x}{2}} + \frac{x}{2}e^{\frac{x}{2}} \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \Big( e^x + \frac{x}{2}e^x \Big) - \Big( \frac{x}{2}e^x \Big) \\ &= e^x }$
With their independence assured, we form the homogeneous solution:
$\eqalign{ y_h = c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} }$
and check that it satisfies the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= 4y_h'' - 4y_h' + y_h \\ 0 &= 4\Big( c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} \Big)'' - 4\Big( c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} \Big)' + \Big( c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} \Big) \\ 0 &= c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} + 4c_2e^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} - 2c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} - 4c_2e^{\frac{x}{2}} - 2c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_1e^{\frac{x}{2}} + c_2xe^{\frac{x}{2}} \\ 0 &= 0 }$
