Problem
Suppose that:
$\eqalign{ y_1 &= e^x \\ y_2 &= e^{-x} }$
are two solutions of a homogeneous linear second order differential equation. Explain why:
$\eqalign{ y_3 &= \cosh(x) \\ y_4 &= \sinh(x) }$
are also solutions of the equation.
Solution
Since we are dealing with a homogeneous linear equation, the general solution is given by the homogeneous solution:
$\eqalign{ y_h = c_1e^x + c_2e^{-x} }$
In comparison:
$\eqalign{ \cosh(x) &= \frac{e^x + e^{-x}}{2} \\ \sinh(x) &= \frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{2} }$
So letting $c_1 = c_2 = \frac{1}{2}$ gives the hyperbolic cosine and $c_1 = -c_2 = \frac{1}{2}$ gives the hyperbolic sine.
