Problem
Given that there exist non-homogeneous solutions to the differential equations:
$\eqalign{ j_1'' + k_1j_1' + k_2j_1 &= f_1(x) \\ j_2'' + k_1j_2' + k_2j_2 &= f_2(x) \\ j_3'' + k_1j_3' + k_2j_3 &= f_3(x) \\ & \vdots \\ j_n'' + k_1j_n' + k_2j_n &= f_n(x) \\ }$
where:
$\eqalign{ k_1, k_2 \in \mathbb{R} }$
Use this information to determine the non-homogeneous solution to:
$\eqalign{ w'' + k_1w' + k_2w = a_1f_1(x) + a_2f_2(x) + \dots + a_nf_n(x) }$
where:
$\eqalign{ a_1,a_2,a_3,\dots,a_n \in \mathbb{R} }$
Solution
A good idea might be to try a sum of multiples of the original non-homogeneous solutions:
$\eqalign{ w_{nh} = a_1j_1 + a_2j_2 + a_3j_3 + \dots + a_nj_n = \sum_{l = 1}^n a_lj_l }$
and plugging in gives:
$\eqalign{ \sum_{i = 1}^n a_if_i(x) &= w_{nh}'' + k_1w_{nh}' + k_2w_{nh} \\ \sum_{i = 1}^n a_if_i(x) &= \sum_{l = 1}^n a_lj_l'' + k_1\sum_{l = 1}^n a_lj_l' + k_2\sum_{l = 1}^n a_lj_l \\ \sum_{i = 1}^n a_if_i(x) &= \sum_{l = 1}^n a_l(j_l'' + k_1j_l' + k_2j_l) \\ \sum_{i = 1}^n a_if_i(x) &= \sum_{l = 1}^n a_lf_l(x) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
