Problem
Given the differential equations:
$\eqalign{ y'' - 6y' + 5y &= -9e^{2x} \\ w'' - 6w' + 5w &= 5x^2 + 3x - 16 }$
where the general solutions are:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + 3e^{2x} \\ w(x) &= c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + x^2 + 3x }$
First verify that these solutions indeed satisfy the differential equations and use this information to determine the solution to:
$\eqalign{ z'' - 6z' + 5z &= -9e^{2x} + 5x^2 + 3x - 16 }$
Solution
To verify the first solution:
$\eqalign{ -9e^{2x} &= y'' - 6y' + 5y \\ -9e^{2x} &= \frac{d^2}{dx^2}(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + 3e^{2x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 6\frac{d}{dx}(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + 3e^{2x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 5(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + 3e^{2x}) \\ -9e^{2x} &= (c_1e^x + 25c_2e^{5x} + 12e^{2x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 6(c_1e^x + 5c_2e^{5x} + 6e^{2x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 5(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + 3e^{2x}) \\ -9e^{2x} &= -9e^{2x} \\ 0 &= 0 }$
Now for the second solution:
$\eqalign{ 5x^2 + 3x - 16 &= w'' - 6w' + 5w \\ 5x^2 + 3x - 16 &= \frac{d^2}{dx^2}(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + x^2 + 3x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 6\frac{d}{dx}(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + x^2 + 3x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 5(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + x^2 + 3x) \\ 5x^2 + 3x - 16 &= (c_1e^x + 25c_2e^{5x} + 2) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 6(c_1e^x + 5c_2e^{5x} + 2x + 3) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 5(c_1e^x + c_2e^{5x} + x^2 + 3x) \\ 5x^2 + 3x - 16 &= 5x^2 + 3x - 16 \\ 0 &= 0 }$
Lastly to solve the new differential equation, using the principle of superposition of non-homogeneous differential equations:
