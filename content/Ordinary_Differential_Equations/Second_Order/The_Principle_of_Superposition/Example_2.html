Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) = 2y'' + 8y' + 6y }$
and two independent solutions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = e^{-3x} \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = e^{-x} }$
to the homogeneous differential equation and a non-homogeneous solution:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \frac{\sin(x)}{10} - \frac{\cos(x)}{5} }$
determine the general solution and show that it satisfies the differential equation.
Solution
Since this is a non-homogeneous linear second order differential equation, the general solution will be given by the addition of the homogeneous and non-homogeneous solutions:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = y_h(x) + y_{nh}(x) = c_1e^{-3x} + c_2e^{-x} + \frac{\sin(x)}{10} - \frac{\cos(x)}{5} }$
To confirm this:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) &= y'' + 4y' + 3y \\ \sin(x) &= \frac{d^2}{dx^2}(y_h + y_{nh}) + 4\frac{d}{dx}(y_h + y_{nh}) + 3(y_h + y_{nh}) \\ \sin(x) &= \Bigg( 9c_1e^{-3x} + c_2e^{-x} -\frac{\sin(x)}{10} + \frac{\cos(x)}{5} \Bigg) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 4\Bigg( -3c_1e^{-3x} - c_2e^{-x} + \frac{\cos(x)}{10} + \frac{\sin(x)}{5} \Bigg) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 3\Bigg( c_1e^{-3x} + c_2e^{-x} + \frac{\sin(x)}{10} - \frac{\cos(x)}{5} \Bigg) \\ \sin(x) &= \sin(x) }$
