Problem
Given the non-homogeneous solution:
$\eqalign{ y_{nh} = 4x^2 + 2 }$
that satisfies the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' + k_1y' + k_2y = f(x) }$
where:
$\eqalign{ k_1, k_2 \in \mathbb{R} }$
Use this information to determine the non-homogeneous solution to:
$\eqalign{ w'' + k_1w' + k_2w = 2f(x) }$
Solution
A good idea might be to try double the given non-homogeneous solution:
$\eqalign{ w_{nh} = 2y_{nh} = 8x^2 + 4 }$
and plugging in gives:
$\eqalign{ 2f(x) &= w_{nh}'' + k_1w_{nh}' + k_2w_{nh} \\ 2f(x) &= (2y_{nh})'' + k_1(2y_{nh})' + k_2(2y_{nh}) \\ 2f(x) &= 2(y_{nh}'' + k_1y_{nh}' + k_2y_{nh}) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
