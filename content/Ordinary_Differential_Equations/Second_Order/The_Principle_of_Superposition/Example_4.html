Problem
Given the differential equations:
$\eqalign{ y'' + 7y' + 12y &= x^2 \\ w'' + 7w' + 12w &= e^{7x} \\ r'' + 7r' + 12r &= \cos(2x) \\ }$
where the general solutions are:
$\eqalign{ y(x) &= c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{x^2}{12} - \frac{7x}{72} + \frac{37}{864} \\ w(x) &= c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{e^{7x}}{110} \\ r(x) &= c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{7\sin(2x)}{130} + \frac{2\cos(2x)}{65} \\ }$
First verify that these solutions indeed satisfy the differential equations and use this information to determine the solution to:
$\eqalign{ z'' + 7z' + 12z &= x^2 + e^{7x} + \cos(2x) }$
Solution
To verify the first solution:
$\eqalign{ x^2 &= y'' + 7y' + 12y \\ x^2 &= \Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{x^2}{12} - \frac{7x}{72} + \frac{37}{864} \Big)'' \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 7\Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{x^2}{12} - \frac{7x}{72} + \frac{37}{864} \Big)' \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 12\Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{x^2}{12} - \frac{7x}{72} + \frac{37}{864} \Big) \\ x^2 &= \Big( 16c_1e^{-4x} + 9c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{1}{6} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Big( -28c_1e^{-4x} - 21c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{7x}{6} - \frac{49}{72} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Big( 12c_1e^{-4x} + 12c_2e^{-3x} + x^2 - \frac{7x}{6} + \frac{37}{72} \Big) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
Now for the second solution:
$\eqalign{ e^{7x} &= y'' + 7y' + 12y \\ e^{7x} &= \Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{e^{7x}}{110} \Big)'' \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 7\Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{e^{7x}}{110} \Big)' \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 12\Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{e^{7x}}{110} \Big) \\ e^{7x} &= \Big( 16c_1e^{-4x} + 9c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{49e^{7x}}{110} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Big( -28c_1e^{-4x} - 21c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{49e^{7x}}{110} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Big( 12c_1e^{-4x} + 12c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{12e^{7x}}{110} \Big) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
and the third solution:
$\eqalign{ \cos(2x) &= y'' + 7y' + 12y \\ \cos(2x) &= \Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{7\sin(2x)}{130} + \frac{2\cos(2x)}{65} \Big)'' \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 7\Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{7\sin(2x)}{130} + \frac{2\cos(2x)}{65} \Big)' \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 12\Big( c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{7\sin(2x)}{130} + \frac{2\cos(2x)}{65} \Big) \\ \cos(2x) &= \Big( 16c_1e^{-4x} + 9c_2e^{-3x} - \frac{28\sin(2x)}{130} - \frac{8\cos(2x)}{65} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Big( -28c_1e^{-4x} - 21c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{98\cos(2x)}{130} - \frac{28\sin(2x)}{65} \Big) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Big( 12c_1e^{-4x} + 12c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{84\sin(2x)}{130} + \frac{24\cos(2x)}{65} \Big) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
Lastly to solve the new differential equation, using the principle of superposition of non-homogeneous differential equations:
$\eqalign{ z(x) = c_1e^{-4x} + c_2e^{-3x} + \frac{x^2}{12} - \frac{7x}{72} + \frac{37}{864} + \frac{e^{7x}}{110} + \frac{7\sin(2x)}{130} + \frac{2\cos(2x)}{65} }$
