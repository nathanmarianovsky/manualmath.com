Problem
Given the set of functions:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{S} = \{ \cosh(2x), \sinh(2x) \} }$
Determine whether $\mathcal{S}$ satisfies as a fundamental set for the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' - 4y = 0 }$
Solution
First we have to check for the independence of the functions by looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1, y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} \cosh(2x) & \sinh(2x) \\ 2\sinh(2x) & 2\cosh(2x) \end{pmatrix} \\ &= 2\cosh^2(2x) - 2\sinh^2(2x) \\ &= 2 }$
With their independence assured, we form the homogeneous solution:
$\eqalign{ y_h = c_1\cosh(2x) + c_2\sinh(2x) }$
and check that it satisfies the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y_h'' - 4y_h \\ 0 &= (c_1\cosh(2x) + c_2\sinh(2x))'' - 4(c_1\cosh(2x) + c_2\sinh(2x)) \\ 0 &= (4c_1\cosh(2x) + 4c_2\sinh(2x)) - (4c_1\cosh(2x) + 4c_2\sinh(2x)) \\ 0 &= 0 }$
