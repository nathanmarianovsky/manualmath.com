Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' - xy = 0 }$
determine a series solution centered at $x_0 = 0$.
Solution
For a series solutiok we form:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty a_n x^n }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' - xy \\ &= \sum_{n = 2}^\infty n(n - 1)a_n x^{n - 2} - x\sum_{n = 0}^\infty a_n x^n \\ &= \sum_{n = 2}^\infty n(n - 1)a_n x^{n - 2} - \sum_{n = 0}^\infty a_n x^{n + 1} \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty (n + 2)(n + 1)a_{n + 2}x^n - \sum_{n = 1}^\infty a_{n - 1}x^n \\ &= 2a_2 + \sum_{n = 1}^\infty (n + 2)(n + 1)a_{n + 2}x^n - \sum_{n = 1}^\infty a_{n - 1}x^n \\ &= 2a_2 + \sum_{n = 0}^\infty \Big[ (n + 2)(n + 1)a_{n + 2} - a_{n - 1} \Big]x^n }$
to arrive at:
$\eqalign{ a_2 &= 0 \\ a_{n + 2} &= \frac{a_{n - 1}}{(k + 2)(k + 1)} }$
where the coefficients take on the values:
$k$ $a_{3k}$ $a_{3k + 1}$ $a_{3k + 2}$
$1$ $\eqalign{ a_{3} = \frac{a_0}{(3)(2)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{4} = \frac{a_1}{(4)(3)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{5} = \frac{a_2}{(5)(4)} = 0 }$
$2$ $\eqalign{ a_{6} = \frac{a_3}{(6)(5)} = \frac{a_0}{(6)(5)(3)(2)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{7} = \frac{a_4}{(7)(6)} = \frac{a_1}{(7)(6)(4)(3)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{8} = \frac{a_5}{(8)(7)} = 0 }$
$3$ $\eqalign{ a_{9} = \frac{a_6}{(9)(8)} = \frac{a_0}{(9)(8)(6)(5)(3)(2)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{10} = \frac{a_7}{(10)(9)} = \frac{a_1}{(10)(9)(7)(6)(4)(3)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{11} = \frac{a_8}{(11)(10)} = 0 }$
$\vdots$ $\vdots$ $\vdots$ $\vdots$
$n$ $\eqalign{ a_{3n} = \frac{a_0}{(3n)(3n - 1)\dots(6)(5)(3)(2)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{3n + 1} = \frac{a_1}{(3n + 1)(3n)\dots(7)(6)(4)(3)} }$ $\eqalign{ a_{3n + 2} = 0 }$
The only missing pieces are the first two coefficients, $c_0$ and $c_1$, which can only be determined when given an initial condition. Therefore, the final solution takes on the form:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = a_0 \Bigg[ 1 + \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{x^{3n}}{(3n)(3n - 1)\dots(6)(5)(3)(2)} \Bigg] + a_1 \Bigg[ x + \sum_{n = 1}^\infty \frac{x^{3n + 1}}{(3n + 1)(3n)\dots(7)(6)(4)(3)} \Bigg] }$
