Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' - xy = 0 }$
determine a series solution centered at $x_0 = 0$.
Solution
For a series solution we form:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = \sum_{n = 0}^\infty a_n x^n }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' - xy \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty n(n - 1)a_n x^{n - 2} - x\sum_{n = 0}^\infty a_n x^n \\ &= \sum_{n = 0}^\infty n(n - 1)a_n x^{n - 2} - \sum_{n = 0}^\infty a_n x^{n + 1} \\ &= \sum_{n = 2}^\infty a_n x^n - \Big[ \sum_{n = 1}^\infty a_n x^{n + 1} + a_0x \Big] \\ &= \sum_{n = 2}^\infty a_n x^n - \Big[ \sum_{n = 2}^\infty a_{n - 1} x^n + a_0x \Big] \\ &= \sum_{n = 2}^\infty (a_n - a_{n - 1}) x^n - a_0x \\ }$
to get two real distinct values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{3x} }$
Now for the non-homogeneous solution, using the method of undetermined coefficients we will guess that the solution takes on the form:
$\eqalign{ y_{nh}(x) = Ae^{2x} }$
Plugging into the differential equation gives:
$\eqalign{ e^{2x} &= y_{nh}'' - 2y_{nh}' - 3y_{nh} \\ e^{2x} &= 4Ae^{2x} - 4Ae^{2x} - 3Ae^{2x} \\ e^{2x} &= -3Ae^{2x} \\ A &= -\frac{1}{3} }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{3x} - \frac{1}{3}e^{2x} }$
