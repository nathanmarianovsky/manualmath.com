Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' - 3y' - 10y = -4\cos(x) + 7\sin(x) }$
determine the general solution.
Solution
Since this is a non-homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the addition of the homogeneous and non-homogeneous solutions. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in to solve the homogeneous portion:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' - 3y' - 10y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} - 3me^{mx} - 10e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 - 3m - 10 \\ 0 &= (m + 2)(m - 5) \\ m &= -2, 5 }$
to get two real distinct values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1e^{-2x} + c_2e^{5x} }$
Now for the non-homogeneous solution, using the method of undetermined coefficients we will guess that the solution takes on the form:
$\eqalign{ y_{nh}(x) = A\cos(x) + B\sin(x) }$
Plugging into the differential equation gives:
$\eqalign{ -4\cos(x) + 7\sin(x) &= y_{nh}'' - 3y_{nh}' - 10y_{nh} \\ -4\cos(x) + 7\sin(x) &= (-A\cos(x) - B\sin(x)) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 3(-A\sin(x) + B\cos(x)) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 10(A\cos(x) + B\sin(x)) \\ -4\cos(x) + 7\sin(x) &= (-11A - 3B)\cos(x) + (3A - 11B)\sin(x) \\ }$
This can be rewritten in matrix form and using the inverse a solution can be obtained:
$\eqalign{ \begin{pmatrix} -11 & -3 \\ 3 & -11 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} A \\ B \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} -4 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix} \\ \begin{pmatrix} A \\ B \end{pmatrix} &= \frac{1}{130} \begin{pmatrix} -11 & 3 \\ -3 & -11 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} -4 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{2} \\ -\frac{1}{2} \end{pmatrix} }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1e^{-2x} + c_2e^{5x} + \frac{1}{2}\cos(x) - \frac{1}{2}\sin(x) }$
