Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' + 25y = 25x^4 - 25 + e^x }$
determine the general solution.
Solution
Since this is a non-homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the addition of the homogeneous and non-homogeneous solutions. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in to solve the homogeneous portion:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' + 25y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} + 25e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 + 25 \\ 0 &= (m - 5i)(m + 5i) \\ m &= \pm 5i }$
to get two real distinct values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1\cos(5x) + c_2\sin(5x) }$
Now for the non-homogeneous solution, using the method of undetermined coefficients we will guess that the solution takes on the form:
$\eqalign{ y_{nh}(x) = Ax^4 + Bx^3 + Cx^2 + Dx + E + Fe^x }$
Plugging into the differential equation gives:
$\eqalign{ 25x^4 - 25 + e^x &= y_{nh}'' + 25y_{nh} \\ 25x^4 - 25 + e^x &= (12Ax^2 + 6Bx + 2C + Fe^x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + 25(Ax^4 + Bx^3 + Cx^2 + Dx + E + Fe^x) \\ 25x^4 - 25 + e^x &= (25A)x^4 + (25B)x^3 + (12A + 25C)x^2 \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + (6B + 25D)x + (2C + 25E) + (26F)e^x \\ A &= 1 \\ B &= 0 \\ C &= -\frac{12}{25} \\ D &= 0 \\ E &= -\frac{601}{625} \\ F &= \frac{1}{26} }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1\cos(5x) + c_2\sin(5x) + x^4 - \frac{12}{25}x^2 - \frac{601}{625} + \frac{1}{26}e^x }$
