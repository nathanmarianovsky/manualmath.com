Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' + 9y = 39xe^{2x} }$
determine the general solution.
Solution
Since this is a non-homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the addition of the homogeneous and non-homogeneous solutions. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in to solve the homogeneous portion:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' + 9y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} + 9e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 + 9 \\ 0 &= (m - 3i)(m + 3i) \\ m &= \pm 3i }$
to get two distinct complex values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1\cos(3x) + c_2\sin(3x) }$
Now for the non-homogeneous solution, using the method of undetermined coefficients we will guess that the solution takes on the form:
$\eqalign{ y_{nh}(x) = (Ax + B)e^{2x} }$
Plugging into the differential equation gives:
$\eqalign{ 39xe^{2x} &= y_{nh}'' + 9y_{nh} \\ 39xe^{2x} &= 4(Ax + A + B)e^{2x} + 9(Ax + B)e^{2x} \\ 39xe^{2x} &= 13Axe^{2x} + (4A + 13B)e^{2x} \\ A &= 3 \\ B &= -\frac{12}{13} }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1\cos(3x) + c_2\sin(3x) + \Big( 3x - \frac{12}{13} \Big)e^{2x} }$
