Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' = 6xe^x\sin(x) }$
where:
$\eqalign{ y(0) = 0 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y'(0) = 0 }$
determine the particular solution.
Solution
Since this is a non-homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the addition of the homogeneous and non-homogeneous solutions. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in to solve the homogeneous portion:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 \\ m &= 0 }$
to get one real value for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1 + c_2x }$
Now for the non-homogeneous solution, using the method of undetermined coefficients we will guess that the solution takes on the form:
$\eqalign{ y_{nh}(x) = ((Ax + B)\sin(x) + (Cx + D)\cos(x))e^x }$
Plugging into the differential equation gives:
$\eqalign{ 6xe^x\sin(x) &= y_{nh}'' \\ 6xe^x\sin(x) &= (-2C)xe^x\sin(x) + (2A)xe^x\cos(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + (2A - 2C - 2D)e^x\sin(x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + (2A + 2B + 2C)e^x\cos(x) \\ C &= -3 \\ A &= 0 \\ D &= 3 \\ B &= 3 }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1 + c_2x + (3\sin(x) + 3(1 - x)\cos(x))e^x }$
where the first derivative is:
$\eqalign{ y'(x) = c_2 + 3e^x(x\sin(x) + (1 - x)\cos(x)) }$
Now using the initial conditions:
$\eqalign{ y(0) &= c_1 + c_2(0) + (3\sin(0) + 3(1 - (0))\cos(0))e^0 = 0 \\ y'(0) &= c_2 + 3e^0(x\sin(0) + (1 - (0))\cos(0)) = 0 }$
From the equations:
$\eqalign{ c_1 &= -3 \\ c_2 &= -3 }$
With this the particular solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = -3 - 3x + (3\sin(x) + 3(1 - x)\cos(x))e^x }$
