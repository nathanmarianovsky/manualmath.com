Problem
Prove that the annihilator operator for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sum_{i = 0}^n a_ix^i \hspace{.5cm} \text{where} \hspace{.5cm} a_1,a_2,\dots,a_n \in \mathbb{R} }$
is given by:
$\eqalign{ A(D) = D^{n + 1} }$
Solution
The easiest way to prove is to simply plug in to show that it goes to zero:
$\eqalign{ A(D)f(x) &= D^{n + 1} \Bigg[ \sum_{i = 0}^n a_ix^i \Bigg] \\ &= D^{n + 1} (a_0 + a_1x + a_2x^2 + \dots + a_{n - 1}x^{n - 1} + a_nx^n) \\ &= 0 }$
