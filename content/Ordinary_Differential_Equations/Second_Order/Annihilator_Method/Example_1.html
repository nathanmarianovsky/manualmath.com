Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' - 3y' - 4y = 10e^{4x} }$
determine the general solution using the annihilator method.
Solution
We begin by noting that the annihilator operator from the table is going to be given by:
$\eqalign{ A(D) = D - 4 }$
Now rewriting the differential equation using operators and applying the annihilator to both sides gives:
$\eqalign{ 10e^{4x} &= y'' - 3y' - 4y \\ 10e^{4x} &= (D^2 - 3D - 4)y \\ (D - 4)10e^{4x} &= (D - 4)(D^2 - 3D - 4)y \\ 0 &= (D - 4)(D^2 - 3D - 4)y }$
With this we are left with solving the characteristic polynomial:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= (\lambda - 4)(\lambda^2 - 3\lambda - 4) \\ 0 &= (\lambda - 4)(\lambda - 4)(\lambda + 1) \\ \lambda &= -1, 4 }$
where one of the roots repeats. Knowing this we can write down the fundamental set of solutions as:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{S} = \{ e^{-x}, e^{4x}, xe^{4x} \} }$
We now form our general solution as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{4x} + c_3xe^{4x} }$
and plug into the differential equation to determine the constants for the non-homogeneous component:
$\eqalign{ 10e^{4x} &= y'' - 3y' - 4y \\ 10e^{4x} &= (c_1e^{-x} + 16c_2e^{4x} + c_3(16x + 8)e^{4x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 3(-c_1e^{-x} + 4c_2e^{4x} + c_3(1 + 4x)e^{4x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 4(c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{4x} + c_3xe^{4x}) \\ 10e^{4x} &= 5c_3e^{4x} \\ c_3 &= 2 }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{4x} + 2xe^{4x} }$
