Problem
Prove that the annihilator operator for:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = \sum_{i = 1}^n a_ie^{ix} \hspace{.5cm} \text{where} \hspace{.5cm} a_1,a_2,\dots,a_n \in \mathbb{R} }$
is given by:
$\eqalign{ A(D) = \prod_{i \in S} (D - i) }$
Solution
First we note that for the function:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = ke^{\alpha x} }$
the annihilator is given by:
$\eqalign{ A(D) = D - \alpha }$
With this in mind, the easiest way to prove is to simply plug in to show that it goes to zero:
$\eqalign{ A(D)f(x) &= \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S} (D - i) \Bigg] \Bigg[ \sum_{i \in S} a_ie^{ix} \Bigg] \\ &= \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S} (D - i) \Bigg] (a_{i_0}e^{i_0x} + a_{i_1}e^{i_1x} + \dots + a_{i_n}e^{i_nx}) \\ &= \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S \setminus \{i_0\}} (D - i) \Bigg] (D - i_0)(a_{i_0}e^{i_0x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S \setminus \{i_1\}} (D - i) \Bigg] (D - i_1)(a_{i_1}e^{i_1x}) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + \dots + \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S \setminus \{i_n\}} (D - i) \Bigg] (D - i_n)(a_{i_n}e^{i_nx}) \\ &= \sum_{k \in S} \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S \setminus \{i_k\}} (D - i) \Bigg] (D - i_k)(a_{i_k}e^{i_kx}) \\ &= \sum_{k \in S} \Bigg[ \prod_{i \in S \setminus \{i_k\}} (D - i) \Bigg] (0) \\ &= 0 }$
where the breakup of the product is allowed since it does not matter in which order we apply the annihilators since the derivative operator commutes with itself.
