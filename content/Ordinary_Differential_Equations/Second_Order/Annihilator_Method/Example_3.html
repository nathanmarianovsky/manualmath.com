Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ 3y'' - y' - 2y = 2x^2 + 6x - 4 }$
determine the general solution using the annihilator method.
Solution
We begin by noting that the annihilator operator from the table is going to be given by:
$\eqalign{ A(D) = D^3 }$
Now rewriting the differential equation using operators and applying the annihilator to both sides gives:
$\eqalign{ 2x^2 + 6x - 4 &= 3y'' - y' - 2y \\ 2x^2 + 6x - 4 &= (3D^2 - D - 2)y \\ D^3(2x^2 + 6x - 4) &= (D^3)(3D^2 - D - 2)y \\ 0 &= (D^3)(D^2 - D - 2)y }$
With this we are left with solving the characteristic polynomial:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= (\lambda^3)(3\lambda^2 - \lambda - 2) \\ 0 &= (\lambda^3)(3\lambda + 2)(\lambda - 1) \\ \lambda &= -\frac{2}{3}, 0, 1 }$
where one of the roots repeats three times. Knowing this we can write down the fundamental set of solutions as:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{S} = \{ e^{-x}, e^{-\frac{2}{3}x}, 1, x, x^2 \} }$
We now form our general solution as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{-\frac{2}{3}x} + c_3 + c_4x + c_5x^2 }$
and plug into the differential equation to determine the constants for the non-homogeneous component:
$\eqalign{ 2x^2 + 6x - 4 &= 3y'' - y' - 2y \\ 2x^2 + 6x - 4 &= 3(c_1e^{-x} + \frac{4}{9}c_2e^{-\frac{2}{3}x} + 2c_5) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - (-c_1e^{-x} - \frac{2}{3}c_2e^{-\frac{2}{3}x} + c_4 + 2c_5x) \\ & \hspace{.4cm} - 2(c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{-\frac{2}{3}x} + c_3 + c_4x + c_5x^2) \\ 2x^2 + 6x - 4 &= (-2c_5)x^2 + (-2c_4 - 2c_5)x + (-2c_3 - c_4 + 6c_5) \\ c_5 &= -1 \\ c_4 &= -2 \\ c_3 &= 0 }$
With this the general solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = c_1e^{-x} + c_2e^{-\frac{2}{3}x} - 2x - x^2 }$
