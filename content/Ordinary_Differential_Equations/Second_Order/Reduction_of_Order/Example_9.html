Problem
Given the non-linear first order differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{4}{x^2} - \frac{1}{x}y + y^2 }$
and a solution:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = \frac{2}{x} }$
Assume:
$\eqalign{ y_2(x) = y_1(x) + v }$
and determine the second solution using this substitution.
Solution
First we need:
$\eqalign{ y_2 &= \frac{2}{x} + v \\ y_2' &= -\frac{2}{x^2} + v' }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ y_2' &= -\frac{4}{x^2} - \frac{1}{x}y_2 + y_2^2 \\ -\frac{2}{x^2} + v' &= -\frac{4}{x^2} - \frac{1}{x}\Big( \frac{2}{x} + v \Big) + \Big( \frac{2}{x} + v \Big)^2 \\ -\frac{2}{x^2} + v' &= -\frac{6}{x^2} - \frac{1}{x}v + \Big( \frac{4}{x^2} + v^2 + \frac{4}{x}v \Big) \\ v' &= \frac{3}{x}v + v^2 \\ v' - \frac{3}{x}v &= v^2 }$
a Bernoulli differential equation. Now making the substitution:
$\eqalign{ w &= v^{-1} \\ w' &= -v^{-2}v' }$
and plugging in:
$\eqalign{ v' - \frac{3}{x}v &= v^2 \\ v^{-2}v' - \frac{3}{x}v^{-1} &= 1 \\ -w' - \frac{3}{x}w &= 1 \\ w' + \frac{3}{x}w &= -1 }$
gives a first order linear differential equation. First we need an integrating factor:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int \frac{3}{x} dx} \\ &= e^{3\ln|x|} \\ &= |x|^3 \\ &= x^3 }$
Now multiplying through and solving:
$\eqalign{ w' + \frac{3}{x}w &= -1 \\ x^3w' + 3x^2w &= -x^3 \\ \frac{d}{dx} (x^3w) &= -x^3 \\ \int d(x^3w) &= \int -x^3 dx \\ x^3w &= -\frac{1}{4}x^4 + C_1 \\ w &= -\frac{1}{4}x + Cx^{-3} }$
Now we need to determine $v$:
$\eqalign{ v^{-1} &= w \\ v &= w^{-1} \\ &= \Big( -\frac{x}{4} + \frac{C}{x^3} \Big)^{-1} }$
With this we can finally write down the second solution:
$\eqalign{ y_2(x) = \frac{2}{x} + v = \frac{2}{x} + \Big( -\frac{x}{4} + \frac{C}{x^3} \Big)^{-1} }$
