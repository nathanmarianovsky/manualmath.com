Problem
The non-linear first order differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = q_0(x) + q_1(x)y + q_2(x)y^2 }$
is known as a Riccati equation. Assume you have some known solution, $y_1(x)$, and that:
$\eqalign{ y_2(x) = y_1(x) + v }$
Use this substitution to obtain the second solution.
Solution
First we need:
$\eqalign{ y_2 &= y_1 + v \\ y_2' &= y_1' + v' }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ y_2' &= q_0(x) + q_1(x)y_2 + q_2(x)y_2^2 \\ y_1' + v' &= q_0(x) + q_1(x)(y_1 + v) + q_2(x)(y_1 + v)^2 \\ y_1' + v' &= (q_0(x) + q_1(x)y_1 + q_2(x)y_1^2) + q_1(x)v + 2q_2(x)y_1v + q_2(x)v^2 \\ y_1' + v' &= y_1' + q_1(x)v + 2q_2(x)y_1v + q_2(x)v^2 \\ v' &= v(q_1(x) + 2q_2(x)y_1(x)) + q_2(x)v^2 \\ v' + (-q_1(x) - 2q_2(x)y_1(x))v &= q_2(x)v^2 }$
a Bernoulli differential equation. Now making the substitution:
$\eqalign{ w &= v^{-1} \\ w' &= -v^{-2}v' }$
and plugging in:
$\eqalign{ v' + (-q_1(x) - 2q_2(x)y_1(x))v &= q_2(x)v^2 \\ v^{-2}v' + (-q_1(x) - 2q_2(x)y_1(x))v^{-1} &= q_2(x) \\ -w' - (q_1(x) + 2q_2(x)y_1(x))w &= q_2(x) \\ w' + (q_1(x) + 2q_2(x)y_1(x))w &= -q_2(x) }$
gives a first order linear differential equation. First we need an integrating factor:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int (q_1(x) + 2q_2(x)y_1(x)) dx} }$
Now multiplying through and solving:
$\eqalign{ w' + (q_1(x) + 2q_2(x)y_1(x))w &= -q_2(x) \\ \mu w' + (q_1(x) + 2q_2(x)y_1(x))\mu w &= -q_2(x)\mu \\ \frac{d}{dx} (w\mu) &= -q_2(x)\mu \\ \int d(w\mu) &= \int -q_2(x)\mu dx \\ w &= -\mu^{-1} \int q_2(x)\mu dx + C\mu^{-1} }$
Now we need to determine $v$:
$\eqalign{ v^{-1} &= w \\ v &= w^{-1} \\ &= \Big( -\mu^{-1} \int q_2(x)\mu dx + C\mu^{-1} \Big)^{-1} }$
With this we can finally write down the second solution:
$\eqalign{ y_2(x) = y_1(x) + v = y_1(x) + \Big( -\mu^{-1} \int q_2(x)\mu dx + C\mu^{-1} \Big)^{-1} }$
