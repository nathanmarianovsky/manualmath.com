Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = f(x)g(x) \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} }$
where $f(x),g(x) \in C^1$ and $g(x) \neq 0$, determine what condition makes the two linearly dependent.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} f(x)g(x) & \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \\ f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x) & \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{g^2(x)} \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \frac{f(x)f'(x)g(x) - f^2(x)g'(x)}{g(x)} - f(x)f'(x) \\ &= -\frac{f^2(x)g'(x)}{g(x)} }$
From this it seems that in order to make the two functions linearly dependent, they must satisfy:
$\eqalign{ 0 = f^2(x)g'(x) }$
which implies that either:
$\eqalign{ f(x) = 0 \hspace{.5cm} \text{or} \hspace{.5cm} g(x) = C }$
where $C$ is any real number.
