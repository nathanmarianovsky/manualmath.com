Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = e^{k_1x} \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = e^{k_2x} }$
where $k_1,k_2 \in \mathbb{R}$, determine what values of $k_1$ and $k_2$ make these two linearly dependent and independent.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} e^{k_1x} & e^{k_2x} \\ k_1e^{k_1x} & k_2e^{k_2x} \end{pmatrix} \\ &= k_2e^{(k_1 + k_2)x} - k_1e^{(k_1 + k_2)x} \\ &= (k_2 - k_1)e^{(k_1 + k_2)x} }$
It seems that whenever $k_1 = k_2$ the two are linearly dependent, otherwise they are independent.
