Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = f(x) + g(x) \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = f(x) - g(x) }$
where $f(x),g(x) \in C^1$, determine what condition makes the two linearly dependent.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} f(x) + g(x) & f(x) - g(x) \\ f'(x) + g'(x) & f'(x) - g'(x) \end{pmatrix} \\ &= (f(x) + g(x))(f'(x) - g'(x)) - (f(x) - g(x))(f'(x) + g'(x)) \\ &= 2(g(x)f'(x) - f(x)g'(x)) }$
From this it seems that in order to make the two functions linearly dependent, they must satisfy:
$\eqalign{ 0 = g(x)f'(x) - f(x)g'(x) }$
