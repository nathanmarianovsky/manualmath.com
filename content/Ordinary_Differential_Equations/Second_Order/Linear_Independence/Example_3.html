Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = x^{2n} \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = x^{2n-2} }$
where $x \neq 0$ and $n \in \mathbb{R}$, determine whether they are linearly independent or not.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} x^{2n} & x^{2n-2} \\ 2nx^{2n-1} & (2n-2)x^{2n-3} \end{pmatrix} \\ &= (2n-2)x^{4n-3} - 2nx^{4n-3} \\ &= -2x^{4n-3} }$
Since the Wronskian is non-zero, the two functions are linearly independent.
