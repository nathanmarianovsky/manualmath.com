Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = f(x) + k_1 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = f(x) + k_2 }$
where $f(x) \in C^1$ and $k_1,k_2 \in \mathbb{R}$, determine what condition makes the two linearly dependent.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} f(x) + k_1 & f(x) + k_2 \\ f'(x) & f'(x) \end{pmatrix} \\ &= f'(x)(f(x) + k_1) - f'(x)(f(x) + k_2) \\ &= f'(x)(k_1 - k_2) }$
It seems that whenever $k_1 = k_2$ the two are linearly dependent. This essentially states that given any function, a linearly independent function could be constructed from the one given by performing a vertical translation. Of course this does not hold true if:
$\eqalign{ f'(x) = 0 \implies f(x) = C }$
where $C$ is any real number.
