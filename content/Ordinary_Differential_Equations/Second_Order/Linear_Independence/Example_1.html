Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = \sin(x) \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = \cos(x) }$
determine whether they are linearly independent or not.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} \sin(x) & \cos(x) \\ \cos(x) & -\sin(x) \end{pmatrix} \\ &= -\sin^2(x) - \cos^2(x) \\ &= -1 }$
Since the Wronskian is non-zero, the two functions are linearly independent.
