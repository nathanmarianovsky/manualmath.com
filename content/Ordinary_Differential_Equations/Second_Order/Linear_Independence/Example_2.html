Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = x^7 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = 6x^7 + 4 }$
where $x \neq 0$, determine whether they are linearly independent or not.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} x^7 & 6x^7 + 4 \\ 7x^6 & 42x^6 \end{pmatrix} \\ &= 42x^{13} - 42x^{13} + 28x^6 \\ &= 28x^6 }$
Since the Wronskian is non-zero, the two functions are linearly independent.
