Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = f(k_1x) \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = f(k_2x) }$
where $f(x) \in C^1$ and $k_1,k_2 \in \mathbb{R}$, determine what values of $k_1$ and $k_2$ make these two linearly dependent and independent.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} f(k_1x) & f(k_2x) \\ k_1f(k_1x) & k_2f(k_2x) \end{pmatrix} \\ &= k_2f(k_1x)f(k_2x) - k_1f(k_1x)f(k_2x) \\ &= (k_2 - k_1)f(k_1x)f(k_2x) }$
It seems that whenever $k_1 = k_2$ the two are linearly dependent, otherwise they are independent. This essentially states that given any function, a linearly independent function could be constructed from the one given by performing a horizontal stretch.
