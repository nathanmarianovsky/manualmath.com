Problem
Given the two functions:
$\eqalign{ y_1(x) = \tan(x) \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y_2(x) = \sin(x) }$
determine what values of $x$ make them dependent.
Solution
Looking at the Wronskian:
$\eqalign{ W(y_1,y_2) &= \det \begin{pmatrix} y_1 & y_2 \\ y_1' & y_2' \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \det \begin{pmatrix} \tan(x) & \sin(x) \\ \sec^2(x) & \cos(x) \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \tan(x)\cos(x) - \sin(x)\sec^2(x) \\ &= \sin(x)(1 - \sec^2(x)) }$
To make them dependent we want:
$\eqalign{ \sin(x) &= 0 \\ x &= n\pi }$
or:
$\eqalign{ \sec^2(x) - 1 &= 0 \\ \frac{1}{\cos^2(x)} &= 1 \\ \cos^2(x) &= 1 \\ \cos(x) &= \pm 1 \\ x &= n\pi }$
where $n \in \mathbb{Z}$.
