Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' + 16y = 0 }$
where:
$\eqalign{ y\Big( \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) = -10 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y'\Big( \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) = 3 }$
determine the particular solution.
Solution
Since this is a homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the homogeneous solution. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' + 16y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} + 16e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 + 16 \\ 0 &= (m + 4i)(m - 4i) \\ m &= \pm 4i }$
to get two complex distinct values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1\cos(4x) + c_2\sin(4x) }$
where the first derivative is:
$\eqalign{ y_h'(x) = -4c_1\sin(4x) + 4c_2\cos(4x) }$
Now using the initial conditions:
$\eqalign{ y_h\Big( \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) &= c_1\cos\Big( 4 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) + c_2\sin\Big( 4 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) = -10 \\ y_h'\Big( \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) &= -4c_1\sin\Big( 4 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) + 4c_2\cos\Big( 4 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} \Big) = 3 }$
From these two equations:
$\eqalign{ c_1 &= -10 \\ c_2 &= \frac{3}{4} }$
With this the particular solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = -10\cos(4x) + \frac{3}{4}\sin(4x) }$
