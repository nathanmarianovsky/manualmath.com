Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' + 14y' + 49y = 0 }$
where:
$\eqalign{ y(-4) = -1 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y'(-4) = 5 }$
determine the particular solution.
Solution
Since this is a homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the homogeneous solution. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' + 14y' + 49y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} + 14me^{mx} + 49e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 + 14m + 49 \\ 0 &= (m + 7)^2 \\ m &= -7 }$
to get one real value for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1e^{-7x} + c_2xe^{-7x} }$
where the first derivative is:
$\eqalign{ y_h'(x) = -7c_1e^{-7x} + c_2e^{-7x} - 7c_2xe^{-7x} }$
Now using the initial conditions:
$\eqalign{ y_h(-4) &= c_1e^{-7(-4)} + c_2(-4)e^{-7(-4)} = -1 \\ y_h'(-4) &= -7c_1e^{-7(-4)} + c_2e^{-7(-4)} - 7c_2(-4)e^{-7(-4)} = 5 }$
This can be rewritten in matrix form and using the inverse a solution can be obtained:
$\eqalign{ \begin{pmatrix} e^{28} & -4e^{28} \\ -7e^{28} & 29e^{28} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} \\ \begin{pmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \end{pmatrix} &= \frac{1}{e^{56}} \begin{pmatrix} 29e^{28} & 4e^{28} \\ 7e^{28} & e^{28} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \begin{pmatrix} -9e^{-28} \\ -2e^{-28} \end{pmatrix} }$
With this the particular solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = -9e^{-7x - 28} - 2xe^{-7x - 28} }$
