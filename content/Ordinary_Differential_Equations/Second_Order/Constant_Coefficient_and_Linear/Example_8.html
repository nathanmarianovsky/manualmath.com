Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' - 8y' + 17y = 0 }$
where:
$\eqalign{ y(0) = -4 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y'(0) = -1 }$
determine the particular solution.
Solution
Since this is a homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the homogeneous solution. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' - 8y' + 17y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} - 8me^{mx} + 17e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 - 8m + 17 \\ m &= \frac{8 \pm \sqrt{64 - 68}}{2} \\ m &= 4 \pm i }$
to get two complex distinct values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = e^{4x}[c_1\cos(x) + c_2\sin(x)] }$
where the first derivative is:
$\eqalign{ y_h'(x) = 4e^{4x}[c_1\cos(x) + c_2\sin(x)] + e^{4x}[-c_1\sin(x) + c_2\cos(x)] }$
Now using the initial conditions:
$\eqalign{ y_h(0) &= e^{4(0)}[c_1\cos(0) + c_2\sin(0)] = -4 \\ y_h'(0) &= 4e^{4(0)}[c_1\cos(0) + c_2\sin(0)] \\ & \hspace{.4cm} + e^{4(0)}[-c_1\sin(0) + c_2\cos(0)] = -1 }$
From these two equations:
$\eqalign{ c_1 &= -4 \\ c_2 &= 15 }$
With this the particular solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{4x}[-4\cos(x) + 15\sin(x)] }$
