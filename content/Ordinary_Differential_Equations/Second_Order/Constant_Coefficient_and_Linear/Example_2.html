Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ y'' + 3y' - 10y = 0 }$
where:
$\eqalign{ y(0) = 4 \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} y'(0) = -2 }$
determine the particular solution.
Solution
Since this is a homogeneous linear constant coefficient equation the general solution will be given by the homogeneous solution. First we assume that:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = e^{mx} }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y'' + 3y' - 10y \\ 0 &= m^2e^{mx} + 3me^{mx} - 10e^{mx} \\ 0 &= m^2 + 3m - 10 \\ 0 &= (m - 2)(m + 5) \\ m &= -5, 2 }$
to get two real distinct values for $m$. With this the homogeneous solution is given by:
$\eqalign{ y_h(x) = c_1e^{-5x} + c_2e^{2x} }$
where the first derivative is:
$\eqalign{ y_h'(x) = -5c_1e^{-5x} + 2c_2e^{2x} }$
Now using the initial conditions:
$\eqalign{ y_h(0) &= c_1e^{-5(0)} + c_2e^{2(0)} = 4 \\ y_h'(0) &= -5c_1e^{-5(0)} + 2c_2e^{2(0)} = -2 }$
This can be rewritten in matrix form and using the inverse a solution can be obtained:
$\eqalign{ \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ -5 & 2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \end{pmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} \\ \begin{pmatrix} c_1 \\ c_2 \end{pmatrix} &= \frac{1}{7} \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 5 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \begin{pmatrix} \frac{10}{7} \\ \frac{18}{7} \end{pmatrix} }$
With this the particular solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ y(x) = \frac{10}{7}e^{-5x} + \frac{18}{7}e^{2x} }$
