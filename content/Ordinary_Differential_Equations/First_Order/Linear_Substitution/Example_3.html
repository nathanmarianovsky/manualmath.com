Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = 2 + \sqrt{y - 2x + 3} }$
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y - 2x + 3 \\ u' &= y' - 2 }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= 2 + \sqrt{y - 2x + 3} \\ y' - 2 &= \sqrt{y - 2x + 3} \\ u' &= \sqrt{u} \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= u^{\frac{1}{2}} \\ u^{-\frac{1}{2}} du &= dx \\ \int u^{-\frac{1}{2}} du &= \int dx \\ 2\sqrt{u} &= x + C \\ 2\sqrt{y - 2x + 3} - x &= C }$
