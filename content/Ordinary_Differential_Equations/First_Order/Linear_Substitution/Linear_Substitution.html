The Setup
Given a differential equation in the form:
$\eqalign{ y' = f(ax + by + c) }$
where $y'$ denotes the first derivative and $a,b,c \in \mathbb{R}$, using a substitution the differential equation can be solved for exactly.
Solving the Equation
Make the substitution for the linear factor:
$\eqalign{ u &= ax + by + c \\ u' &= a + by' }$
and plug into the equation:
$\eqalign{ y' &= f(ax + by + c) \\ \frac{u' - a}{b} &= f(u) \\ u' - a &= bf(u) \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= bf(u) + a \\ \frac{du}{bf(u) + a} &= dx \\ \int \frac{du}{bf(u) + a} &= \int dx \\ L(u) &= x + K \\ L(ax + by + c) - x &= K }$
where:
$\eqalign{ L(u) = \int \frac{du}{bf(u) + a} \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} K \in \mathbb{R} }$
