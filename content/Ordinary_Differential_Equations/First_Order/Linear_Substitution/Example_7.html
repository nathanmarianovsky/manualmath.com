Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = e^{9y - x} }$
where $y(0) = 0$.
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= 9y - x \\ u' &= 9y' - 1 }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= e^{9y - x} \\ \frac{1}{9}(u' + 1) &= e^u \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= 9e^u - 1 \\ \frac{du}{9e^u - 1} &= dx \\ \int \frac{du}{9e^u - 1} &= \int dx \\ \int \frac{e^{-u}}{9 - e^{-u}} &= \int dx \\ \ln|9 - e^{-u}| &= x + C \\ \ln|9 - e^{9y - x}| - x &= C }$
Now for the constant use the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ C &= \ln|9 - e^{9y - x}| - x \\ &= \ln|9 - e^{9(0) - (0)}| - (0) \\ &= \ln(8) }$
and so the final solution is:
$\eqalign{ \ln|9 - e^{9y - x}| - x = \ln(8) }$
