Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \tan^2(x + y) }$
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x + y \\ u' &= 1 + y' }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= \tan^2(x + y) \\ u' - 1 &= \tan^2(u) \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= \tan^2(u) + 1 \\ \frac{du}{\tan^2(u) + 1} &= dx \\ \int \frac{du}{\tan^2(u) + 1} &= \int dx \\ \frac{1}{2}(u + \sin(u)\cos(u)) &= x + C \\ \frac{1}{2}(u + \sin(u)\cos(u)) - x &= C \\ \frac{1}{2}(y - x + \sin(x + y)\cos(x + y)) &= C }$
