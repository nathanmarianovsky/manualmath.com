Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \cos(x + y) - 1 }$
where $y(0) = \frac{\pi}{4}$.
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x + y \\ u' &= 1 + y' }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= \cos(x + y) - 1 \\ y' + 1 &= \cos(x + y) \\ u' &= \cos(u) \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= \cos(u) \\ \sec(u) du &= dx \\ \int \sec(u) du &= \int dx \\ \ln|\sec(u) + \tan(u)| &= x + C \\ \ln|\sec(x + y) + \tan(x + y)| - x &= C }$
Now for the constant use the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ C &= \ln|\sec(x + y) + \tan(x + y)| - x \\ &= \ln\Bigg| \sec\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big) + \tan\Big( \frac{\pi}{4} \Big) \Bigg| \\ &= \ln(\sqrt{2} + 1) }$
and so the final solution is:
$\eqalign{ \ln|\sec(x + y) + \tan(x + y)| - x = \ln(\sqrt{2} + 1) }$
