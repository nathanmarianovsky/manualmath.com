Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = (x + y + 1)^2 }$
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x + y + 1 \\ u' &= 1 + y' }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= (x + y + 1)^2 \\ u' - 1 &= u^2 \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= u^2 + 1 \\ \frac{du}{1 + u^2} &= dx \\ \int \frac{du}{1 + u^2} &= \int dx \\ \arctan(u) &= x + C \\ \arctan(u) - x &= C \\ \arctan(x + y + 1) - x &= C }$
