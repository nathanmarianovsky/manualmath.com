Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = 1 + e^{y - x + 5} }$
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y - x + 5 \\ u' &= y' - 1 }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= 1 + e^{y - x + 5} \\ y' - 1 &= e^{y - x + 5} \\ u' &= e^u \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= e^u \\ e^{-u} du &= dx \\ \int e^{-u} du &= \int dx \\ -e^u &= x + C \\ e^{y - x + 5} + x &= K }$
where:
$\eqalign{ K = -C }$
gets rid of the unnecessary negative sign.
