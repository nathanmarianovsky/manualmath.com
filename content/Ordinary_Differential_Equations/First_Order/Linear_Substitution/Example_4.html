Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1 - x - y}{x + y} }$
Solution
Since the function contains a linear input make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x + y \\ u' &= 1 + y' }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y' &= \frac{1 - x - y}{x + y} \\ u' - 1 &= \frac{1 - u}{u} \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= \frac{1 - u}{u} + \frac{u}{u} \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= \frac{1}{u} \\ u du &= dx \\ \int u du &= \int dx \\ \frac{1}{2}u^2 &= x + C \\ \frac{1}{2}(x + y)^2 - x &= C }$
