Problem
Keeping substitution in mind, solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ xy' = y\ln(xy) }$
where $y'$ is the first derivative.
Solution
To motivate the approach consider the fact that we want to simplify the inside of the logarithm as much as possible. Therefore, it would be useful if the inside could become a single variable. Using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ y &= \frac{u}{x} \\ y' &= \frac{u'x - u}{x^2} }$
and plugging in:
$\eqalign{ xy' &= y\ln(xy) \\ x \cdot \frac{u'x - u}{x^2} &= \frac{u}{x}\ln\Big( x \cdot \frac{u}{x} \Big) \\ u'x - u &= u\ln(u) \\ \frac{du}{dx}x &= u(\ln(u) - 1) \\ \frac{du}{u(\ln(u) - 1)} &= \frac{dx}{x} \\ \int \frac{du}{u(\ln(u) - 1)} &= \int \frac{dx}{x} \\ \ln|\ln(u) - 1| &= \ln|x| + C \\ \ln\Bigg| \frac{\ln(xy) - 1}{x} \Bigg| &= C }$
