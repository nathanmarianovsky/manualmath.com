Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = 2x + 3y + 1 }$
and the initial condition $y(1) = 5$, determine $y(1.2)$ using a $h = 0.1$ with Euler's Method up to four decimal places and compare the result to the exact solution by finding the error.
Solution
Using Euler's Method:
$n$ $x_{n + 1} = x_n + h$ $y_{n + 1} = y_n + hf(x_n,y_n)$
0 $x_1 = x_0 + h = 1.1$ $y_1 = y_0 + hf(x_0,y_0) = 6.8$
1 $x_2 = x_1 + h = 1.2$ $y_2 = y_1 + hf(x_1,y_1) = 9.16$
So according to Euler's Method $y(1.2) \approx 9.16$. To solve for exactly, use the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= 2x + 3y + 1 \\ u' &= 2 + 3y' }$
to make the equation separable:
$\eqalign{ y' &= 2x + 3y + 1 \\ \frac{1}{3}(u' - 2) &= u \\ \frac{du}{dx} &= 3u + 2 \\ \frac{du}{3u + 2} &= dx \\ \int \frac{du}{3u + 2} &= \int dx \\ \frac{1}{3}\ln|3u + 2| &= x + C \\ \frac{1}{3}\ln|6x + 9y + 5| - x &= C }$
Now using the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ C &= \frac{1}{3}\ln|6x + 9y + 5| - x \\ &= \frac{1}{3}\ln|6(1) + 9(5) + 5| - 1 \\ &= \frac{1}{3}\ln(56) - 1 }$
which gives the final solution:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{3}\ln|6x + 9y + 5| - x = \frac{1}{3}\ln(56) - 1 }$
To determine the value of $y$ first lets isolate it:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{3}\ln|6x + 9y + 5| - x &= \frac{1}{3}\ln(56) - 1 \\ \ln|6x + 9y + 5| &= \ln(56) - 3 + 3x \\ 6x + 9y + 5 &= e^{\ln(56) - 3 + 3x} \\ 9y &= 56e^{3x - 3} - 6x - 5 \\ y &= \frac{56}{9}e^{3x - 3} - \frac{2}{3}x - \frac{5}{9} }$
Now to determine the exact value:
$\eqalign{ y(1.2) &= \frac{56}{9}e^{3(1.2) - 3} - \frac{2}{3}(1.2) - \frac{5}{9} &= 9.9821 }$
and so the error is:
$\eqalign{ \Delta = \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \text{Exact} \hspace{.1cm} - \text{Approximation} \hspace{.1cm} \Big| = |9.9821 - 9.16| = 0.8221 }$
