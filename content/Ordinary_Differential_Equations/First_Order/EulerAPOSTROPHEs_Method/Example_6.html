Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = y(10 - 2y) }$
and the initial condition $y(0) = 1$, determine $y(1)$ using a $h = 0.2$ with Euler's Method up to four decimal places and compare the result to the exact solution by finding the error.
Solution
Using Euler's Method:
$n$ $x_{n + 1} = x_n + h$ $y_{n + 1} = y_n + hf(x_n,y_n)$
0 $x_1 = x_0 + h = 0.2$ $y_1 = y_0 + hf(x_0,y_0) = 2.6000$
1 $x_2 = x_1 + h = 0.4$ $y_2 = y_1 + hf(x_1,y_1) = 5.0960$
2 $x_3 = x_2 + h = 0.6$ $y_3 = y_2 + hf(x_2,y_2) = 4.9003$
3 $x_4 = x_3 + h = 0.8$ $y_4 = y_3 + hf(x_3,y_3) = 5.0957$
4 $x_5 = x_4 + h = 1$ $y_5 = y_4 + hf(x_4,y_4) = 4.9006$
So according to Euler's Method $y(1) \approx 4.9006$. To solve for exactly separate:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= y(10 - 2y) \\ \frac{dy}{y(10 - 2y)} &= dx \\ \int \frac{dy}{y(10 - 2y)} &= \int dx \\ \frac{1}{10}\ln\Bigg| \frac{y}{5 - y} \Bigg| &= x + C \\ \frac{y}{5 - y} &= e^{10x + 10C} \\ y &= 5e^{10x + D} - ye^{10x + D} \\ y(1 + e^{10x + D}) &= 5e^{10x + D} \\ y &= \frac{5e^{10x + D}}{1 + e^{10x + D}} }$
where:
$\eqalign{ D = 10C }$
Now using the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ y &= \frac{5e^{10x + D}}{1 + e^{10x + D}} \\ 1 &= \frac{5e^D}{1 + e^D} \\ 1 + e^D &= 5e^D \\ 1 &= 4e^D \\ D &= \ln\Big( \frac{1}{4} \Big) }$
which gives the final solution:
$\eqalign{ y = \frac{5e^{10x}}{4 + e^{10x}} }$
Now to determine the exact value:
$\eqalign{ y(1) &= \frac{5e^{10(1)}}{4 + e^{10(1)}} &= 4.9991 }$
and so the error is:
$\eqalign{ \Delta = \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \text{Exact} \hspace{.1cm} - \text{Approximation} \hspace{.1cm} \Big| = |4.9991 - 4.9006| = 0.0985 }$
