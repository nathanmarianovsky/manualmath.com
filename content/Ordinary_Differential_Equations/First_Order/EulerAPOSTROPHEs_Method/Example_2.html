Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = 2xy }$
and the initial condition $y(1) = 1$, determine $y(1.3)$ using a $h = 0.05$ with Euler's Method up to four decimal places and compare the result to the exact solution by finding the error.
Solution
Using Euler's Method:
$n$ $x_{n + 1} = x_n + h$ $y_{n + 1} = y_n + hf(x_n,y_n)$
0 $x_1 = x_0 + h = 1.05$ $y_1 = y_0 + hf(x_0,y_0) = 1.1000$
1 $x_2 = x_1 + h = 1.1$ $y_2 = y_1 + hf(x_1,y_1) = 1.2155$
2 $x_3 = x_2 + h = 1.15$ $y_3 = y_2 + hf(x_2,y_2) = 1.3492$
3 $x_4 = x_3 + h = 1.2$ $y_4 = y_3 + hf(x_3,y_3) = 1.5044$
4 $x_5 = x_4 + h = 1.25$ $y_5 = y_4 + hf(x_4,y_4) = 1.6849$
5 $x_6 = x_5 + h = 1.3$ $y_6 = y_5 + hf(x_5,y_5) = 1.8955$
So according to Euler's Method $y(1.3) \approx 1.8955$. To solve for exactly separate:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= 2xy \\ \frac{dy}{y} &= 2x dx \\ \int \frac{dy}{y} &= \int 2x dx \\ \ln|y| &= x^2 + C \\ y &= De^{x^2} }$
where:
$\eqalign{ D = e^C }$
Now using the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ y &= De^{x^2} \\ 1 &= De \\ D &= e^{-1} }$
gives the final solution:
$\eqalign{ y = e^{x^2 - 1} }$
Now to determine the exact value:
$\eqalign{ y(1.3) &= e^{(1.3)^2 - 1} &= 1.9937 }$
and so the error is:
$\eqalign{ \Delta = \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \text{Exact} \hspace{.1cm} - \text{Approximation} \hspace{.1cm} \Big| = |1.9937 - 1.8955| = 0.0982 }$
