Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = y - y^2 }$
and the initial condition $y(0) = 0.5$, determine $y(0.5)$ using a $h = 0.1$ with Euler's Method up to four decimal places and compare the result to the exact solution by finding the error.
Solution
Using Euler's Method:
$n$ $x_{n + 1} = x_n + h$ $y_{n + 1} = y_n + hf(x_n,y_n)$
0 $x_1 = x_0 + h = 0.1$ $y_1 = y_0 + hf(x_0,y_0) = 0.5250$
1 $x_2 = x_1 + h = 0.2$ $y_2 = y_1 + hf(x_1,y_1) = 0.5499$
2 $x_3 = x_2 + h = 0.3$ $y_3 = y_2 + hf(x_2,y_2) = 0.5747$
3 $x_4 = x_3 + h = 0.4$ $y_4 = y_3 + hf(x_3,y_3) = 0.5991$
4 $x_5 = x_4 + h = 0.5$ $y_5 = y_4 + hf(x_4,y_4) = 0.6231$
So according to Euler's Method $y(0.5) \approx 0.6231$. To solve for exactly separate:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= y - y^2 \\ \frac{dy}{y(1 - y)} &= dx \\ \int \frac{dy}{y(1 - y)} &= \int dx \\ \ln\Bigg| \frac{y}{1 - y} \Bigg| &= x + C \\ \frac{y}{1 - y} &= De^x \\ y &= De^x - Dye^x \\ y(1 + De^x) &= De^x \\ y &= \frac{De^x}{1 + De^x} }$
where:
$\eqalign{ D = e^C }$
Now using the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ y &= \frac{De^x}{1 + De^x} \\ \frac{1}{2} &= \frac{D}{1 + D} \\ 1 + D &= 2D \\ D &= 1 }$
gives the final solution:
$\eqalign{ y = \frac{e^x}{1 + e^x} }$
Now to determine the exact value:
$\eqalign{ y(0.5) &= \frac{e^{(0.5)}}{1 + e^{(0.5)}} &= 0.6225 }$
and so the error is:
$\eqalign{ \Delta = \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \text{Exact} \hspace{.1cm} - \text{Approximation} \hspace{.1cm} \Big| = |0.6225 - 0.6231| = 0.0006 }$
