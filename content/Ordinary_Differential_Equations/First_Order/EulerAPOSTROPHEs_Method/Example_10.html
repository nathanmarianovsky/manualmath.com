Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = xy^2 - \frac{y}{x} }$
and the initial condition $y(1) = 1$, determine $y(1.2)$ using a $h = 0.05$ with Euler's Method up to four decimal places and compare the result to the exact solution by finding the error.
Solution
Using Euler's Method:
$n$ $x_{n + 1} = x_n + h$ $y_{n + 1} = y_n + hf(x_n,y_n)$
0 $x_1 = x_0 + h = 1.05$ $y_1 = y_0 + hf(x_0,y_0) = 1.0000$
1 $x_2 = x_1 + h = 1.10$ $y_2 = y_1 + hf(x_1,y_1) = 1.0049$
2 $x_3 = x_2 + h = 1.15$ $y_3 = y_2 + hf(x_2,y_2) = 1.0148$
3 $x_4 = x_3 + h = 1.20$ $y_4 = y_3 + hf(x_3,y_3) = 1.0299$
So according to Euler's Method $y(1.2) \approx 1.0299$. To solve first reorganize:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= xy^2 - \frac{y}{x} \\ y' + \frac{1}{x}y &= xy^2 \\ y^{-2}y' + \frac{1}{x}y^{-1} &= x }$
Now use the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y^{-1} \\ u' &= -y^{-2}y' }$
and plug in:
$\eqalign{ y^{-2}y' + \frac{1}{x}y^{-1} &= x \\ -u' + \frac{1}{x}u &= x \\ u' - \frac{1}{x}u &= -x }$
Follow through by finding the integrating factor and solving:
$\eqalign{ u' - \frac{1}{x}u &= -x \\ x^{-1}u' - x^{-2}u &= -1 \\ \frac{d}{dx}(x^{-1}u) &= -1 \\ d(x^{-1}u) &= -dx \\ \int d(x^{-1}u) &= \int -dx \\ x^{-1}u &= -x + C \\ u &= -x^2 + Cx }$
To finish off get the solution for $y$:
$\eqalign{ y^{-1} &= u \\ y &= u^{-1} \\ &= \frac{1}{Cx - x^2} \\ }$
Now using the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ y &= \frac{1}{Cx - x^2} \\ 1 &= \frac{1}{C - 1} \\ C - 1 &= 1 \\ C &= 2 }$
gives the final solution:
$\eqalign{ y = \frac{1}{2x - x^2} }$
Now to determine the exact value:
$\eqalign{ y(1.2) &= \frac{1}{2(1.2) - (1.2)^2} &= 1.0417 }$
and so the error is:
$\eqalign{ \Delta = \Big| \hspace{.1cm} \text{Exact} \hspace{.1cm} - \text{Approximation} \hspace{.1cm} \Big| = |1.0417 - 1.0299| = 0.0118 }$
