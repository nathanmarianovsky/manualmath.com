Problem
The velocity, $v$, of a body of mass $m$ that is falling under the influence of gravity can be modeled by:
$\eqalign{ m\frac{dv}{dt} = mg - kv }$
where $g$ is the acceleration due to gravity and $k$ is some positive constant. Because the term $-kv$ represents air resistance, the velocity of a body falling from a great height does not increase without bound as time $t$ increases. Use this to determine the terminal velocity of the body at large $t$.
Solution
First to obtain the equilibrium point:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dv}{dt} &= 0 \\ g - \frac{k}{m}v &= 0 \\ v &= \frac{mg}{k} }$
Now to determine the behavior of the derivative:
Range $v'(t)$ Behavior
$\Big( 0, \frac{mg}{k} \Big)$ +
$\Big( \frac{mg}{k}, \infty \Big)$ -
For the single equilibrium point, both below and above the vectors point towards. Therefore:
$\eqalign{ \lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} v(t) = \frac{mg}{k} }$
for any initial condition.
