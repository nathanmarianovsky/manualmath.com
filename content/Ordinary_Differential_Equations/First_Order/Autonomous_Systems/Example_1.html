Problem
Given the autonomous system:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = y^2 - 3y }$
determine all the equilibrium points and graph the phase plot. Which equilibrium points are stable? Unstable? Semistable?
Solution
First to obtain all the equilibrium points:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= 0 \\ y^2 - 3y &= 0 \\ y(y - 3) &= 0 \\ y &= 0, 3 }$
Now to determine the behavior of the derivative:
Range $y'(x)$ Behavior
$(-\infty,0)$ +
$(0,3)$ -
$(3,\infty)$ +
and use this to graph the phase plot:
For $y = 0$ since the vectors are pointing towards from both below and above, the equilibrium point is stable. On the other hand, for $y = 1$ the vectors are pointing away from both below and above, thus the equilibrium point is unstable.
