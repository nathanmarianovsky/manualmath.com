Problem
Consider the autonomous system:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = y - y\sin(y) }$
determine all the equilibrium points and graph the phase plot for the region:
$\eqalign{ \mathcal{R} = \{ (x,y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 \hspace{.1cm} | \hspace{.1cm} -4\pi \leq x,y \leq 4\pi \} }$
Which equilibrium points are stable? Unstable? Semistable?
Solution
First to obtain all the equilibrium points:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= 0 \\ y - y\sin(y) &= 0 \\ y(1 - \sin(y)) &= 0 \\ y &= 0, \frac{\pi + 4k\pi}{2} }$
where:
$\eqalign{ k \in \mathbb{Z} }$
Now to determine the behavior of the derivative consider the fact that the only thing contributing to the sign of the derivative is the $y$ outside since the rest is always positive. So whenever $y < 0$ the derivative is negative and for $y > 0$ it is positive. Using this to graph the phase plot:
For $y = 0$ the vectors below and above are pointing away, thus the equilibrium point is unstable. All other equilibrium points are semistable because one side will lean towards, while the other away since the direction is going to stay downwards for the region below $y = 0$ and upwards for the region above.
