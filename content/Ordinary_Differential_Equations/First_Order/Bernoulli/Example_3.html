Problem
Determine the family of solutions to:
$\eqalign{ y' = 5y - 5ty^3 }$
Solution
Begin by rearranging to see the Bernoulli form and divide throughout by the non-linear term:
$\eqalign{ y' &= 5y - 5ty^3 \\ y' - 5y &= -5ty^3 \\ y'y^{-3} - 5y^{-2} &= -5t }$
Now make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y^{-2} \\ u' &= -2y^{-3}y' }$
and plug in to get:
$\eqalign{ y'y^{-3} - 5y^{-2} &= -5t \\ -\frac{1}{2}u' - 5u &= -5t \\ u' + 10u &= 10t }$
a first order linear differential equation. Now an integrating factor is needed:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int p(t) dt} \\ &= e^{\int 10 dt} \\ &= e^{10t} }$
Multiplying throughout and solving for $u$ gives:
$\eqalign{ u' + 10u &= 10t \\ e^{10t}u' + 10e^{10t}u &= 10te^{10t} \\ \frac{d}{dt}(e^{10t}u) &= 10te^{10t} \\ d(e^{10t}u) &= 10te^{10t} dt \\ \int d(e^{10t}u) &= \int 10te^{10t} dt \\ e^{10t}u &= \frac{1}{10}e^{10t}(10t - 1) + C \\ u &= \frac{1}{10}(10t - 1) + Ce^{-10t} }$
Now to solve for $y$ use the original substitution:
$\eqalign{ y^{-2} &= u \\ y &= \pm u^{-\frac{1}{2}} \\ &= \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{1}{10}(10t - 1) + Ce^{-10t}}} }$
