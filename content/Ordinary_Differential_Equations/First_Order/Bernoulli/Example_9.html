Problem
Determine the family of solutions to:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{x}y = y^2 }$
and the interval of existance for each solution.
Solution
Right away it is clear that this differential equation conforms to the Bernoulli one. To begin divide throughout by the non-linear term:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{x}y &= y^2 \\ y'y^{-2} + \frac{1}{x}y^{-1} &= 1 }$
Now make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y^{-1} \\ u' &= -y^{-2}y' }$
and plug in to get:
$\eqalign{ y'y^{-2} + \frac{1}{x}y^{-1} &= 1 \\ -u' + \frac{1}{x}u &= 1 \\ u' - \frac{1}{x}u &= -1 }$
a first-order linear differential equation. Now an integrating factor is needed:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int p(x) dx} \\ &= e^{\int -\frac{1}{x} dx} \\ &= e^{-\ln|x|} \\ &= \frac{1}{|x|} \\ &= \frac{1}{x} }$
where dropping the absolute value is allowed since putting a negative sign in front will not chance anything. Next multiplying throughout and solving for $u$ gives:
$\eqalign{ u' - \frac{1}{x}u &= -1 \\ x^{-1}u' - x^{-2}u &= -x^{-1} \\ \frac{d}{dx}(x^{-1}u) &= -x^{-1} \\ d(x^{-1}u) &= -x^{-1} dx \\ \int d(x^{-1}u) &= \int -x^{-1} dx \\ x^{-1}u &= -\ln|x| + C \\ u &= -x\ln|x| + Cx }$
Now to solve for $y$ use the original substitution:
$\eqalign{ y^{-1} &= u \\ y &= u^{-1} \\ &= \frac{1}{-x\ln|x| + Cx} }$
To determine the interval of existance, we just have to account for the vertical asymptotes of the function:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= -x\ln|x| + Cx \\ 0 &= x(-\ln|x| + C) \\ x &= 0, e^C, -e^C }$
With the bad points accounted for, the interval of existance for all solutions is:
$\eqalign{ (-\infty, -e^C) \cup (-e^C, 0) \cup (0, e^C) \cup (e^C, \infty) }$
