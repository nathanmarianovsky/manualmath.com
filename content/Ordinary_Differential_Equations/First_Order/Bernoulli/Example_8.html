Problem
Determine the family of solutions to:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{3}y = e^xy^4 }$
Solution
Right away it is clear that this differential equation conforms to the Bernoulli one. To begin divide throughout by the non-linear term:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{3}y &= e^xy^4 \\ y'y^{-4} + \frac{1}{3}y^{-3} &= e^x }$
Now make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y^{-3} \\ u' &= -3y^{-4}y' }$
and plug in to get:
$\eqalign{ y'y^{-4} + \frac{1}{3}y^{-3} &= e^x \\ -\frac{1}{3}u' + \frac{1}{3}u &= e^x \\ u' - u &= -3e^x }$
a first-order linear differential equation. Now an integrating factor is needed:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int p(x) dx} \\ &= e^{\int -1 dx} \\ &= e^{-x} }$
where dropping the absolute value is allowed since squaring guarantees a positive result. Next multiplying throughout and solving for $u$ gives:
$\eqalign{ u' - u &= -3e^x \\ e^{-x}u' - e^{-x}u &= -3 \\ \frac{d}{dx}(e^{-x}u) &= -3 \\ d(e^{-x}u) &= -3 dx \\ \int d(e^{-x}u) &= \int -3 dx \\ e^{-x}u &= -3x + C \\ u &= -3xe^x + Ce^x }$
Now to solve for $y$ use the original substitution:
$\eqalign{ y^{-3} &= u \\ y &= u^{-\frac{1}{3}} \\ &= \Big( -3xe^x + Ce^x \Big)^{-\frac{1}{3}} \\ }$
