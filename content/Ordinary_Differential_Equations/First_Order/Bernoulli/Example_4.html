Problem
Determine the family of solutions to:
$\eqalign{ x\frac{dy}{dx} + y = y^2x^2\ln(x) }$
Solution
Begin by rearranging to see the Bernoulli form and divide throughout by the non-linear term:
$\eqalign{ x\frac{dy}{dx} + y &= y^2x^2\ln(x) \\ \frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{1}{x}y &= y^2x\ln(x) \\ y'y^{-2} + \frac{1}{x}y^{-1} &= x\ln(x) }$
Now make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= y^{-1} \\ u' &= -y^{-2}y' }$
and plug in to get:
$\eqalign{ y'y^{-2} + \frac{1}{x}y^{-1} &= x\ln(x) \\ -u' + \frac{1}{x}u &= x\ln(x) \\ u' - \frac{1}{x}u &= -x\ln(x) }$
a first order linear differential equation. Now an integrating factor is needed:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int p(x) dx} \\ &= e^{\int -\frac{1}{x} dx} \\ &= e^{-\ln|x|} \\ &= e^{\ln(|x|^{-1})} \\ &= \frac{1}{|x|} \\ &= \frac{1}{x} }$
where dropping the absolute value is allowed since putting a negative sign in front will not change anything. Next multiplying throughout and solving for $u$ gives:
$\eqalign{ u' - \frac{1}{x}u &= -x\ln(x) \\ \frac{1}{x}u' - \frac{1}{x^2}u &= -\ln(x) \\ \frac{d}{dx}\Big( \frac{u}{x} \Big) &= -\ln(x) \\ d\Big( \frac{u}{x} \Big) &= -\ln(x) dx \\ \int d\Big( \frac{u}{x} \Big) &= \int -\ln(x) dx \\ \frac{u}{x} &= -x\ln(x) + x + C \\ u &= -x^2\ln(x) + x^2 + Cx }$
Now to solve for $y$ use the original substitution:
$\eqalign{ y^{-1} &= u \\ y &= u^{-1} \\ &= \frac{1}{-x^2\ln(x) + x^2 + Cx} }$
