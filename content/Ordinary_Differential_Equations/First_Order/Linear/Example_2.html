Problem
Solve the following differential equation:
$\eqalign{ ty' - 2y = t^5\sin(2t) - t^3 + 4t^4 }$
where $y(\pi) = \frac{3}{2}\pi^4$.
Solution
First rearranging gives:
$\eqalign{ ty' - 2y &= t^5\sin(2t) - t^3 + 4t^4 \\ y' - \frac{2}{t}y &= t^4\sin(2t) - t^2 + 4t^3 }$
Now this is in the form of a first order linear differential equation. Next the integrating factor is needed:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int -\frac{2}{t} dt} \\ &= e^{-2\ln|t|} \\ &= e^{\ln(|t|^{-2})} \\ &= (|t|)^{-2} \\ &= t^{-2} }$
Now multiplying the equation throughout by the integrating factor gives:
$\eqalign{ y' - \frac{2}{t}y &= t^4\sin(2t) - t^2 + 4t^3 \\ t^{-2}y' - 2t^{-3}y &= t^2\sin(2t) - 1 + 4t \\ \frac{d}{dt} (t^{-2}y) &= t^2\sin(2t) - 1 + 4t \\ d(t^{-2}y) &= (t^2\sin(2t) - 1 + 4t) dt \\ \int d(t^{-2}y) &= \int (t^2\sin(2t) - 1 + 4t) dt \\ t^{-2}y &= \frac{1}{4}((1 - 2t^2)\cos(2t) + 2t\sin(2t)) - t + 2t^2 + C \\ y &= \frac{1}{4}((t^2 - 2t^4)\cos(2t) + 2t^3\sin(2t)) - t^3 + 2t^4 + Ct^2 }$
To determine the value of $C$ use the initial value given:
$\eqalign{ \frac{3}{2}\pi^4 &= y(\pi) \\ \frac{3}{2}\pi^4 &= \frac{1}{4}(((\pi)^2 - 2(\pi)^4)\cos(2\pi) + 2(\pi)^3\sin(2\pi)) - (\pi)^3 + 2(\pi)^4 + C(\pi)^2 \\ 0 &= \frac{1}{4}\pi^2 - \pi^3 + C\pi^2 \\ C &= \pi - \frac{1}{4} }$
so that the final solution can be written down as:
$\eqalign{ y = \frac{1}{4}((t^2 - 2t^4)\cos(2t) + 2t^3\sin(2t)) - t^3 + 2t^4 + \Big( \pi - \frac{1}{4} \Big)t^2 }$
