Problem
Determine all solutions to:
$\eqalign{ t\ln(t)\frac{dr}{dt} + r = te^t }$
Solution
First rearranging gives:
$\eqalign{ t\ln(t)\frac{dr}{dt} + r &= te^t \\ \frac{dr}{dt} + \frac{1}{t\ln(t)}r &= \frac{e^t}{\ln(t)} }$
Now this is in the form of a first-order linear differential equation. Next the integrating factor is needed:
$\eqalign{ \mu &= e^{\int \frac{1}{t\ln(t)} dt} \\ &= e^{\ln|\ln(t)|} \\ &= |\ln(t)| \\ &= \ln(t) }$
where dropping the absolute value is allowed since the original domain of the function to begin with is $(0,\infty)$. Even if $t \in (0,1)$, putting a negative sign in front will not change the result. Now multiplying the equation throughout by the integrating factor gives:
$\eqalign{ \ln(t)\frac{dr}{dt} + \frac{1}{t}r &= e^t \\ \frac{d}{dt}(\ln(t)r) &= e^t \\ d(\ln(t)r) &= e^t dt \\ \int d(\ln(t)r) &= \int e^t dt \\ \ln(t)r &= e^t + C \\ r &= \frac{e^t + C}{\ln(t)} }$
