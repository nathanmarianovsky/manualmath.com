Problem
Determine all solutions to:
$\eqalign{ w'' + p(x)w' = q(x) }$
Solution
First make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ y &= w' \\ y' &= w'' }$
and plug in to get:
$\eqalign{ y' + p(x)y &= q(x) \\ y &= \frac{1}{\mu(x)}(L(x) + C) }$
the general solution obtained in the notes. Now to solve for $w$:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dw}{dx} &= y(x) \\ dw &= y(x) dx \\ \int dw &= \int \frac{1}{\mu(x)}(L(x) + C) dx \\ w(x) &= J(x) + D }$
where:
$\eqalign{ J(x) = \int \frac{1}{\mu(x)}(L(x) + C) dx \hspace{.5cm} }$
and $D$ is the constant of integration coming out of the second integral.
