Problem
Determine all solutions to:
$\eqalign{ w^{(n)} + p(x)w^{(n-1)} = q(x) }$
where $(n)$ denotes the $n$th derivative.
Solution
First make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ y &= w^{(n-1)} \\ y' &= w^{(n)} }$
and plug in to get:
$\eqalign{ y' + p(x)y &= q(x) \\ y &= \frac{1}{\mu(x)}(L(x) + C_1) }$
the general solution obtained in the notes. Now to solve for $w$:
$\eqalign{ \frac{d^{(n-1)}}{dx^{(n-1)}} &= y(x) \\ d^{(n-1)}w &= y(x) dx^{(n-1)} \\ \underbrace{\int \int \int \dots \int}_{n-1 \hspace{.1cm} \text{of them}} d^{(n-1)}w &= \underbrace{\int \int \int \dots \int}_{n-1 \hspace{.1cm} \text{of them}} \frac{1}{\mu(x)}(L(x) + C) dx^{(n-1)} \\ w(x) &= T(x) + C_n }$
where $T(x)$ denotes the $(n-1)$th antiderivative of $(\mu(x))^{-1}(L(x) + C_1)$ and $C_n$ is the constant of integration coming out of the last integral.
