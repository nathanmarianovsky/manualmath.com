Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ x\frac{dy}{dx} = y + \sqrt{x^2 - y^2} }$
where $x > 0$.
Solution
First rewriting this gives:
$\eqalign{ x\frac{dy}{dx} &= y + \sqrt{x^2 - y^2} \\ \frac{dy}{dx} &= \frac{y + \sqrt{x^2 - y^2}}{x} }$
Now to see if the homogeneous substitution can be used, first lets check that the function is homogeneous with degree zero:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) &= \frac{y + \sqrt{x^2 - y^2}}{x} \\ f(tx,ty) &= \frac{(ty) + \sqrt{(tx)^2 - (ty)^2}}{(tx)} \\ &= \frac{ty + t\sqrt{x^2 - y^2}}{tx} \\ &= \frac{y + \sqrt{x^2 - y^2}}{x} \\ &= f(x,y) }$
With the condition checked, using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ y &= ux \\ y' &= u'x + u }$
and plugging in gives:
$\eqalign{ y' &= \frac{y + \sqrt{x^2 - y^2}}{x} \\ u'x + u &= \frac{ux + \sqrt{x^2 - u^2x^2}}{x} \\ u'x + u &= u + \sqrt{1 - u^2} \\ u'x &= \sqrt{1 - u^2} \\ \frac{du}{dx} x &= \sqrt{1 - u^2} \\ \frac{du}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} &= \frac{dx}{x} \\ \int \frac{du}{\sqrt{1 - u^2}} &= \int \frac{dx}{x} \\ \arcsin(u) &= \ln|x| + C \\ \arcsin(u) - \ln|x| &= C }$
To finish off consider that the original equation started with $x$'s and $y$'s, but so far we have a solution in terms of $x$'s and $u$'s. So replace all of the $u$'s to get:
$\eqalign{ \arcsin\Big( \frac{y}{x} \Big) - \ln|x| &= C }$
