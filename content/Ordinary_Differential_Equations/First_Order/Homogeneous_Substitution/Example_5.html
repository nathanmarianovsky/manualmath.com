Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{y - x}{y + x} }$
Solution
To see if the homogeneous substitution can be used, first lets check that the function is homogeneous with degree zero:
$\eqalign{ f(x,y) &= \frac{y - x}{y + x} \\ f(tx,ty) &= \frac{(ty) - (tx)}{(ty) + (tx)} \\ &= \frac{y - x}{y + x} \\ &= f(x,y) }$
With the condition checked, using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ y &= ux \\ y' &= u'x + u }$
and plugging in gives:
$\eqalign{ y' &= \frac{y - x}{y + x} \\ u'x + u &= \frac{(ux) - x}{(ux) + x} \\ u'x + u &= \frac{u - 1}{u + 1} \\ \frac{du}{dx}x &= \frac{u - 1}{u + 1} - \frac{u(u + 1)}{u + 1} \\ \frac{du}{dx}x &= -\frac{u^2 + 1}{u + 1} \\ \frac{u + 1}{u^2 + 1} du &= -\frac{dx}{x} \\ \int \frac{u + 1}{u^2 + 1} du &= \int -\frac{dx}{x} \\ \frac{1}{2}\ln|u^2 + 1| + \arctan(u) &= -\ln|x| + C \\ \frac{1}{2}\ln|u^2 + 1| + \arctan(u) + \ln|x| &= C }$
To finish off consider that the original equation started with $x$'s and $y$'s, but so far we have a solution in terms of $x$'s and $u$'s. So replace all of the $u$'s to get:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{2}\ln\Bigg| \Big( \frac{y}{x} \Big)^2 + 1 \Bigg| + \arctan\Big( \frac{y}{x} \Big) + \ln|x| = C }$
