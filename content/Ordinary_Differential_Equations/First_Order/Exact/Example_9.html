Problem
Given the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) dx + \Big( xe^{xy} + 2xy + \frac{1}{x} \Big) dy = 0 }$
determine $M(x,y)$ s.t. the differential equation is exact.
Solution
First identify the component as:
$\eqalign{ N(x,y) = xe^{xy} + 2xy + \frac{1}{x} }$
Now in order for it to be exact, the condition must hold:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ M_y &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \Big( xe^{xy} + 2xy + \frac{1}{x} \Big) \\ M_y &= xye^{xy} + 2y - \frac{1}{x^2} }$
With this we can determine $M(x,y)$ as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= \int M_y dy \\ &= \int \Big( xye^{xy} + 2y - \frac{1}{x^2} \Big) dy \\ &= \frac{e^{xy}(xy - 1)}{x} + y^2 - \frac{y}{x^2} + f(x) }$
Notice that the solution has an extra function of $x$ only. This occurs because the condition uses the partial derivative with respect to $y$ and thus when checking that function will disappear. In general this is the final solution unless more information is given.
