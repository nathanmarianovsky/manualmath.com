Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ (\tan(x) - \sin(x)\sin(y)) dx + \cos(x)\cos(y) dy = 0 }$
Solution
If we are to guess that this equation is exact, first identify the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= \tan(x) - \sin(x)\sin(y) \\ N(x,y) &= \cos(x)\cos(y) }$
Now to check if these satisfy the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (\tan(x) - \sin(x)\sin(y)) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (\cos(x)\cos(y)) \\ -\sin(x)\cos(y) &= -\sin(x)\cos(y) }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(x,y) dx &= \int (\tan(x) - \sin(x)\sin(y)) dx \\ &= \ln|\sec(x)| + \cos(x)\sin(y) \\ \int N(x,y) dy &= \int (\cos(x)\cos(y)) dy \\ &= \cos(x)\sin(y) }$
and with this the final solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ C &= \ln|\sec(x)| + \cos(x)\sin(y) }$
