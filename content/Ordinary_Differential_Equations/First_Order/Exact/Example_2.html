Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \Big( 1 + \ln(x) + \frac{y}{x} \Big) dx &= \Big( 1 - \ln(x) \Big) dy }$
Solution
If we are to guess that this equation is exact, first reorganize:
$\eqalign{ \Big( 1 + \ln(x) + \frac{y}{x} \Big) dx + \Big( \ln(x) - 1 \Big) dy = 0 }$
and identify the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= 1 + \ln(x) + \frac{y}{x} \\ N(x,y) &= \ln(x) - 1 }$
Now to check if these satisfy the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \Big( 1 + \ln(x) + \frac{y}{x} \Big) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (\ln(x) - 1) \\ \frac{1}{x} &= \frac{1}{x} }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(x,y) dx &= \int \Big( 1 + \ln(x) + \frac{y}{x} \Big) dx \\ &= x + x\ln(x) - x + y\ln(x) \\ &= x\ln(x) + y\ln(x) \\ \int N(x,y) dy &= \int (\ln(x) - 1) dy \\ &= y\ln(x) - y }$
and with this the final solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ C &= y\ln(x) + x\ln(x) - y }$
