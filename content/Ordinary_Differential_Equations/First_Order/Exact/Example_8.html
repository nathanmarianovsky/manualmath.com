Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ (x^2 + y^2 - 5) dx = (y + xy) dy }$
where $y(0) = 1$.
Solution
If we are to guess that this equation is exact, first reorganize:
$\eqalign{ (x^2 + y^2 - 5) dx + (-y - xy) dy = 0 }$
and identify the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= x^2 + y^2 - 5 \\ N(x,y) &= -y - xy }$
Now to check if these satisfy the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (x^2 + y^2 - 5) &= (\frac{\partial}{\partial x}) (-y - xy) \\ 2y &\neq -y }$
Unfortunately the condition does not hold, but perhaps an integrating factor can fix this:
$\eqalign{ \mu(x) &= e^{\int \frac{M_y - N_x}{N} dx} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{(2y) - (-y)}{-y - xy} dx} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{3y}{y(-1 - x)} dx} \\ &= e^{\int -\frac{3}{(1 + x)} dx} \\ &= e^{-3\ln|x + 1|} \\ &= e^{\ln(|x + 1|^{-3})} \\ &= |x + 1|^{-3} \\ &= (x + 1)^{-3} }$
where the absolute value can be dropped since multiplying by a constant will not change the outcome. Now:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \frac{x^2 + y^2 - 5}{(x + 1)^3} &= (\frac{\partial}{\partial x}) -y \cdot \frac{1 + x}{(1 + x)^3} \\ \frac{2y}{(x + 1)^3} &= \frac{2y}{(x + 1)^3} }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(x,y) dx &= \int \frac{x^2 + y^2 - 5}{(x + 1)^3} dx \\ &= \frac{4x + 8}{2(x + 1)^2} + \ln|x + 1| - \frac{y^2}{2(x + 1)^2} \\ \int N(x,y) dy &= \int -y \cdot \frac{1 + x}{(1 + x)^3} dy \\ &= -y^2 \cdot \frac{1}{2(1 + x)^2} }$
and with this the final solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ C = \frac{4x + 8}{2(x + 1)^2} + \ln|x + 1| - \frac{y^2}{2(x + 1)^2} }$
Now to determine the value of the constant using the initial condition:
$\eqalign{ C &= \frac{4x + 8}{2(x + 1)^2} + \ln|x + 1| - \frac{y^2}{2(x + 1)^2} \\ &= \frac{4(0) + 8}{2((0) + 1)^2} + \ln|(0) + 1| - \frac{(1)^2}{2((0) + 1)^2} \\ &= 4 + 0 - \frac{1}{2} \\ &= \frac{7}{2} }$
Putting it all together, the final solution is:
$\eqalign{ \frac{7}{2} = \frac{4x + 8}{2(x + 1)^2} + \ln|x + 1| - \frac{y^2}{2(x + 1)^2} }$
