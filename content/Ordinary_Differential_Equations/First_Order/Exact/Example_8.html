Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ (y^2 + xy^3) dx + (5y^2 - xy + y^3\sin(y)) dy = 0 }$
Solution
If we are to guess that this equation is exact, first identify the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= y^2 + xy^3 \\ N(x,y) &= 5y^2 - xy + y^3\sin(y) }$
Now to check if these satisfy the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (y^2 + xy^3) &= (\frac{\partial}{\partial x}) (5y^2 - xy + y^3\sin(y)) \\ 2y + 3xy^2 &\neq -y + 3y^2\sin(y) + y^3\cos(y) }$
Unfortunately the condition does not hold, but perhaps an integrating factor can fix this:
$\eqalign{ \mu(x) &= e^{\int \frac{N_x - M_y}{M} dy} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{(-y + 3y^2\sin(y) + y^3\cos(y)) - (2y + 3xy^2)}{y^2 + xy^3} dy} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{-3y + 3y^2\sin(y) + y^3\cos(y) - 3xy^2}{y^2(1 + xy)} dy} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{-3y + 3\sin(y) + y\cos(y) - 3x}{1 + xy} dy} }$
No matter the simplification, there is no way to cancel out the $y$'s, so trying the other integrating factor gives:
$\eqalign{ \mu(y) &= e^{\int \frac{M_y - N_x}{N} dx} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{(2y + 3xy^2) - (-y + 3y^2\sin(y) + y^3\cos(y))}{5y^2 - xy + y^3\sin(y)} dx} \\ &= e^{\int \frac{3y + 3xy^2 - 3y^2\sin(y) - y^3\cos(y)}{5y^2 - xy + y^3\sin(y)} dx} }$
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (2y^2x + 3x^2) &= (\frac{\partial}{\partial x}) (2x^2y) \\ 4yx &= 4xy }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(x,y) dx &= \int (2y^2x + 3x^2) dx \\ &= y^2x^2 + x^3 \\ \int N(x,y) dy &= \int (2x^2y) dy \\ &= x^2y^2 }$
and with this the final solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ C &= x^2y^2 + x^3 }$
