Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ \frac{3y^2 - t^2}{y^5} \frac{dy}{dt} + \frac{t}{2y^4} = 0 }$
where $y(1) = 1$.
Solution
If we are to guess that this equation is exact, first reorganize:
$\eqalign{ \frac{3y^2 - t^2}{y^5} \frac{dy}{dt} + \frac{t}{2y^4} &= 0 \\ \frac{t}{2y^4} dt + \frac{3y^2 - t^2}{y^5} dy &= 0 }$
and identify the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(t,y) &= \frac{t}{2y^4} \\ N(t,y) &= \frac{3y^2 - t^2}{y^5} }$
Now to check if these satisfy the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_t \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} \frac{t}{2y^4} &= \frac{\partial}{\partial t} \frac{3y^2 - t^2}{y^5} \\ -\frac{2t}{y^5} &= -\frac{2t}{y^5} }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(t,y) dt &= \int \frac{t}{2y^4} dt \\ &= \frac{t^2}{4y^4} \\ \int N(t,y) dy &= \int \frac{3y^2 - t^2}{y^5} dy \\ &= \int (3y^{-3} - t^2y^{-5}) dy \\ &= -\frac{3}{2}y^{-2} + \frac{1}{4}t^2y^{-4} }$
and with this the final solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ C &= \frac{t^2}{4y^4} - \frac{3}{2y^2} }$
Now to satisfy the initial condition the solution must lie along the curve that passes through the point $(1,1)$:
$\eqalign{ C &= \frac{t^2}{4y^4} - \frac{3}{2y^2} \\ &= \frac{(1)^2}{4(1)^4} - \frac{3}{2(1)^2} \\ &= \frac{1}{4} - \frac{3}{2} \\ &= -\frac{5}{4} }$
and so the final solution is written as:
$\eqalign{ -\frac{5}{4} &= \frac{t^2}{4y^4} - \frac{3}{2y^2} }$
