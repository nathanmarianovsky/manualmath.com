Problem
Prove that all first order separable differential equations are exact.
Solution
Any separable differential equation can be written in the format:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = j(x)k(y) }$
Now reorganizing:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= j(x)k(y) \\ \frac{1}{k(y)} dy &= j(x) dx \\ -j(x) dx + \frac{1}{k(y)} dy &= 0 }$
and identifying the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= -j(x) \\ N(x,y) &= \frac{1}{k(y)} }$
checks the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} -j(x) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \frac{1}{k(y)} \\ 0 &= 0 }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(x,y) dx &= \int -j(x) dx \\ &= -J(x) \\ \int N(x,y) dy &= \int \frac{1}{k(y)} dx \\ &= K(y) }$
Putting all of this together gives the final solution:
$\eqalign{ C = K(y) - J(x) }$
where:
$\eqalign{ K(y) = \int \frac{dy}{k(y)} \hspace{.5cm} \text{and} \hspace{.5cm} J(x) = \int j(x) dx }$
