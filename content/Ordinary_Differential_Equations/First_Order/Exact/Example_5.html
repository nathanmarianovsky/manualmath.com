Problem
Solve the differential equation:
$\eqalign{ (y^2\cos(x) - 3x^2y - 2x) dx + (2y\sin(x) - x^3 + \ln(y)) dy = 0 }$
where $y(0) = e$.
Solution
If we are to guess that this equation is exact, first identify the components as:
$\eqalign{ M(x,y) &= y^2\cos(x) - 3x^2y - 2x \\ N(x,y) &= 2y\sin(x) - x^3 + \ln(y) }$
Now to check if these satisfy the condition:
$\eqalign{ M_y &= N_x \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial y} (y^2\cos(x) - 3x^2y - 2x) &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (2y\sin(x) - x^3 + \ln(y)) \\ 2y\cos(x) - 3x^2 &= 2y\cos(x) - 3x^2 }$
With the condition checked, to determine the solution:
$\eqalign{ \int M(x,y) dx &= \int (y^2\cos(x) - 3x^2y - 2x) dx \\ &= y^2\sin(x) - x^3y - x^2 \\ \int N(x,y) dy &= \int (2y\sin(x) - x^3 + \ln(y)) dy \\ &= y^2\sin(x) - x^3y + y\ln(y) - y }$
and with this the final solution is written down as:
$\eqalign{ C &= y^2\sin(x) - x^3y - x^2 + y\ln(y) - y }$
Now to satisfy the initial condition the solution must lie along the curve that passes through the point $(0,e)$:
$\eqalign{ C &= y^2\sin(x) - x^3y - x^2 + y\ln(y) - y \\ &= (e)^2\sin(0) - (0)^3(e) - (0)^2 + (e)\ln(e) - e \\ &= 0 }$
and so the final solution is written as:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= y^2\sin(x) - x^3y - x^2 + y\ln(y) - y }$
