Problem
Find all solutions to:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{\sin(x)}{\ln(y)} }$
Solution
Since the function is separable:
$\eqalign{ \frac{dy}{dx} &= \frac{\sin(x)}{\ln(y)} \\ \ln(y) dy &= \sin(x) dx \\ \int \ln(y) dy &= \int \sin(x) dx \\ y\ln(y) - y &= -\cos(x) + C \\ y\ln(y) - y + \cos(x) &= C }$
The above solution does not provide any simple means of obtaining an explicit solution, therefore just stick with the implicit solution.
