Problem
Find all solutions to:
$\eqalign{ 2y' = e^{x + y + 3} }$
Solution
Since the function is separable:
$\eqalign{ 2y' &= e^{x + y + 3} \\ 2\frac{dy}{dx} &= e^{x + 3}e^{y} \\ 2e^{-y} dy &= e^{x + 3} dx \\ \int 2e^{-y} dy &= \int e^{x + 3} dx \\ -2e^{-y} &= e^{x + 3} + C \\ 2e^{-y} + e^{x + 3} &= -C }$
To put the solution in explicit form:
$\eqalign{ 2e^{-y} + e^{x + 3} &= -C \\ 2e^{-y} &= -e^{x + 3} - C \\ e^{-y} &= \frac{-e^{x + 3} - C}{2} \\ -y &= \ln\Big( \frac{-e^{x + 3} - C}{2} \Big) \\ y &= \ln\Big( -\frac{2}{e^{x + 3} + C} \Big) }$
