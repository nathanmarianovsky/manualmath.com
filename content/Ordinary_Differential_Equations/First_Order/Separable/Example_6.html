Problem
Find all solutions to:
$\eqalign{ T' = k(T - T_0) }$
where $k, T_0 \in \mathbb{R}$.
Solution
Since the function is separable:
$\eqalign{ T' &= k(T - T_0) \\ \frac{dT}{dt} &= k(T - T_0) \\ \frac{dT}{T - T_0} &= k dt \\ \int \frac{dT}{T - T_0} &= \int k dt \\ \ln|T - T_0| &= kt + C \\ \ln|T - T_0| - kt &= C }$
In case an implicit solution does not satisfy you, here is how the explicit solution can be found using simple algebraic manipulation:
$\eqalign{ \ln|T - T_0| - kt &= C \\ \ln|T - T_0| &= kt + C \\ T - T_0 &= e^{kt + C} \\ T &= e^{kt}e^C + T_0 \\ T &= De^{kt} + T_0 }$
where $D = e^C$ is a more comfortable way of writing down the constant of integration.
