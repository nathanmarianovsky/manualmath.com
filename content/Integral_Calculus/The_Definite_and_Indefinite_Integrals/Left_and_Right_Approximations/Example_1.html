Problem
Approximate the area under the curve $f(x) = x^2$ on the domain $[0,3]$ using the left and right approximations with $n = 6$. What is the difference in the approximations? What is the difference if instead 10 partitions are used?
Solution
First to define the width of each rectanlge:
$\eqalign{ \Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} = \frac{3 - 0}{6} = \frac{1}{2} }$
Now for the values needed:
$\textbf{x}$ $0$ $\frac{1}{2}$ $1$ $\frac{3}{2}$ $2$ $\frac{5}{2}$ $3$
$\textbf{f(x)}$ $0$ $\frac{1}{4}$ $1$ $\frac{9}{4}$ $4$ $\frac{25}{4}$ $9$
With this the approximations can be written down as:
$\eqalign{ \textbf{Area}_L(n) &= \sum_{i=0}^{6} f(x_i) \Delta x \\ &= \Big( 0 + \frac{1}{4} + 1 + \frac{9}{4} + 4 + \frac{25}{4} \Big) \cdot \frac{1}{2} \\ &= \frac{55}{8} \\ \textbf{Area}_R(n) &= \sum_{i=1}^{7} f(x_i) \Delta x \\ &= \Big( \frac{1}{4} + 1 + \frac{9}{4} + 4 + \frac{25}{4} + 9 \Big) \cdot \frac{1}{2} \\ &= \frac{91}{8} }$
As for the difference:
$\eqalign{ \Big| \textbf{Area}_R(n) - \textbf{Area}_L(n) \Big| &= \Big| \frac{91}{8} - \frac{55}{8} \Big| \\ &= \frac{9}{2} }$
If instead $n = 10$:
$\eqalign{ \Big| \textbf{Area}_R(n) - \textbf{Area}_L(n) \Big| &= \Big| f(x_{n+1}) - f(x_0) \Big| \cdot \Delta x \\ &= \Big| 9 - 0 \Big| \cdot \frac{3 - 0}{10} \\ &= \frac{27}{10} }$
