Problem
Approximate the area under the curve $f(x) = x^2$ on the domain $[-2,2]$ using the left and right approximations with $n = 4$. What is the difference in the approximations? Does the difference imply that this calculation gives the exact area?
Solution
First to define the width of each rectanlge:
$\eqalign{ \Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} = \frac{2 - (-2)}{4} = 1 }$
Now for the values needed:
$\textbf{x}$ $-2$ $-1$ $0$ $1$ $2$
$\textbf{f(x)}$ $4$ $1$ $0$ $1$ $4$
With this the approximations can be written down as:
$\eqalign{ \textbf{Area}_L(n) &= \sum_{i=0}^{3} f(x_i) \Delta x \\ &= \Big( 4 + 1 + 0 + 1 \Big) \cdot 1 \\ &= 6 \\ \textbf{Area}_R(n) &= \sum_{i=1}^{4} f(x_i) \Delta x \\ &= \Big( 1 + 0 + 1 + 4 \Big) \cdot 1 \\ &= 6 }$
As for the difference:
$\eqalign{ \Big| \textbf{Area}_R(n) - \textbf{Area}_L(n) \Big| &= \Big| 6 - 6 \Big| \\ &= 0 }$
Even though the difference is zero, this does not imply that the above approximations give the exact area. On the given interval $[-2,2]$ the function is not strictly monotonic since it is decreasing on $[-2,0]$ and increasing on $[0,2]$.
