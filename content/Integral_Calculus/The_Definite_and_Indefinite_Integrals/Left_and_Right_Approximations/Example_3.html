Problem
Approximate the area under the curve $f(x) = \sin(x)$ on the domain $[0,\pi]$ using the left and right approximations with $n = 4$. Using the new found information, what would the approximations evaluate out to if the domain was $[\pi, 2\pi]$ instead?
Solution
First to define the width of each rectanlge:
$\eqalign{ \Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} = \frac{\pi - 0}{4} = \frac{\pi}{4} }$
Now for the values needed:
$\textbf{x}$ $0$ $\frac{\pi}{4}$ $\frac{\pi}{2}$ $\frac{3\pi}{4}$ $\pi$
$\textbf{f(x)}$ $0$ $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ $1$ $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ $0$
With this the approximations can be written down as:
$\eqalign{ \textbf{Area}_L(n) &= \sum_{i=0}^{3} f(x_i) \Delta x \\ &= \Big( 0 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} + 1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \Big) \cdot \frac{\pi}{4} \\ &= \frac{(1 + \sqrt{2})\pi}{4} \\ \textbf{Area}_R(n) &= \sum_{i=1}^{4} f(x_i) \Delta x \\ &= \Big( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} + 1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} + 0 \Big) \cdot \frac{\pi}{4} \\ &= \frac{(1 + \sqrt{2})\pi}{4} }$
Using the known fact that $\sin(x)$ on the domain $[\pi, 2\pi]$ is equivalent to $-\sin(x)$ on the domain $[0, \pi]$, the area approximations for the other domain are:
$\eqalign{ \textbf{Area}_L(n) &= -\frac{(1 + \sqrt{2})\pi}{4} \\ \textbf{Area}_R(n) &= -\frac{(1 + \sqrt{2})\pi}{4} }$
