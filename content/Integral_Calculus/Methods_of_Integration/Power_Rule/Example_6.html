Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_2^5 (x^3 - \pi x^2) dx }$
Solution
According to the power rule:
$\eqalign{ \int_a^b x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} \Big|_a^b }$
so:
$\eqalign{ \int_2^5 (x^3 - \pi x^2) dx &= \int_2^5 x^3 dx - \pi \int_2^5 x^2 dx \\ &= \Big( \frac{x^4}{4} - \frac{\pi x^3}{3} \Big) \Big|_2^5 \\ &= \Big( \frac{625}{4} - \frac{125\pi}{3} \Big) - \Big( 4 - \frac{8\pi}{3} \Big) \\ &= \frac{609}{4} - 39\pi }$
