Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^2 x^2 e^{x^3+1} dx }$
Solution
Make the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x^3 + 1 \\ du &= 3x^2 dx \rightarrow \frac{du}{3} = x^2 dx }$
to simplify the integral:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^2 x^2 e^{x^3+1} dx &= \frac{1}{3} \int_1^9 e^u du \\ &= \frac{1}{3} e^u \Big|_1^9 \\ &= \frac{e}{3} \Big( e^8 - 1 \Big) }$
