Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_1^2 \frac{x+1}{\sqrt{\frac{x^2}{2} + x}} dx }$
Solution
Make the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= \frac{x^2}{2} + x \\ du &= (x + 1) dx }$
to simplify the integral:
$\eqalign{ \int_1^2 \frac{x+1}{\sqrt{\frac{x^2}{2} + x}} dx &= \int_\frac{3}{2}^4 \frac{du}{\sqrt{u}} \\ &= \int_\frac{3}{2}^4 u^{-\frac{1}{2}} du \\ &= 2u^\frac{1}{2} \Big|_\frac{3}{2}^4 \\ &= 4 - \sqrt{6} }$
