Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int x\cosh(x^2) dx }$
Solution
Make the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= x^2\\ du &= 2x dx \rightarrow \frac{du}{2} = x dx }$
to simplify the integral:
$\eqalign{ \int x\cosh(x^2) dx &= \frac{1}{2} \int \cosh(u) du \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\sinh(u) + C \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\sinh(x^2) + C }$
