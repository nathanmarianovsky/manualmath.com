Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{x\cos(x^2)}{\sqrt{\sin(x^2)}} dx }$
Solution
Make the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= \sin(x^2) \\ du &= 2x\cos(x^2) dx \rightarrow \frac{du}{2} = x\cos(x^2) dx }$
to simplify the integral:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{x\cos(x^2)}{\sqrt{\sin(x^2)}} dx &= \int \frac{du}{2\sqrt{u}} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \int u^{-\frac{1}{2}} du \\ &= u^\frac{1}{2} + C \\ &= \sqrt{\sin(x^2)} + C }$
