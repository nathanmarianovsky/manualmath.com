Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{e^{2x}}{e^{2x} + 1} dx }$
Solution
Make the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= e^{2x} \\ du &= 2e^{2x} dx \rightarrow \frac{du}{2} = e^{2x} dx }$
to simplify the integral:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{e^{2x}}{e^{2x} + 1} du &= \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{du}{u + 1} }$
To continue another substitution is necessary:
$\eqalign{ w &= u + 1 \\ dw &= du }$
Now the integral becomes:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{du}{u + 1} &= \frac{1}{2} \int \frac{dw}{w} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \ln|w| + C \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \ln|u + 1| + C \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \ln|e^{2x} + 1| + C }$
