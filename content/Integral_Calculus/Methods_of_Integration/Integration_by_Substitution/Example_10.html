Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} e^{-\cos(\theta)} \sin(\theta) d\theta }$
Solution
Make the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ u &= -\cos(\theta) \\ du &= \sin(\theta) d\theta }$
to simplify the integral:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} e^{-\cos(\theta)} \sin(\theta) d\theta &= \int_{-1}^0 e^u du \\ &= e^u \Big|_{-1}^0 \\ &= 1 - e^{-1} }$
