Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{x+1}{6x+x^2} dx }$
Solution
Before integrating using the method of partial fractions:
$\eqalign{ \frac{x+1}{6x+x^2} &= \frac{x+1}{x(6+x)} \\ &= \frac{C_1}{x} + \frac{C_2}{6+x} }$
This breakup is the case of distinct linear factors. Now to determine the constants:
$\eqalign{ \frac{x+1}{6x+x^2} &= \frac{C_1}{x} + \frac{C_2}{6+x} \\ \frac{x+1}{6x+x^2} &= \frac{C_1(6+x)}{x(6+x)} + \frac{C_2(x)}{x(6+x)} \\ x + 1 &= C_1(6 + x) + C_2x }$
Normally at this point you could compare the coefficients of all powers of $x$ to determine the constants. A much faster way is to pick particular values for $x$ that help determine the coefficients:
$\eqalign{ &x = 0: \hspace{.5cm} 1 = 6C_1 \rightarrow C_1 = \frac{1}{6} \\ &x = -6: \hspace{.3cm} -5 = -6C_2 \rightarrow C_2 = \frac{5}{6} }$
With the constants determined, the integration follows as:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{x+1}{6x+x^2} dx &= \frac{1}{6} \int \Big( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{5}{6+x} \Big) dx \\ &= \frac{1}{6} \Bigg[ \int \frac{dx}{x} + 5 \int \frac{dx}{6+x} \Bigg] \\ &= \frac{1}{6} \Big( \ln|x| + 5\ln|6+x| \Big) + C \\ &= \frac{1}{6}\ln|x(6+x)^5| + C }$
