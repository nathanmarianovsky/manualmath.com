Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{2(s+1)}{s(s^2+3s+2)} ds }$
Solution
Before integrating using the method of partial fractions:
$\eqalign{ \frac{2(s+1)}{s(s^2+3s+2)} &= \frac{2(s+1)}{s(s+2)(s+1)} \\ &= \frac{2}{s(s+2)} \\ &= \frac{C_1}{s} + \frac{C_2}{s+2} }$
This breakup is the case of distinct linear factors. Now to determine the constants:
$\eqalign{ \frac{2}{s(s+2)} &= \frac{C_1}{s} + \frac{C_2}{s+2} \\ \frac{2}{s(s+2)} &= \frac{C_1(s+2)}{s(s+2)} + \frac{C_2(s)}{s(s+2)} \\ 2 &= C_1(s+2) + C_2s }$
Normally at this point you could compare the coefficients of all powers of $s$ to determine the constants. A much faster way is to pick particular values for $s$ that help determine the coefficients:
$\eqalign{ &x = 0: \hspace{.5cm} 2 = 2C_1 \rightarrow C_1 = 1 \\ &x = -2: \hspace{.3cm} 2 = -2C_2 \rightarrow C_2 = -1 }$
With the constants determined, the integration follows as:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{2(s+1)}{s(s^2+3s+2)} ds &= \int \Big( \frac{1}{s} - \frac{1}{s+2} \Big) ds \\ &= \int \frac{ds}{s} - \int \frac{ds}{s+2} \\ &= \ln|s| - \ln|s+2| + C \\ &= \ln \Big| \frac{s}{s+2} \Big| + C }$
