Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{1}{w^4-w^3} dw }$
Solution
Before integrating using the method of partial fractions:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{w^4-w^3} &= \frac{1}{w^3(w-1)} \\ &= \frac{C_1}{w} + \frac{C_2}{w^2} + \frac{C_3}{w^3} + \frac{C_4}{w-1} }$
This breakup is the case of distinct linear factors, one of which is repeated. Now to determine the constants:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{w^3(w-1)} &= \frac{C_1}{w} + \frac{C_2}{w^2} + \frac{C_3}{w^3} + \frac{C_4}{w-1} \\ \frac{1}{w^3(w-1)} &= \frac{C_1(w^2)(w-1)}{w^3(w-1)} + \frac{C_2(w)(w-1)}{w^3(w-1)} + \frac{C_3(w-1)}{w^3(w-1)} + \frac{C_4(w^3)}{w^3(w-1)} \\ 1 &= C_1w^2(w-1) + C_2w(w-1) + C_3(w-1) + C_4w^3 \\ 1 &= C_1w^3 - C_1w^2 + C_2w^2 - C_2w + C_3w - C_3 + C_4w^3 \\ 1 &= (C_1 + C_4)w^3 + (C_2 - C_1)w^2 + (C_3 - C_2)w + (-C_3) }$
A system of four equations is left to find the values of the four constants:
$\eqalign{ 1 &= -C_3 \rightarrow C_3 = -1 \\ 0 &= C_3 - C_2 \rightarrow C_2 = C_3 = -1 \\ 0 &= C_2 - C_1 \rightarrow C_1 = C_2 = -1 \\ 0 &= C_1 + C_4 \rightarrow C_4 = -C_1 = 1 }$
With the constants determined, the integration follows as:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{1}{w^4-w^3} dw &= \int \Big( -\frac{1}{w} - \frac{1}{w^2} - \frac{1}{w^3} + \frac{1}{w-1} \Big) dw \\ &= - \int \frac{dw}{w} - \int \frac{dw}{w^2} - \int \frac{dw}{w^3} + \int \frac{dw}{w-1} \\ &= -\ln|w| + \frac{1}{w} + \frac{1}{2w^2} + \ln|w-1| + C \\ &= \ln \Big| \frac{w-1}{w} \Big| + \frac{2w + 1}{2w^2} + C }$
