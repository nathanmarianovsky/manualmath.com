Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{10x+2}{x^3-5x^2+x-5} dx }$
Solution
Before integrating using the method of partial fractions:
$\eqalign{ \frac{10x+2}{x^3-5x^2+x-5} &= \frac{10x+2}{x^2(x-5)+(x-5)} \\ &= \frac{10x+2}{(x^2+1)(x-2)} \\ &= \frac{C_1}{x-2} + \frac{C_2x+C_3}{x^2+1} }$
This breakup is the case of a linear factor and an irreducible quadratic factor. Now to determine the constants:
$\eqalign{ \frac{10x+2}{x^3-5x^2+x-5} &= \frac{C_1}{x-2} + \frac{C_2x+C_3}{x^2+1} \\ \frac{10x+2}{x^3-5x^2+x-5} &= \frac{C_1(x^2+1)}{(x^2+1)(x-2)} + \frac{(C_2x+C_3)(x-2)}{(x^2+1)(x-2)} \\ 10x + 2 &= C_1(x^2 + 1) + (C_2x + C_3)(x - 2) \\ 10x + 2 &= C_1x^2 + C_1 + C_2x^2 - 2C_2x + C_3x - 2C_3 \\ 10x + 2 &= (C_1 + C_2)x^2 + (C_3 - 2C_2)x + (C_1 - 2C_3) }$
A system of three equations is left to find the values of the three constants:
$\eqalign{ 0 &= C_1 + C_2 \rightarrow C_1 = -C_2 \\ 10 &= C_3 - 2C_2 \rightarrow C_3 = 10 + 2C_2 \\ 2 &= C_1 - 2C_3 = -C_2 - 2(10 + 2C_2) = -5C_2 - 20 \rightarrow C_2 = -\frac{22}{5} \\ C_3 &= 10 + 2C_2 \rightarrow C_2 = \frac{1}{2}C_3 - 5 = -\frac{36}{5} \\ C_1 &= -C_2 = \frac{22}{5} }$
With the constants determined, the integration follows as:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{10x+2}{x^3-5x^2+x-5} dx &= \frac{1}{5} \int \Big( \frac{22}{x-2} + \frac{-22x-36}{x^2+1} \Big) dx \\ &= \frac{1}{5} \Bigg[ 22 \int \frac{dx}{x-2} - 22 \int \frac{x}{x^2+1} - 36 \int \frac{dx}{x^2+1} \Bigg] \\ &= \frac{1}{5} \Big( 22\ln|x-2| - 11\ln|x^2+1| - 36\arctan(x) \Big) + C \\ &= \frac{11}{5} \ln \Big| \frac{(x-2)^2}{x^2+1} \Big| - \frac{36}{5}\arctan(x) + C }$
