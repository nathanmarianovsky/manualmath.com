Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{8}{y^3-4y} dy }$
Solution
Before integrating using the method of partial fractions:
$\eqalign{ \frac{8}{y^3-4y} &= \frac{8}{y(y+2)(y-2)} \\ &= \frac{C_1}{y} + \frac{C_2}{y+2} + \frac{C_3}{y-2} }$
This breakup is the case of distinct linear factors. Now to determine the constants:
$\eqalign{ \frac{8}{y^3-4y} &= \frac{C_1}{y} + \frac{C_2}{y+2} + \frac{C_3}{y-2} \\ \frac{8}{y^3-4y} &= \frac{C_1(y+2)(y-2)}{y(y+2)(y-2)} + \frac{C_2(y)(y-2)}{y(y+2)(y-2)} + \frac{C_3(y)(y+2)}{y(y+2)(y-2)} \\ 8 &= C_1(y+2)(y-2) + C_2(y)(y-2) + C_3(y)(y+2) }$
Normally at this point you could compare the coefficients of all powers of $y$ to determine the constants. A much faster way is to pick particular values for $y$ that help determine the coefficients:
$\eqalign{ &y = 0: \hspace{.5cm} 8 = -4C_1 \rightarrow C_1 = -2 \\ &y = 2: \hspace{.3cm} 8 = 8C_3 \rightarrow C_3 = 1 \\ &y = -2: \hspace{.3cm} 8 = 8C_2 \rightarrow C_2 = 1 }$
With the constants determined, the integration follows as:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{8}{y^3-4y} dy &= \int \Big( \frac{-2}{y} + \frac{1}{y+2} + \frac{1}{y-2} \Big) dy \\ &= -2 \int \frac{dy}{y} + \int \frac{dy}{y+2} + \int \frac{dy}{y-2} \\ &= -2\ln|y| + \ln|y+2| + \ln|y-2| + C \\ &= \ln \Big| \frac{(y+2)(y-2)}{y^2} \Big| + C }$
