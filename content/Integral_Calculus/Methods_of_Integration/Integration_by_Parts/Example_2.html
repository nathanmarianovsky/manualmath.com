Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int x(\ln(x))^4 dx }$
Solution
In this scenario the integral is bundled with a polynomial and logarithm. Since logarithms take precendence on the $\textbf{LIPET}$ acrynomy, define the components as:
$\eqalign{ u = (\ln(x))^4 \rightarrow& du = \frac{4(\ln(x))^3}{x} dx \\ dv = x dx \rightarrow& v = \frac{1}{2}x^2 }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int x(\ln(x))^4 dx &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - 2 \int x(\ln(x))^3 dx }$
Once again we need to use integration by parts, just this time define the components as:
$\eqalign{ u = (\ln(x))^3 \rightarrow& du = \frac{3(\ln(x))^2}{x} dx \\ dv = x dx \rightarrow& v = \frac{1}{2}x^2 }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - 2 \int x(\ln(x))^3 dx &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - 2 \Big( \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^3 - \frac{3}{2} \int x(\ln(x))^2 dx \Big) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + 3 \int x(\ln(x))^2 dx }$
Once more integration by parts is needed, identify the components:
$\eqalign{ u = (\ln(x))^2 \rightarrow& du = \frac{2\ln(x)}{x} dx \\ dv = x dx \rightarrow& v = \frac{1}{2}x^2 }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + 3 \int x(\ln(x))^2 dx &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + 3 \Big( \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^2 - \int x\ln(x) dx \Big) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + \frac{3}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^2 - 3 \int x\ln(x) dx }$
For the final stretch another set of parts is needed so the components are:
$\eqalign{ u = \ln(x) \rightarrow& du = \frac{dx}{x} \\ dv = x dx \rightarrow& v = \frac{1}{2}x^2 }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + \frac{3}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^2 - 3 \int x\ln(x) dx &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + \frac{3}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^2 - 3 \Big( \frac{1}{2}x^2\ln(x) - \frac{1}{2} \int x dx \Big) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^4 - x^2(\ln(x))^3 + \frac{3}{2}x^2(\ln(x))^2 - \frac{3}{2}x^2\ln(x) - \frac{1}{4}x^2 + C }$
