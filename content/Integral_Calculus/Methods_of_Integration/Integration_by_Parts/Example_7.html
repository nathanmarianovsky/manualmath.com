Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^{10} ze^{-z} dz }$
Solution
In this scenario the integral is bundled with a polynomial and an exponential. Since polynomials take precendence on the $\textbf{LIPET}$ acrynomy, define the components as:
$\eqalign{ u = z \rightarrow& du = dz \\ dv = e^{-z} dt \rightarrow& v = -e^{-z} }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^{10} ze^{-z} dz &= -ze^{-z} \Big|_0^{10} + \int_0^{10} e^{-z} dz \\ &= \Big[ -ze^{-z} - e^{-z} \Big] \Big|_0^{10} \\ &= \Big( -10e^{-10} - e^{-10} \Big) - \Big( -e^0 \Big) \\ &= 1 - 11e^{-10} }$
