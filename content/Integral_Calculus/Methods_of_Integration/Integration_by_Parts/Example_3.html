Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^1 \arctan(y) dy }$
Solution
In this scenario the integral is bundled with just an inverse trigonometric function. We can also consider the polynomial $f(x) = 1$ to be in there just to make sense of the parts. Since trigonometric inverses take precendence on the $\textbf{LIPET}$ acrynomy, define the components as:
$\eqalign{ u = \arctan(y) \rightarrow& du = \frac{dy}{y^2+1} \\ dv = dy \rightarrow& v = y }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^1 \arctan(y) dy &= y\arctan(y) \Big|_0^1 - \int_0^1 \frac{y}{y^2+1} dy \\ &= \arctan(1) - \arctan(0) - \int_0^1 \frac{y}{y^2+1} dy \\ &= \frac{\pi}{4} - \int_0^1 \frac{y}{y^2+1} dy }$
To integrate the rest consider the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ w &= y^2 + 1 \\ dw &= 2y dy \rightarrow \frac{dw}{2} = y dy }$
Plugging in gives:
$\eqalign{ \frac{\pi}{4} - \int_0^1 \frac{y}{y^2+1} dy &= \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \int_1^2 \frac{dw}{w} \\ &= \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2}\ln|w| \Big|_1^2 \\ &= \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \Big( \ln(2) - \ln(1) \Big) \\ &= \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{\ln(2)}{2} \\ &= \frac{\pi - 2\ln(2)}{4} }$
