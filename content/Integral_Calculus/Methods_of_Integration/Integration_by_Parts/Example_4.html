Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^5 \ln(1+t) dt }$
Solution
In this scenario the integral is bundled with just a logarithm. We can also consider the polynomial $f(x) = 1$ to be in there just to make sense of the parts. Since logarithms take precendence on the $\textbf{LIPET}$ acrynomy, define the components as:
$\eqalign{ u = \ln(1+t) \rightarrow& du = \frac{dt}{1+t} \\ dv = dt \rightarrow& v = t }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int_0^5 \ln(1+t) dt &= t\ln(1+t) \Big|_0^5 - \int_0^5 \frac{t}{1+t} dt \\ &= 5\ln(6) - \int_0^5 \frac{t}{1+t} dt }$
To integrate the rest consider the following substitution:
$\eqalign{ w &= 1 + t \rightarrow w - 1 = t \\ dw &= dt }$
Plugging in gives:
$\eqalign{ 5\ln(6) - \int_0^5 \frac{t}{1+t} dt &= 5\ln(6) - \int_1^6 \frac{w-1}{w} dw \\ &= 5\ln(6) - \int_1^6 dw + \int_1^6 \frac{dw}{w} \\ &= 5\ln(6) + \Big( -w + \ln|w| \Big) \Big|_1^6 \\ &= 5\ln(6) + \Big( -6 + \ln(6) \Big) - \Big( -1 + \ln(1) \Big) \\ &= 6\ln(6) - 5 }$
