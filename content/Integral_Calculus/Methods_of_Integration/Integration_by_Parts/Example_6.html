Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2} dx }$
Solution
In this scenario the integral is bundled with a logarithm and some non-elementary function. This other function is not directly handled in the acronym, but consider the fact that logarithms are all the way on the top for a reason. We do not want to be integrating logarithms, so consider this component setup:
$\eqalign{ u = \ln(x) \rightarrow& du = \frac{dx}{x} \\ dv = x^{-2} dt \rightarrow& v = -x^{-1} }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int \frac{\ln(x)}{x^2} dx &= -\frac{\ln(x)}{x} + \int x^{-2} dx \\ &= -\frac{\ln(x)}{x} - x^{-1} + C }$
