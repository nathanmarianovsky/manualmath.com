Problem
Deduce the following identities:
$\eqalign{ &\int x^n e^x dx = x^n e^x - n \int x^{n-1} e^x dx \\ &\int x^n \cos(ax) dx = \frac{1}{a}x^n \sin(ax) - \frac{n}{a} \int x^{n-1} \sin(ax) dx \\ &\int x^n \sin(ax) dx = -\frac{1}{a}x^n \cos(ax) + \frac{n}{a} \int x^{n-1} \cos(ax) dx \\ &\int \cos^n(x) dx = \frac{1}{n} \cos^{n-1}(x) \sin(x) + \frac{n-1}{n} \int \cos^{n-2}(x) dx }$
where $a \in \mathbb{R}$.
Solution
Each of these identities just requires using integration by parts once to arrive at the results above. For the first integral consider the following setup:
$\eqalign{ u = x^n \rightarrow& du = nx^{n-1} dx \\ dv = e^x dx \rightarrow& v = e^x }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int x^n e^x dx = x^n e^x - n \int x^{n-1} e^x dx }$
For the second identity consider this setup:
$\eqalign{ u = x^n \rightarrow& du = nx^{n-1} dx \\ dv = \cos(ax) dx \rightarrow& v = \frac{\sin(ax)}{a} }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int x^n \cos(ax) dx = \frac{1}{a}x^n \sin(ax) - \frac{n}{a} \int x^{n-1} \sin(ax) dx }$
For the third identity consider this setup:
$\eqalign{ u = x^n \rightarrow& du = nx^{n-1} dx \\ dv = \sin(ax) dx \rightarrow& v = -\frac{\cos(ax)}{a} }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int x^n \sin(ax) dx = -\frac{1}{a}x^n \cos(ax) + \frac{n}{a} \int x^{n-1} \cos(ax) dx }$
For the fourth identity consider this setup:
$\eqalign{ u = \cos^{n-1}(x) \rightarrow& du = (1-n)\cos^{n-2}(x)\sin(x) dx \\ dv = \cos(x) dx \rightarrow& v = \sin(x) }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int \cos^n(x) dx &= \sin(x)\cos^{n-1}(x) - (1-n) \int \cos^{n-2}(x) \sin^2(x) dx \\ \int \cos^n(x) dx &= \sin(x)\cos^{n-1}(x) + (n-1) \int \cos^{n-2}(x) (1 - \cos^2(x)) dx \\ \int \cos^n(x) dx &= \sin(x)\cos^{n-1}(x) + (n-1) \int \cos^{n-2}(x) dx + (1-n) \int \cos^n(x) dx \\ n \int \cos^n(x) dx &= \sin(x)\cos^{n-1}(x) + (n-1) \int \cos^{n-2}(x) dx \\ \int \cos^n(x) dx &= \frac{1}{n} \cos^{n-1}(x) \sin(x) + \frac{n-1}{n} \int \cos^{n-2}(x) dx }$
