Problem
Evaluate the following:
$\eqalign{ \int t\sin(t) dt }$
Solution
In this scenario the integral is bundled with a polynomial and trigonometric function. Since polynomials take precendence on the $\textbf{LIPET}$ acrynomy, define the components as:
$\eqalign{ u = t \rightarrow& du = dt \\ dv = \sin(t) dt \rightarrow& v = -\cos(t) }$
Now applying the formula yields:
$\eqalign{ \int t\sin(t) dt &= -t\cos(t) + \int \cos(t) dt \\ &= -t\cos(t) + \sin(t) + C }$
