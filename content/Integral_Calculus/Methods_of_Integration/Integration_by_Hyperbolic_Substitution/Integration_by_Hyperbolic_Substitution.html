Hyperbolic Substitution
I first remind you about these specific identities:
$\eqalign{ \cosh^2(x) - \sinh^2(x) &= 1 \\ \tanh^2(x) + \sech^2(x) &= 1 \\ \coth^2(x) - \csch^2(x) &= 1 }$
Now given an integral in any of these forms:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx, \hspace{.3cm} \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx, \hspace{.3cm} \text{or} \hspace{.3cm} \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{Z} }$
there exist specific reverse substitutions which will make the integration possible. This is exactly the same scenario as trigonometric substitution, but it may happen sometimes that a hyperbolic substitution might make the integration far simpler.
First Case
Take the case of:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx }$
and make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\tanh(\theta) \\ dx &= a\sech^2(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\tanh(\theta)) \Big( a^2 - a^2\tanh^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} a\sech^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= \int f(a\tanh(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 - \tanh^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \sech^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\tanh(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\sech^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \sech^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\tanh(\theta)) \Big| \sech^n(\theta) \Big| \sech^2(\theta) d\theta }$
At the point the obstacle becomes the absolute value. It so happens that $\sech(\theta)$ is always positive so:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\tanh(\theta)) \sech^{n+2}(\theta) d\theta }$
Incidentially this integral can also be computed using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\sech(\theta) \\ dx &= -a\sech(\theta)\tanh(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\sech(\theta)) \Big( a^2 - a^2\sech^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} (-a\sech(\theta)\tanh(\theta)) d\theta \\ &= - \int f(a\sech(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 - \sech^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \sech(\theta)\tanh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\sech(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\tanh^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \sech(\theta)\tanh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\sech(\theta)) \Big| \tanh^n(\theta) \Big| \sech(\theta)\tanh(\theta) d\theta }$
Once again the absolute value provides an obstacle. Like before make life easy by ignoring the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\tanh(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\sech(\theta)) \tanh^{n+1}(\theta) \sech(\theta) d\theta }$
Second Case
Take the case of:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx }$
and make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\sinh(\theta) \\ dx &= a\cosh(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\sinh(\theta)) \Big( a^2 + a^2\sinh^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} a\cosh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= \int f(a\sinh(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 + \sinh^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \cosh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sinh(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\cosh^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \cosh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sinh(\theta)) \Big| \cosh^n(\theta) \Big| \cosh(\theta) d\theta }$
At the point the obstacle becomes the absolute value. It so happens that $\cosh(\theta)$ is always positive so:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sinh(\theta)) \cosh^{n+1}(\theta) d\theta }$
Incidentially this integral can also be computed using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\csch(\theta) \\ dx &= -a\csch(\theta)\coth(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\csch(\theta)) \Big( a^2 + a^2\csch^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} (-a\csch(\theta)\coth(\theta)) d\theta \\ &= - \int f(a\csch(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 + \csch^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \csch(\theta)\coth(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\csch(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\coth^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \csch(\theta)\coth(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\csch(\theta)) \Big| \coth^n(\theta) \Big| \csch(\theta)\coth(\theta) d\theta }$
Once again the absolute value provides an obstacle. Like before make life easy by ignoring the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\coth(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\csch(\theta)) \coth^{n+1}(\theta) \csch(\theta) sd\theta }$
Third Case
Take the case of:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx }$
and make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\cosh(\theta) \\ dx &= a\sinh(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\cosh(\theta)) \Big( a^2\cosh^2(\theta) - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} a\sinh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= \int f(a\cosh(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( \cosh^2(\theta) - 1 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \sinh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\cosh(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\sinh^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \sinh(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\cosh(\theta)) \Big| \sinh^n(\theta) \Big| \sinh(\theta) d\theta }$
At the point the obstacle becomes the absolute value. To make life easy, just ignore the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\sinh(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\cosh(\theta)) \sinh^{n+1}(\theta) d\theta }$
Incidentially this integral can also be computed using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\coth(\theta) \\ dx &= -a\csch^2(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\coth(\theta)) \Big( a^2\coth^(\theta) - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} (-a\csch^2(\theta)) d\theta \\ &= - \int f(a\coth(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( \coth^2(\theta) - 1 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \csch^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\coth(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\csch^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \csch^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\coth(\theta)) \Big| \csch^n(\theta) \Big| \csch^2(\theta) d\theta }$
Once again the absolute value provides an obstacle. Like before make life easy by ignoring the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\csch(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\coth(\theta)) \csch^{n+2}(\theta) d\theta }$
