Trigonometric Substitution
I first remind you about these specific identities:
$\eqalign{ \cos^2(x) + \sin^2(x) &= 1 \\ 1 + \tan^2(x) &= \sec^2(x) \\ \cot^2(x) + 1 &= \csc^2(x) }$
Now given an integral in any of these forms:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx, \hspace{.3cm} \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx, \hspace{.3cm} \text{or} \hspace{.3cm} \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx \hspace{.3cm} \text{where} \hspace{.3cm} n \in \mathbb{Z} }$
there exist specific reverse substitutions which will make the integration possible. These integrals can also come in two forms, definite and indefinite. For the definie case, redefine the boundaries at each substitution. For an indefinite integral remember to always put the solution in terms of the original variable.
First Case
Take the case of:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx }$
and make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\sin(\theta) \\ dx &= a\cos(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\sin(\theta)) \Big( a^2 - a^2\sin^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} a\cos(\theta) d\theta \\ &= \int f(a\sin(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 - \sin^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \cos(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sin(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\cos^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \cos(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sin(\theta)) \Big| \cos^n(\theta) \Big| \cos(\theta) d\theta }$
At the point the obstacle becomes the absolute value. To make life easy, just ignore the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\cos(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sin(\theta)) \cos^{n+1}(\theta) d\theta }$
Incidentially this integral can also be computed using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\cos(\theta) \\ dx &= -a\sin(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\cos(\theta)) \Big( a^2 - a^2\cos^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} (-a\sin(\theta)) d\theta \\ &= - \int f(a\cos(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 - \cos^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \sin(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\cos(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\sin^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \sin(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\cos(\theta)) \Big| \sin^n(\theta) \Big| \sin(\theta) d\theta }$
Once again the absolute value provides an obstacle. Like before make life easy by ignoring the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\sin(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 - x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\cos(\theta)) \sin^{n+1}(\theta) d\theta }$
Second Case
Take the case of:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx }$
and make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\tan(\theta) \\ dx &= a\sec^2(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\tan(\theta)) \Big( a^2 + a^2\tan^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} a\sec^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= \int f(a\tan(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 + \tan^2(x) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \sec^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\tan(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\sec^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \sec^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\tan(\theta)) \Big| \sec^n(\theta) \Big| \sec^2(\theta) d\theta }$
At the point the obstacle becomes the absolute value. To make life easy, just ignore the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\sec(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\tan(\theta)) \sec^{n+2}(\theta) d\theta }$
Incidentially this integral can also be computed using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\cot(\theta) \\ dx &= -a\csc^2(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\cot(\theta)) \Big( a^2 + a^2\cot^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} (-a\csc^2(\theta)) d\theta \\ &= - \int f(a\cot(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( 1 + \cot^2(\theta) \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \csc^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\cot(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\csc^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \csc^2(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\cot(\theta)) \Big| \csc^n(\theta) \Big| \csc^2(\theta) d\theta }$
Once again the absolute value provides an obstacle. Like before make life easy by ignoring the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\csc(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( a^2 + x^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\cot(\theta)) \csc^{n+2}(\theta) d\theta }$
Third Case
Take the case of:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx }$
and make the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\sec(\theta) \\ dx &= a\sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\sec(\theta)) \Big( a^2\sec^2(\theta) - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} a\sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) d\theta \\ &= \int f(a\sec(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( \sec^2(x) - 1 \Big)^{n}{2} \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sec(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\tan^2(\theta)} \Big)^n \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) d\theta \\ &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sec(\theta)) \Big| \tan^n(\theta) \Big| \sec(\theta)\tan(\theta) d\theta }$
At the point the obstacle becomes the absolute value. To make life easy, just ignore the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\tan(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= a^{n+1} \int f(a\sec(\theta)) \tan^{n+1}(\theta)\sec(\theta) d\theta }$
Incidentially this integral can also be computed using the substitution:
$\eqalign{ x &= a\csc(\theta) \\ dx &= -a\csc(\theta)\cot(\theta) d\theta }$
which when plugged in gives:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= \int f(a\csc(\theta)) \Big( a^2\csc^2(\theta) - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} (-a\csc(\theta)\cot(\theta)) d\theta \\ &= - \int f(a\csc(\theta)) a^{n+1} \Big( \csc^2(\theta) - 1 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} \csc(\theta)\cot(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\csc(\theta)) \Big( \sqrt{\cot(\theta)} \Big)^n \csc(\theta)\cot(\theta) d\theta \\ &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\csc(\theta)) \Big| \cot^n(\theta) \Big| \csc(\theta)\cot(\theta) d\theta }$
Once again the absolute value provides an obstacle. Like before make life easy by ignoring the absolute value so long as the assumption that $\cot(\theta) > 0$ is held true. Moving on:
$\eqalign{ \int f(x)\Big( x^2 - a^2 \Big)^\frac{n}{2} dx &= -a^{n+1} \int f(a\csc(\theta)) \cot^{n+1}(\theta) \csc(\theta) d\theta }$
